4.12 H3PO3 can be represented by structures 1 and 2 shown below. Can these two structures be taken as the canonical forms of the resonance hybrid representing H3PO3? If not, give reasons for the same.
Structure 1: H-O-P(=O)(-O-H)(-H) [phosphorus bonded to: OH, OH, =O, and H directly]
Structure 2: H-O-P(-O-H)(-O-H) [phosphorus bonded to: OH, OH, OH — i.e. all three O-H, no P-H, no P=O]
(The two structures differ in whether phosphorus has a direct P-H bond and a P=O, or three P-OH bonds — they are NOT interconvertible by only moving electrons, since atom connectivity itself differs.)

[Given skeletal structure: H3C-C(=O)-O-H drawn with a double bond error — one H shown bonded directly to the carbonyl O by a vertical line instead of the O-H being on the other oxygen; the correct structure has the carbonyl C=O double bond and a separate -O-H single-bonded oxygen.]
