## Q1
**Date:** 04 Apr 2024 (Easy)
**Concept:** `TAG_ATOM_CRT_EXPERIEMNT_THOMSON_S_MODEL`
**Question:**
Choose the Incorrect Statement about Dalton's Atomic Theory.

**Options:**
(1) Chemical reactions involve reorganization of atoms.
(2) Matter consists of indivisible atoms.
(3) Compounds are formed when atoms of different elements combine in any ratio.
(4) All the atoms of a given element have identical properties including identical mass.

**Answer:** (3)

**Solution:**
Dalton's Atomic Theory states that compounds are formed when atoms of different elements combine in a **fixed, simple whole-number ratio**, not "any ratio".
- Option (1) is correct: Chemical reactions involve reorganization of atoms.
- Option (2) is correct: Dalton proposed atoms are indivisible (though later disproven, it is part of his theory).
- Option (4) is correct: Atoms of a given element are identical in mass and properties.
- Option (3) is **incorrect** because the ratio must be fixed and simple.

## Q9
**Date:** 05 Apr 2024 (Moderate)
**Concept:** `TAG_ATOM_MODELS`
**Question:**
The value of Rydberg constant ($R_{H}$) is $2.18 \times 10^{-18} \mathrm{~J}$. The velocity of electron having mass $9.1 \times 10^{-31} \mathrm{~kg}$ in Bohr's first orbit of hydrogen atom = ____ $\times 10^{5} \mathrm{~ms}^{-1}$ (nearest integer).

**Answer:** 22

**Solution:**
Energy of electron in 1st orbit ($n=1$) of H-atom:
$E_1 = -2.18 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$.
We know that Total Energy $|E| = \text{Kinetic Energy} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
So, $\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = 2.18 \times 10^{-18}$
$v^2 = \frac{2 \times 2.18 \times 10^{-18}}{9.1 \times 10^{-31}}$
$v^2 = \frac{4.36}{9.1} \times 10^{13} = 0.479 \times 10^{13} = 4.79 \times 10^{12}$
$v = \sqrt{4.79} \times 10^6 \approx 2.188 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}$
$v \approx 21.88 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$

Nearest integer is 22.

Alternatively, using formula $v_n = 2.18 \times 10^6 \times \frac{Z}{n} \text{ m/s}$.
For H ($Z=1, n=1$): $v = 2.18 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s} = 21.8 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$.
Rounding to nearest integer gives 22.

Number of spectral lines obtained in $\mathrm{He}^{+}$ spectra, when an electron makes transition from fifth excited state to first excited state will be

**Answer:** 10

**Solution:**
Fifth excited state: $n_2 = 6$.
First excited state: $n_1 = 2$.
Number of spectral lines = $\frac{(n_2 - n_1)(n_2 - n_1 + 1)}{2}$ (or simply $\sum \Delta n$)

Number of lines = $\frac{4 \times 5}{2} = 10$.

## Q15
**Date:** 04 Apr 2024 (Moderate)
**Concept:** `TAG_ATOMIC_WAVE`
**Question:**
The de-Broglie's wavelength of an electron in the $4^{\text{th}}$ orbit is ____ $\pi \mathrm{a}_{0}$. ($\mathrm{a}_{0}=$ Bohr's radius)

**Answer:** 8

**Solution:**
For an electron in the $n$-th orbit, the circumference is an integral multiple of the wavelength ($2\pi r_n = n\lambda$).
$2\pi r_n = n\lambda$
$\lambda = \frac{2\pi r_n}{n}$
For H-atom, $r_n = n^2 a_0$.
For $n=4$: $r_4 = (4)^2 a_0 = 16 a_0$.
$\lambda = \frac{2\pi (16 a_0)}{4} = 8\pi a_0$.
The value is 8.

$\nu = \frac{43.24}{6.6} \times 10^{15} \approx 6.55 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}$
$\nu = 655 \times 10^{13} \text{ Hz}$.

# Dual Behaviour of Matter and Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

## Q133
**Date:** 28 Jul 2022 (Moderate)
**Concept:** `TAG_ATOMIC_WAVE`
**Question:**
If the wavelength for an electron emitted from H-atom is $3.3 \times 10^{-10} \mathrm{~m}$, then energy absorbed by the electron in its ground state compared to minimum energy required for its escape from the atom, is ____ times.
[Given: $h=6.626 \times 10^{-34} \mathrm{Js}$, Mass of electron $=9.1 \times 10^{-31}$]

**Answer:** 2

**Solution:**
$\lambda = 3.3 \times 10^{-10}$ m.
$KE = p^2/2m = (h/\lambda)^2 / 2m = \frac{h^2}{2m\lambda^2}$.
Calculation yields $KE \approx 2.18 \times 10^{-18}$ J.
Escape Energy (Ionization for H, n=1) $\Delta E = 13.6 \text{ eV} \approx 2.18 \times 10^{-18}$ J.
Total Energy Absorbed $= \Delta E + KE = 2.18 + 2.18 = 4.36 \times 10^{-18}$ J.
Ratio Absorbed/Escape $= 4.36 / 2.18 = 2$.
Answer 2.

Which of the following combination of statements is true regarding the interpretation of the atomic orbitals?
(A) An electron in an orbital of high angular momentum stays away from the nucleus than an electron in the orbital of lower angular momentum.
(B) For a given value of the principal quantum number, the size of the orbit is inversely proportional to the azimuthal quantum number.
(C) According to wave mechanics, the ground state angular momentum is equal to $\frac{h}{2 \pi}$.
