# Chapter 2 review — The Pot Maker (Kaveri, Class 9 English)

SUMMARY: This is a strong, source-faithful chapter — the narrated passages quote Temsula Ao's text verbatim (the workflow's hardest rule, honoured), the author/illustration bios check out against known fact, and the pedagogical scaffolding (tension-not-talent diagnosis, expert-vs-teacher contrast, the deliberate-mystery ending) is genuinely well-built. The single biggest systemic weakness is **AI-slop cross-page uniformity in the quiz layer**: nearly every inline_quiz runs the identical 3-question beat (recall-1 → theme-2 → "why did the writer do X / what does this teach us"-3) with the same reveal-framing prose, and a recurring distractor-construction tic where the correct option is the only plainly-sensible one while the three wrong options are stylistically padded throwaways — which lets a test-wise student pass several quizzes without reading. Secondary issues: a handful of glosses/quizzes drift subtly from the source's literal meaning (the "dao", the "two batches" inference being over-asserted as fact in a worked example), one factual overreach about Naga communities, and heavy redundancy between Page 6 (worked examples), Page 13 (poem worked examples) and Page 17 (26-Q practice) that repeats the same handful of insights many times. No CRITICAL source errors found in the marked answers.

TOP 5 MOST IMPORTANT FINDINGS (ranked):
1. [MAJOR] Cross-page formulaic uniformity in the 13 inline_quizzes — identical 3-beat structure + identical reveal cadence makes the chapter machine-stamped and quiz-gameable (dimension D).
2. [MAJOR] Recurring distractor pattern: the correct MCQ option is the only sensible/plain one; the three distractors are stylistically inflated non-misconceptions ("a brand-new pottery factory has opened", "an infant has just been born"), so answers are findable by tone, not comprehension (dimension C).
3. [MAJOR] Worked example Q4 (Page 6) and practice Q19/Q25 (Page 17) assert the supernatural "mother's skill passed in her final hours" reading as the answer, when the text + the chapter's own reasoning_prompts insist the ending is deliberately unexplained — the assessment layer contradicts the teaching layer (dimensions C/E).
4. [MINOR/MAJOR] Factual overreach: "Communities like the **Pochury** and the potters of villages such as **Longsa** kept it alive" (Page 10) — Temsula Ao is Ao Naga and the story is Ao; pinning the craft to Pochury/Longsa is an unsourced specific claim not supported by the PDF (dimension A).
5. [MINOR] Gloss drift: "dao" glossed as "a heavy knife or tool used for digging and cutting" / "DOW" — defensible, but the NCERT side-gloss says specifically "an instrument used to dig"; the pronunciation "DOW" is presented as settled when the Naga term's vowel is not that (dimension A/B).

## A. Source fidelity

Largely clean — this is the chapter's strongest dimension. The three narrated_passages reproduce Ao's sentences word-for-word (spot-checked against PDF lines 28–292): "Ever since Sentila became old enough…", "And the reward? A few rupees.", "A new pot maker was born." all match exactly. The *Quality* extract (Page 16) and the *Gifts of Grace* poem (Page 12) also match the PDF verbatim. Author bios verified: Temsula Ao 1945–2022, *Laburnum for My Head* / Sahitya Akademi 2013, Padma Shri — all correct; Galsworthy 1867–1933, Nobel 1932, *The Forsyte Saga* — all correct; Whitman *I Hear America Singing* 1860 — correct.

[MINOR] A. Page 10 › block#4/callout (threads_of_curiosity) — Unsourced ethnographic specifics. EVIDENCE: "Communities like the **Pochury** and the potters of villages such as **Longsa** kept it alive". The story and author are **Ao Naga** (the chapter itself says so on Page 1 block#2). Naming Pochury and Longsa as the keepers of this specific craft is a precise factual claim with no support in the NCERT source and risks conflating distinct Naga groups. FIX: soften to "various Naga communities" or cite, since the PDF gives no village/community name.

[MINOR] A. Page 5 › block#5/inline_quiz q1 (distractor) — Mangled near-quote used as a distractor. EVIDENCE: option "'it simply came to me, out of nowhere'" — the source line is "it simply came to me" (PDF l.267); "out of nowhere" is invented. It is only a *distractor*, so no student is mis-marked, but presenting an altered string inside single quotes as if it were a textual option is sloppy. FIX: drop the invented tail; quote real lines even in distractors.

[MINOR] A. Page 3 › block#3/narrated_passage gloss "dao" — Pronunciation over-asserted. EVIDENCE: Page 7 block#3 gives "dao … pronunciation: DOW". The NCERT gloss (PDF l.151) only defines it as "an instrument used to dig"; the Ao "dao" is not securely the diphthong "DOW". FIX: drop the confident phonetic respelling for a borrowed Naga word, or mark it approximate.

## C. Assessment integrity

This is where the chapter is weakest. The marked answers are correct, but the *construction* is repetitive and gameable.

[MAJOR] C. (chapter-wide, all 13 inline_quizzes) — Distractors are not real misconceptions; the correct option is identifiable by plausibility/tone alone. EVIDENCE: Page 3 block#8 q2 ("'A new pot maker was born'") distractors include "An infant has just been born in one of the potters' households" and "A brand-new pottery factory has opened up to serve the whole village"; Page 13 block#7 q3 distractor "the maze of bright cables and tangled wires" (not even a candidate thesis line). These are throwaways a student eliminates without comprehension. THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT QUIZ WEAKNESS per the rubric. FIX: replace silly distractors with *defensible-but-wrong* readings (e.g., for the final line, "Sentila has replaced her mother as the village's only potter" — tempting, textually wrong).

[MAJOR] C. Page 6 › block#8/worked_example Q4 — Answer over-asserts the supernatural reading as fact. EVIDENCE: "the mother's skill seems to have passed to the daughter in her final hours" is hedged with "seems," but Page 17 block#2 q19 explanation states it flatly: "the identical batches… hint that Arenla passed everything she had to Sentila," and q25 explanation: "two identical batches… tell Onula that Sentila has truly become her mother's equal." Meanwhile Page 3 block#7 reveal explicitly teaches there is "no single correct reading." The assessment layer marks one interpretation correct that the teaching layer calls deliberately open. FIX: in q19/q25 explanations, frame the transmission reading as "the strongest supported inference" and acknowledge the coincidence reading, matching the reasoning_prompt.

[MAJOR] C. (chapter-wide) — Explanation-restates-the-option / answer-in-stem pattern. EVIDENCE: Page 13 block#7 q1: stem "Is the poem in strict rhyme or free verse?" → correct option "free verse; its music comes from repetition, not rhyme" → explanation "Free verse: no regular end-rhyme. The repeated structure and the refrain carry the music." The explanation merely re-says the option. Recurs in Page 12 block#6 q3, Page 14 block#5 q3. FIX: make explanations teach the *discrimination* (why the runner-up is wrong), not echo the winner.

[MINOR] C. Page 6 › block#2/comprehension_checkpoint q1 — Giveaway via the explanation. EVIDENCE: the explanation prints the *entire* correct event sequence "(4)→(6)→(3)→(1)→(8)→(5)→(9)→(2)→(7)", which is the answer to the NCERT sequencing exercise the chapter is meant to make students do themselves. FIX: reveal only the single ordering relation the question tests, not the whole key.

[MINOR] C. Page 17 › block#2/chapter_practice — 26 questions, but interpretive variety is thin; ~9 are vocab-in-context single-word definitions (pittance, malleable, deftly, indifference, tedious, precision…) repeating the Page 1/Page 7 flashcards almost verbatim. Padding the count, not the depth. FIX: trim duplicate vocab items; add more inference items at difficulty 4–5 (only 4 exist).
