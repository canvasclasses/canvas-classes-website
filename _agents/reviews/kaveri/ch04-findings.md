# Chapter 4 review — Vitamin-M (Kaveri, Class 9 English)

SUMMARY: This is a strong, careful adaptation. Source fidelity is high — quoted lines match the NCERT text, character names/relationships (Ravi, Vidya, Grandpa; Karpov, Fischer, Spassky) are correct, and the three author bios (Asha Nehemiah, Mulk Raj Anand, Tagore) contain no fabricated facts I could disprove against known record. The chapter's real weakness is **assessment integrity**, not fidelity: across the heavy quiz load (18 pages, dozens of MCQs) several items have a defensible second answer or a giveaway that lets a thinking student score without understanding, and one quiz answer is keyed to a reading the text does not establish (the "deliberate cap move" treated as fact). The secondary systemic weakness is **cross-page formulaic uniformity** — every reading page runs the identical beat (banner image → heading → intro → narrated_passage → checkpoint → reasoning_prompt → inline_quiz), and three near-identical "three Indian masters / one tender subject" callouts repeat the same insight three times. AI-slop tells are mostly controlled but present (banned word "vibrant" appears in a vocabulary card the chapter elsewhere teaches as good craft; a few "not X but Y" / reveal-framing closers). Pedagogically the scaffolding is genuinely good for the target reader.

## C. Assessment integrity

This is the weakest dimension. The chapter is quiz-heavy and several items leak the answer.

[CRITICAL] [C] unit-4-reading-part-3-who-needs-vitamin-m › reasoning_prompt (b#5) reveal — presents an inference as established fact, contradicting the story's deliberate ambiguity. EVIDENCE: "the cap given to a stranger was **likely** a deliberate move" softens correctly, but the same reveal then states "the detective-book gift makes it **almost certain** Grandpa *knew* he was followed" and Page 9 dialogue stages Grandpa saying "I knew the moment you ducked behind that elephant bush" **as canon**. The NCERT text ends on the unresolved "whether the twinkle…was an innocent gleam or a mischievous one" — the cap-as-strategy is reader speculation, not text. FIX: keep "knew he was followed" (well-supported by the book gift) but mark the *cap-as-deliberate-shake-off* explicitly as one reading, not fact; the invented dialogue should be framed "imagine" (it is, in scene_description — but the confident "I knew the moment…" line risks being taken as text).
