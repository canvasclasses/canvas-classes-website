--- block #6 [reasoning_prompt] id=26a53eb9-f251-4725-8486-e0672f348bc7 ---
{
 "reasoning_type": "logical",
 "difficulty_level": 3,
 "prompt": "Grandpa says he has 'been looking after myself for the better part of my seventy-five years.' His daughter says he isn't safe alone. **Both are telling the truth.** When an old person's need for safety collides with their need for dignity and freedom, how should a family decide? Is there a 'right' answer here?",
 "reveal": "There is no clean right answer — and the story knows it. Vidya is *right* that Grandpa had a dangerous fall; Grandpa is *right* that he is a capable adult being stripped of his freedom. Safety and dignity are both real needs, and protecting one can wound the other. The wisest families look for the *smallest restriction that keeps the person safe* — and, crucially, they decide *with* the elder, not *for* them. The story's quiet criticism is not that Vidya cares, but that she cares in a way that treats her father like a child instead of a partner in the decision. Holding both truths at once — he needs care AND he deserves respect — is the hard, human work the story asks of us."
}

--- block #7 [text] id=edb8d8db-787e-4f45-9c66-2da40bf184d6 ---
{
 "markdown": "When you quote a person's **exact words** inside quotation marks, that's **direct speech**: *Ravi said, \"I will follow Grandpa.\"* When you **re-tell** those words later, in your own sentence, that's **reported (indirect) speech**: *Ravi said that he would follow Grandpa.*\n\nWhen you make the change, several things usually shift:\n\n- **Tense moves back one step:** present → past, past → past perfect. (*\"I am tired\"* → *he said he **was** tired.*)\n- **Pronouns change** to match the new speaker. (*\"I\"* → *he/she*.)\n- **Time and place words change:** *now → then, today → that day, here → there, tomorrow → the next day.*\n- **Quotation marks disappear**, and we usually add **that** (for statements) or **if/whether** (for yes-no questions)."
}
