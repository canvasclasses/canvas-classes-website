 "prompt": "Shruti says her real problem isn't *what* to tell her father — it's *how* to tell him 'in the right way.' Why is the *way* you say something sometimes harder, and more important, than *what* you say?",
 "reveal": "Because the same message can land as a gift or a wound depending on how it's delivered. Shruti cares deeply about not making her father feel dismissed, so she needs words that honour him *while* claiming her own path. That's genuinely hard: it asks for honesty *and* tenderness at once. --- block #3 [text] id=6a2ae9a5-f8cc-49cb-bba2-9be01e8c323d ---
{
 "markdown": "- **The aside** — a character speaks *to the audience*, and the other characters on stage are understood not to hear it. It reveals a character's private thoughts. (*Shruti, Aside: 'Oh, how much I enjoy performing this piece…'* — we hear her secret heart.)\n- **Stage directions** — instructions in **brackets** telling the actor how to move or feel: *(calmly but firmly), (getting up), (a little loudly).* They replace the reporting verbs ('he said firmly') that a story would use. The words in brackets are **not spoken aloud**.\n- **The ellipsis (…)** — three dots showing a **pause or hesitation**: *'Yes, actually I … er … was finding the right words…'* It makes the dialogue sound real and uncertain.\n- **Non-lexical fillers** — sounds like *ahem, er, um* that fill a pause while a speaker gathers their thoughts. (*'Ahem ahem,' 'I … er …'*)"
}

--- block #2 [text] id=546ed994-91d1-4287-b62c-49cf2aad1c63 ---
{
 "markdown": "**The steps:**\n\n1. Use a verb that names the feeling — *exclaimed (with wonder / with joy / sadly), applauded, marvelled* — instead of *said*.\n2. Remove the **interjection** (Wow! Wah! Bravo!), the **exclamation mark**, and the **quotation marks**.\n3. Add **that** and turn it into a normal statement, with the usual tense/pronoun changes of reported speech.\n\n**Example:** *'Wow Shruti! You're a sensation!' said Avinash.* → *Avinash **exclaimed with wonder that** Shruti was a great sensation.*"
