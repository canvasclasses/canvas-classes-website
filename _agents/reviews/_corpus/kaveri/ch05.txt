--- block #5 [text] id=7e7629a8-8ce7-4569-82e7-bfd8a6b6e095 ---
{
 "markdown": "**Modal auxiliaries** are helping verbs that add meaning to the main verb: *can, could, may, might, shall, should, will, would, must.* The same modal can do **different jobs** depending on the situation:\n\n- **could** → possibility (*it could rain*), past ability (*she could swim as a child*), polite request (*could you help?*), suggestion (*we could try*)\n- **should** → advice (*you should rest*), probability (*they should be here by now*)\n- **would** → past habit (*he would walk daily*), polite request (*would you wait?*)\n- **must** → obligation (*you must register*), strong certainty (*she must be tired*)\n\nThe trick is to read the **situation**, not just the word."
}
