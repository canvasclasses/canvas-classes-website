## GRAV (33)

**GRAV-160** (Q1, ans a) — $mg - N = m\omega^2 R$. For weightlessness $N=0 \Rightarrow \omega^2 = g/R$. Day $T = 2\pi/\omega = 2\pi\sqrt{R/g}$. [book sol-fig: sphere with $\omega$, $mg$, $N$ at equator]

**GRAV-161** (Q2, ans a) — $v_E$ = earth-surface speed about axis, $v$ = train speed rel. to ground. A (east→west) opposes rotation: $v_A = v_E - v$; B (west→east): $v_B = v_E + v$. Normal (track) force $N = mg - m v^2/R$, so $N_A = mg - m v_A^2/R$, $N_B = mg - m v_B^2/R$. Since $v_A < v_B$, $N_A > N_B$ → A exerts greater force.

**GRAV-162** (Q3, ans c) — body mass $2M_e$, comparable to earth, both fall toward common COM. Relative acceleration $a = G(M_{body}+M_e)/R^2 = G(3M_e)/R^2 = 3g$. $H = \tfrac12 (3g) t^2 \Rightarrow t = \sqrt{2H/3g}$.

**GRAV-163** (Q4, ans d) — body mass $M_e/2$: $a_{rel} = G(1.5M_e)/R^2 = 1.5g$. $H = \tfrac12(1.5g)t^2 \Rightarrow t = \sqrt{4H/3g}$.

**GRAV-164** (Q6, ans a) — $g = g_0/(1+h/R)^2$. Exact (not approx, since 1% is large): $0.01 g_0 = g_0/(1+h/R)^2 \Rightarrow (1+h/R)^2 = 100 \Rightarrow h = 9R$.

**GRAV-165** (Q7, ans b) — $g \approx g_0(1 - 2h/R)$ for $h\ll R$. $0.99 g_0 = g_0(1-2h/R) \Rightarrow 2h/R = 0.01 \Rightarrow h = 0.005R = 32$ km.

**GRAV-166** (Q8, ans c) — No book hint. $g_h = g(1-2h/R)$, $g_d = g(1-d/R)$. Equate: $2h = d$.

**GRAV-167** (Q9, ans c) — $GM = R^2 g$, $G(\tfrac43\pi R^3 \rho) = R^2 g \Rightarrow g = (\tfrac43\pi G\rho)R \Rightarrow g \propto R$.

**GRAV-168** (Q10, ans b) — No book hint. Potential inside a shell is constant $= -GM/a$ (value at the surface).

**GRAV-169** (Q11, ans d) — Gravitational intensity (field) inside a spherical shell is zero everywhere → no force → particle stays stationary.

**GRAV-170** (Q12, ans c) — $V = -\frac{GM}{2a^3}(3a^2 - r^2)$ inside ($r<a$), $V = -GM/r$ outside. Most negative at centre ($-3GM/2a$), rising (parabola inside, hyperbola outside) toward 0. **QFIG: graph options.**

**GRAV-171** (Q13, ans b) — $V_P = -GM/\sqrt{r^2+a^2}$ with $r=a$: $V_P = -GM/(a\sqrt2)$. $V_C = -GM/a$. $\tfrac12 mv^2 = m(V_P - V_C) = m\frac{GM}{a}(1 - \tfrac{1}{\sqrt2})$. $v = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{a}(1 - \tfrac{1}{\sqrt2})}$.

**GRAV-172** (Q16, ans c) — No book hint. The separated part has the same orbital velocity at the same radius → continues in the same orbit.

**GRAV-173** (Q17, ans d) — $mv^2/d = GMm/d^2 \Rightarrow GM = v^2 d \Rightarrow v \propto 1/\sqrt d$. Total energy $= \tfrac12 mv^2 - GMm/d = -\tfrac12 GMm/d$. Losing energy → $d$ decreases, hence $v$ increases.

**GRAV-174** (Q18, ans b) — Distances from surface $R, 7R$ → from centre $2R, 8R$. $T^2 \propto r^3 \Rightarrow T_1/T_2 = (2/8)^{3/2} = (1/4)^{3/2} = 1/8$.

**GRAV-175** (Q19, ans c) — No book hint. Water & glass both have acceleration $g$ toward earth's centre (free-fall/orbit) → no relative motion → water "doesn't fall."

**GRAV-176** (Q50, ans a,c,d) — Earth's rotation makes apparent weight smaller at equator than at the pole; only a spring balance records this, a beam balance does not (compares masses). $W_B = W_S$ (at pole); $W_B = W_B'$ (beam reads true weight both places); $W_S' < W_S$ (spring reads less at equator). [book sol on pg 1-140]

**GRAV-177** (Q51, ans b) — Apparent wt at equator $= mg - m\omega^2 R$. Wt at height $h$ above pole $= mg(1 - 2h/R)$. Equate: $\omega^2 R = 2gh/R \Rightarrow h = \omega^2 R^2/2g$.

**GRAV-178** (Q52, ans a) — No book hint (uses Q51 method). Wt at depth $d$ at pole $= mg(1 - d/R)$. Equate to $mg - m\omega^2 R$: $\omega^2 R = gd/R \Rightarrow d = \omega^2 R^2/g$.

**GRAV-179** (Q53, ans a,d) — $F = G\frac{m(2m)}{l^2} = m\omega^2 (2l/3)$ ($m$ orbits at $2l/3$ from COM). $\Rightarrow \omega^2 = 3mG/l^3$. $T = 2\pi\sqrt{l^3/3mG}$ → $T \propto l^{3/2}$ and $T \propto m^{-1/2}$. [book sol-fig: $m$—F—CM—$2m$ line]

**GRAV-180** (Q54, ans a,d) — No book hint. 3 masses on triangle, each at distance $a/\sqrt3$ from centre; net gravitational pull provides centripetal. Net force $= \sqrt3\,Gm^2/a^2$. $\sqrt3 Gm^2/a^2 = m\omega^2 (a/\sqrt3) \Rightarrow \omega^2 = 3Gm/a^3 \Rightarrow T \propto a^{3/2},\ m^{-1/2}$.

**GRAV-181** (Q55, ans a,b) — No book hint. Elliptical orbit under central gravity: total mechanical energy conserved; angular momentum about sun conserved. Force varies (distance changes); linear momentum varies (direction & speed change).

**GRAV-182** (Q56, ans c) — Potential at centre $= -3GM/2R$. $\tfrac12 mv^2 = m[0 - (-3GM/2R)] = 3GMm/2R$. $v^2 = 3GM/R = 3Rg = \tfrac32(2Rg) = \tfrac32 v_e^2 \Rightarrow v = \sqrt{1.5}\,v_e$.

**GRAV-183** (Q57, ans b) — Orbital velocity close to surface $= \sqrt{Rg} = v_e/\sqrt2$ (min to orbit). Escapes if $v \ge v_e$. So for a satellite: $v_e/\sqrt2 < v < v_e$.

**GRAV-184** (Q58, ans a,b,c) — No book hint. Closest orbit ⇒ smallest $r$ ⇒ max orbital speed $\sqrt{GM/r}$, min period $2\pi\sqrt{r^3/GM}$, and most-negative (minimum) total energy $-GMm/2r$.

**GRAV-185** (Q59, ans a,d) — No book hint. Orbit plane passes through earth's centre → crosses the equatorial plane (above equator at some time). Min period (lowest orbit) $= 2\pi\sqrt{R/g}$, so any real period $> 2\pi\sqrt{R/g}$.

**GRAV-186** (Q60, ans b,d) — $\omega_0 = 2\pi/24$ (earth), $\omega = 2\pi/1.5$ (satellite). Same sense (W→E): relative $\omega_1 = \omega - \omega_0 \Rightarrow T = 2\pi/\omega_1 = 1.6$ h. Opposite (E→W): $\omega_2 = \omega + \omega_0 \Rightarrow T = 24/17$ h.

**GRAV-187** (Q61, ans a,b,c,d) — No book hint. Geostationary: above equator; same sense as earth (W→E); period 24 h ⇒ $\approx 36{,}000$ km; circular orbit (constant angular speed).

**GRAV-188** (Q62, ans a) — $T^2 = c r^3$. Differentiate: $2T\,\Delta T = 3c r^2\,\Delta r$. Divide by $T^2 = cr^3$: $2\Delta T/T = 3\Delta r/r \Rightarrow \Delta T = \tfrac32 T (\Delta r/r)$.

**GRAV-189** (Q63, ans b) — Gravitational Gauss law. $E = Gm/r^2$. $\vec E\cdot d\vec S = E\,dS\cos(180^\circ - \theta) = -E\,dS\cos\theta$. $\phi = \oint -\frac{Gm}{r^2} dS\cos\theta = -Gm\oint d\omega = -4\pi Gm$. [book sol-fig: closed surface, $\vec E$, $d\vec S$]

**GRAV-190** (Q64, ans b,c) — Work $= m \times$ (difference in gravitational potential). For $h\ll R$: $W = mgh$. For $h=R$: $V_{surf} = -gR$, $V_{R} = -gR/2$, $\Delta V = gR/2 \Rightarrow W = \tfrac12 mgR$.

**GRAV-191** (Q65, ans a,c,d) — Symmetry: the $yz$-plane divides the object (4-unit sphere with two 1-unit cavities at $\pm2$ on x-axis) symmetrically. Net force at O = 0 (cavities symmetric). Potential equal on circles symmetric about the body: $y^2+z^2=36$ (in $yz$-plane) and $y^2+z^2=4$. [QFIG rec: sphere with cavities — coords given in text]

**GRAV-192** (Q66, ans a,b) — $F \propto r$ if $r<R$ (inside uniform sphere), $F \propto 1/r^2$ if $r>R$. Both inside: $F_1/F_2 = r_1/r_2$. Both outside: $F_1/F_2 = r_2^2/r_1^2$.

## SOLD (7)

**SOLD-083** (Q20, ans c) — No book hint. Stretched beyond elastic limit → permanent (plastic) set. On release it contracts but does not return to original length; final length > initial.

**SOLD-084** (Q21, ans a) — Stress $=F/A$, strain $=x/L$, $Y = \frac{F/A}{x/L} = \frac{FL}{xA}$. Equivalent spring constant $k = F/x = YA/L$.

**SOLD-085** (Q23, ans b) — Mass below a point at distance $y$ from top $= \frac{m}{L}(L-y)$; tension there $T = \frac{m}{L}(L-y)g$. Elongation of element $dy$: $dx = \frac{T\,dy}{AY} = \frac{m}{L}(L-y)g\frac{dy}{AY}$. Total $= \frac{mg}{LAY}\int_0^L (L-y)\,dy = \frac{mgL}{2AY}$. [book sol-fig: hanging rod, element]

**SOLD-086** (Q67, ans b,c) — No book hint. Length changes only when a tensile/axial stress acts: pulling along its length (b), or rotating about one end → centrifugal tension stretches it (c). Free fall (no internal tension) and sliding (transverse friction) don't change length.

**SOLD-087** (Q68, ans a,b,c) — $T_B = mg/3$ (force $F = mg/3$ at the bottom). $T_A = T_B + mg = 4mg/3 \Rightarrow T_A = 4T_B$. Breaking governed by stress $S = T/\pi r^2$. If $r_A = r_B$: $S_A = 4S_B$ → A breaks first. If $r_A = 2r_B$: $S_A = 4T_B/\pi(2r_B)^2 = T_B/\pi r_B^2 = S_B$ → equal stress, either may break. **QFIG: A (upper) / m / B (lower) / F arrangement.**

**SOLD-088** (Q69, ans a,c,d) — No book hint. Loss in gravitational PE $= Mgl$ (mass falls by $l$). Elastic PE stored $= \tfrac12 (\text{max force}) \times l = \tfrac12 Mgl$ (force builds 0→Mg linearly). Difference $\tfrac12 Mgl$ → heat (sudden loading).

**SOLD-089** (Q70, ans a,c,d) — No book hint. Quasi-static stretch (F just exceeds resistance): work $= \int_0^l (\frac{YA}{L}x)\,dx = \frac{YAl^2}{2L}$ = elastic PE stored (no heat). $\frac{YAl^2}{2L}$.

## SHM (6) — two-chapter spillovers (gravity/buoyancy → SHM result)

**SHM-129** (Q5, ans b) — "Infinite pendulum" = bob oscillating along earth's surface (chord). Restoring force toward mean A $= mg\sin\theta = mg\,x/R$; $\ddot x = -(g/R)x$, $\omega^2 = g/R \Rightarrow T = 2\pi\sqrt{R/g}$. [book sol-fig: circle, $\theta$, mean position A]

**SHM-130** (Q14, ans b) — $v_e = \sqrt{2Rg}$. $V_{surf} = -GM/R$, $V_{centre} = -3GM/2R$. $\tfrac12 mv^2 = m(V_{surf}-V_{centre}) = m\frac{GM}{2R}$. $v^2 = GM/R = Rg = v_e^2/2 \Rightarrow v = v_e/\sqrt2$.

**SHM-131** (Q15, ans c) — Motion in the tunnel is SHM with $T = 2\pi\sqrt{R/g}$. Surface→centre is a quarter oscillation $= \tfrac14 \cdot 2\pi\sqrt{R/g} = \tfrac{\pi}{2}\sqrt{R/g}$.

**SHM-132** (Q22, ans b) — Wire (as spring, $k_2 = YA/L$) in series with the real spring $k_1 = k$: $k_{eq} = \frac{k_1 k_2}{k_1+k_2} = \frac{kYA}{kL + YA}$. $T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/k_{eq}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m(YA + kL)}{YAk}}$.

**SHM-133** (Q83, ans a,c,d) — Equilibrium $mg = Al\rho g$ ($l$ = immersed depth). Extra dip $x$: $a = mg - A(l+x)\rho g)/m = -(A\rho g/m)x$. $\omega^2 = A\rho g/m \Rightarrow T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/A\rho g}$. So $T \propto \sqrt m$, $\propto 1/\sqrt A$, $\propto 1/\sqrt\rho$. [book sol-fig: floating block, displacement x]

**SHM-134** (Q84, ans b,d) — Push one surface down by $x$, other rises $x$. Net restoring weight $= 2xA\rho g$; column mass $= lA\rho$. $-2xA\rho g = (lA\rho)a \Rightarrow a = -(2g/l)x$. SHM, $\omega^2 = 2g/l \Rightarrow T = 2\pi\sqrt{l/2g}$.
