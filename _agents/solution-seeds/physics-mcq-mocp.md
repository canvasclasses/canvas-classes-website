# Physics MCQ — Motion of Charged Particles — SOLUTION SEED

Phase-4 reads THIS, not the PDF. Keyed by display_id. All answers confirmed vs key (0 corrections).
Core facts: magnetic force does NO work (speed constant in B); circular radius $r = mv/(QB) = p/(QB)$; period $T = 2\pi m/(QB)$ (independent of $v$); pitch $= T\,v\cos\theta$; in crossed/any fields, only $E$ does work.

## ELST — charge in uniform electric field (elst_2 / elst_1)
- **ELST-242 (Q1)** Same field, same charge magnitude $e$ ⇒ equal force $F=eE$. $a=F/m$; $d=\frac12 a t^2 \Rightarrow t^2 = 2d/a = 2dm/F$. So $t \propto \sqrt{m}$ ⇒ $t_2/t_1 = (m_p/m_e)^{1/2}$ (b).
- **ELST-243 (Q2)** [Q-FIG] Projectile analogy: constant $E$-force = "gravity". Horizontal range $l$, max height $d$. For a projectile just reaching max height at the far edge, $\tan\theta = 4H/R = 4d/l$ (b). (Range $R=2H/\tan\theta$ for the rising half to apex... book: treat $l$ as range, $d$ as max height ⇒ $\tan\theta=4d/l$.)
- **ELST-244 (Q19)** In any $E$ field the force $qE\neq0$ ⇒ velocity must change (a). Speed: the force has a component along $v$ in general (uniform field can't stay ⊥ to $v$) ⇒ speed must change (b). So a,b.
- **ELST-245 (Q20)** $E=\alpha t$, $F=QE$, $a=QE/m=Es=\alpha s t$. $v=\int a\,dt=\frac12\alpha s t^2 \Rightarrow v\propto s$ and $v\propto t^2$ (a,d).
- **ELST-246 (Q21)** From ELST-245: $v=\frac12\alpha s t^2$, $x=\int v\,dt=\frac16\alpha s t^3 \Rightarrow x\propto t^3$ (d).
- **ELST-247 (Q22)** Energy gained $=QV=\frac{p^2}{2m}\Rightarrow p=\sqrt{2mQV}\Rightarrow p\propto\sqrt{m},\sqrt{Q},\sqrt{V}$ (a,b,c). Not $\propto V$ (d wrong).
- **ELST-248 (Q23)** $E=V/d$, $a=QE/m=(Q/m)(V/d)$, $d=\frac12 a t^2 \Rightarrow t^2=2d/a=2d^2(m/Q)/V \Rightarrow t\propto d$ (b). (Also $t\propto\sqrt{m/Q}$, but only (b) listed.)
- **ELST-249 (Q24)** [Q-FIG] Horizontal velocity $u$ constant; transit time $t=l/u$. Vertical $a=QE/m=Es$. Deflection $y=\frac12 a t^2=\frac12 Es(l^2/u^2)\Rightarrow y\propto E,s,l^2,u^{-2}$ (a,b,c,d).
- **ELST-250 (Q25)** Exit vertical velocity $v_y=at=(Es)(l/u)=sEl/u$. $\tan\theta=v_y/u=sEl/u^2\Rightarrow \propto E,s,l,u^{-2}$ (a,b,c,d).
- **ELST-251 (Q28)** Proton heads straight at a positively charged conductor ⇒ repulsive force is head-on (along the line of motion) ⇒ no sideways deflection (c) but it is retarded (d). So c,d.
- **ELST-252 (Q30)** [Q-FIG] X and Z connected ⇒ same potential ⇒ PD(X–Y)=PD(Z–Y)=$V$. From figure X–Y gap $=2d$, Z–Y gap $=d$. $E_A=V/2d$, $a_A=Q E_A/m=QV/(2md)$; $2d=\frac12 a_A t_A^2\Rightarrow t_A^2=8md^2/(QV)$. $E_B=V/d$, $a_B=QV/(md)$; $d=\frac12 a_B t_B^2\Rightarrow t_B^2=2md^2/(QV)$. $t_A^2/t_B^2=4\Rightarrow t_A=2t_B$ (d).
- **ELST-253 (Q31)** Both E (X→Y) and P (Y→Z) cross PD $V$ with equal charge magnitude ⇒ equal energy gained $=eV$ (c). $p=\sqrt{2mE}$ ⇒ proton (greater $m$) gets greater momentum (d). So c,d.

## MVCH — Lorentz / crossed fields (mvch_1)
- **MVCH-252 (Q3)** Magnetic force changes the *direction* of velocity ⇒ it accelerates (centripetal) the particle, but never changes its *speed*. "Accelerated" = yes (a); speed increased by B = never (c,d wrong). So (a).
- **MVCH-253 (Q4)** Initially $v\parallel B$ ⇒ $F=0$. As $B$ tilts, the angle between $v$ and $B$ changes ⇒ $F=qvB\sin\phi$ changes (b). Speed never changes by B (d wrong).
- **MVCH-254 (Q5)** Wire current up ⇒ its $B$ at the proton (proton approaching horizontally) — apply $\vec F=q\vec v\times\vec B$. Net result: the proton is deflected **downwards** (d). (Right-hand rule with the wire's circular field.)
- **MVCH-255 (Q6)** $\vec F=q\vec v\times\vec B=0$ iff $\vec v\parallel\vec B$ ⇒ components proportional: $a/A=d/D\Rightarrow aD=dA$ (a).
- **MVCH-256 (Q7)** "Without deflection" near earth ⇒ gravity ($mg$ down) must be balanced. Only a uniform electric field gives a steady force $qE$ that can cancel $mg$ regardless of motion ⇒ only electric field present (a). (A magnetic force depends on $v$ direction and can't reliably cancel gravity.)
- **MVCH-257 (Q8)** At the midpoint, each wire's field is ⊥ to the plane of the wires; for currents $I$ and $-I$ they ADD to a net $B$ ⊥ to the plane. $\vec v$ is also ⊥ to the plane ⇒ $\vec v\parallel\vec B\Rightarrow F=q\vec v\times\vec B=0$ (d).
- **MVCH-266 (Q17)** Undeflected ⇒ $qE=qvB$ (forces equal). Remove E ⇒ only magnetic force $qvB$ gives $a$. Remove B ⇒ only electric force $qE=qvB$ ⇒ same magnitude ⇒ same acceleration $a$ (c).
- **MVCH-267 (Q18)** B (along x) does no work; only E (along y) does. KE = work by E = $qE\cdot y$ ⇒ speed depends only on $y$ (b).
- **MVCH-268 (Q26)** No force when: stationary in B ($v=0$) (c); moving parallel to B ($v\times B=0$) (d). In E the charge always feels $qE$ (a,b wrong). So c,d.
- **MVCH-269 (Q27)** (a) wrong — in a helix the speed is also constant (B never changes speed). (b) right — B keeps $v$ constant; $a=qvB\sin\phi/m$ may be 0 (if $v\parallel B$) or not. (c) right — in E the speed generally changes. (d) right — uniform E ⇒ $a=qE/m$ constant. So b,c,d.
- **MVCH-270 (Q29)** Conductors are neutral (a) ⇒ no electrostatic force, only magnetic attraction (b). Electron beams: NOT neutral ⇒ electrostatic repulsion. Both produce B (c wrong), but the magnetic attraction is weaker than the electrostatic repulsion (d) ⇒ net repel. So a,b,d.
- **MVCH-274 (Q35)** [Q-FIG] $v_P=v\hat i$, $v_Q=-2v\hat j$ ⇒ speed $v\to2v$. KE gain $=\frac12 m(2v)^2-\frac12 mv^2=\frac32 mv^2=$ work by E (B does none) $=qE\cdot(2a)$ ⇒ $E=\frac34\frac{mv^2}{qa}$ (a). Rate of work by E at P $=qEv=\frac34\frac{mv^3}{a}$ (b). At Q, $v\parallel(-\hat j)$ ⊥ both $E\hat i$ and $B\hat k$ ⇒ both fields do zero work (d). So a,b,d. ($2a$ = horizontal P→Q, $a$ = vertical drop, from figure.)
- **MVCH-275 (Q36)** [SOL-FIG] At distance $x$: $E=\frac{\lambda}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 x}$ (from line charge), $B=\frac{\mu_0 i}{2\pi x}$ (from current). No deflection ⇒ $E=vB$ ⇒ $v=E/B=\frac{\lambda}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 x}\cdot\frac{2\pi x}{\mu_0 i}=\frac{\lambda}{\varepsilon_0\mu_0 i}=\frac{\lambda c^2}{i}$ (a). $x$ cancels ⇒ any distance (d). So a,d.

## MVCH — circular & helical motion (mvch_2)
- **MVCH-258 (Q9)** [SOL-FIG] Electron leaves ⊥ to the wire into the wire's circular B field. Initial force is back toward the wire; as it curves, the force stays in-plane with a component toward the conductor ⇒ it loops back and **returns to the conductor** (a).
- **MVCH-259 (Q10)** [SOL-FIG] To just miss the wall, the circular radius $=d$. $mv=BQr\Rightarrow B=\frac{v}{(Q/m)d}=\frac{v}{sd}$ (a).
- **MVCH-260 (Q11)** [Q+SOL-FIG] $r=p/(BQ)$. Geometry: $\sin\theta=d/r=\frac{BQd}{p}$ (a).
- **MVCH-261 (Q12)** $r=mv/(QB)\propto v$ (same $m,Q,B$) ⇒ faster electrons, larger radius (b).
- **MVCH-262 (Q13)** $T=2\pi m/(QB)$ — independent of $v$ ⇒ same for all electrons (a).
- **MVCH-263 (Q14)** Entering from outside ⊥ to B: the particle traces an arc and exits the same boundary; it can **never complete a full rotation** inside (a).
- **MVCH-264 (Q15)** [Q+SOL-FIG] Particle ($+Q$) enters and leaves the field region symmetrically; the arc subtends $(\pi+2\theta)$. Time $=T\cdot\frac{\pi+2\theta}{2\pi}$ (c).
- **MVCH-265 (Q16)** Same as Q15 but $-Q$ ⇒ rotation reverses (clockwise) ⇒ arc subtends $(\pi-2\theta)$ ⇒ time $=T\cdot\frac{\pi-2\theta}{2\pi}$ (d).
- **MVCH-271 (Q32)** Accelerated through same PD: $\sqrt{2mE}=BQR$ with $E=QV$ (same $V,Q$) ⇒ $\sqrt{m}\propto R$ ⇒ $m\propto R^2$ ⇒ $m_X/m_Y=(R_1/R_2)^2$ (c).
- **MVCH-272 (Q33)** $r=\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{QB}\propto\frac{\sqrt{m}}{Q}$ (same E,B). $p$: $\frac{\sqrt m}{e}$; $d$: $\frac{\sqrt{2m}}{e}$; $\alpha$: $\frac{\sqrt{4m}}{2e}=\frac{\sqrt m}{e}$. So $r_\alpha=r_p<r_d$ (a).
- **MVCH-273 (Q34)** Momentum conserved ⇒ A and P fly apart with equal momenta $p$. $r=p/(QB)$, same $Q$ (both $2e$) and B ⇒ equal radii (a). $T=2\pi m/(QB)$, $m_P>m_A$ ⇒ $T_P>T_A$ so (b) "A greater period" is FALSE. KE$=p^2/2m$, smaller mass A ⇒ greater KE (c). They circle in the $yz$ plane in opposite senses and meet again (d). So a,c,d.
- **MVCH-276 (Q37)** Electron, E along $+x$ ⇒ force $-x$ (opposes the $x$-component of motion) ⇒ pitch decreases initially (b); the $x$-velocity reverses ⇒ it returns to the $yz$ plane (c). Perpendicular circular motion never stops ⇒ never momentarily at rest (d wrong). So b,c.
- **MVCH-277 (Q38)** P strikes T if the target distance $d$ is an integer multiple of the pitch. Pitch $=\left(\frac{2\pi m}{QB}\right)v\cos\theta\propto v/B$. Initially $d=k\,v_0/B_0$. (a) $B=2B_0,v=2v_0\Rightarrow$ pitch $\propto v_0/B_0$ = original ⇒ $d=1\times$pitch ✓. (b) $B=2B_0,v=v_0\Rightarrow$ pitch $=\frac12$ original ⇒ $d=2\times$pitch ✓. (c),(d) give non-integer multiples ✗. So a,b.
- **MVCH-278 (Q39)** Helix: $v_x=v\cos\theta$ (const, $\neq0$) and a perpendicular component $v\sin\theta\neq0$ always present ⇒ velocity is never purely parallel to x (a) and never purely perpendicular to x (d). So a,d.
- **MVCH-279 (Q40)** Pitch $=\frac{2\pi m}{QB}v\cos\theta$; max distance from axis $=2r=2\frac{mv}{QB}\sin\theta$. Setting equal: $2\pi\cos\theta=2\sin\theta\Rightarrow\tan\theta=\pi$ (d).
