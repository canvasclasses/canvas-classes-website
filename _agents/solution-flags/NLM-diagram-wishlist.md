## Revision Exercise 1 (Physics MCQ, Deb Mukherji)

- **NLM-171** (book R10) — 🖼️ Visual Sketch. FBD of a chain over a light pulley with unequal hanging lengths: longer side mass $M$ (falling), shorter side mass $m-M$ (rising), tension $T$ at the pulley on each segment, acceleration $a$ marked. Net downward pull on the pulley $= 2T < mg$. *(Book drew this.)*
- **NLM-173** (book R16) — 🖼️ Visual Sketch. Body B on the outside of a large fixed sphere (centre C). At point P with $\angle ACP = \theta$ from the top: normal force $N$ along CP (outward), weight $mg$ down resolved into $mg\cos\theta$ (into surface) and $mg\sin\theta$ (along surface), friction $f$ up the slope. Equilibrium $\Rightarrow \mu = \tan\theta$. *(Book drew this.)*
- **NLM-174** (book R20) — 🖼️ Visual Sketch. Chain on a table with one-third hanging over the corner edge. Hanging portion weight $\lambda(L/3)g$ pulling down; resting portion normal force from $\lambda(2L/3)g$ with limiting friction $\mu N$ opposing slide. Verge-of-sliding balance $\Rightarrow \mu = 1/2$. *(Book drew this — block + pulley schematic.)*
- **NLM-176** (book R24) — 🖼️ Visual Sketch. Atwood machine FBD: fixed pulley, mass $m$ rising (tension $T$ up, weight $mg$ down, acceleration $a$ up) and mass $M$ falling (tension $T$ up, weight $Mg$ down, acceleration $a$ down), with $M \gg m$ so $T \approx 2mg$. *(Book drew this.)*
- **NLM-168** (book R5) — 🖼️ Visual Sketch. Man held horizontally between two vertical walls. At each contact (hands on one wall, feet on the other): wall's normal force horizontal-inward and friction force vertical-upward; weight $mg$ down at the centre of mass midway between the walls. *(Book left as text; FBD makes the "not only horizontal" point obvious.)*
- **NLM-172** (book R12) — 🖼️ Visual Sketch. Insect of mass $m$ on a stick of mass $M$ on a smooth floor. Friction $f = kmg$ forward on the insect, equal reaction $f$ backward on the stick; insect accelerates $a_1 = kg$ one way, stick $a_2 = kmg/M$ the other; relative acceleration $kg(1+m/M)$ over length $L$. *(Book left as text; the two-body reaction picture is key.)*
