**Tier meaning for the author:**
- **A** — the answer *is* an NCERT line. Cite it and quote/paraphrase the line: *"As per NCERT
  (§2.6.4, p.62), pairing does not begin until each degenerate orbital is singly occupied (Hund)."*
- **B** — the question runs an NCERT formula/concept. Cite the section (and the NCERT worked Problem
  it mirrors, where noted): *"This is the de Broglie relation, NCERT §2.5.1, p.49 (Problem 2.13)."*
- **C** — concept's roots are NCERT but the specific item is JEE-Advanced detail (e.g. the
  √(l(l+1))·ℏ angular-momentum formula, explicit 2s wavefunction algebra) or a different chapter
  (mole concept, periodicity, magnetic-moment formula). **Don't force an "As per NCERT" cite** —
  mention the concept's home section at most.

## Where the questions concentrate (biggest citation wins)

1. **§2.4 Bohr model — 41 questions** (p.46–49). Eₙ, rₙ, hydrogen-like Z²/n² scaling. The single
   largest cluster — every one can carry *"As per NCERT §2.4."*
2. **§2.3.3 Atomic spectra / series — 31** (p.44–45). Lyman/Balmer/Paschen, Rydberg. Table 2.3 is
   the exact source for every "which series / which region" question.
3. **§2.3.2 Planck & photoelectric — 29** (p.39–43). E=hν, work function (Table 2.2), Einstein's
   KE=h(ν−ν₀). Many mirror NCERT's own Problems 2.6–2.9.
4. **§2.6.2 Orbital shapes & nodes — 29** (p.57–59). 19 are Tier A (the radial/angular-node rules
   and probability-density statements are stated verbatim in NCERT).
5. **§2.5.1 de Broglie — 28** (p.49–50). λ=h/mv; NCERT Problems 2.12–2.14 are the template.

## The 29 Tier-C (beyond NCERT Ch.2) — for transparency

These shouldn't carry a forced NCERT cite; their roots:

- **Explicit wavefunction algebra / 2p directional probability:** 105, 250, 255, 262.
- **Magnetic moment μ=√(n(n+2)):** 027, 097, 110, 266, 298 (introduced later, not Ch.2).
- **Cross-chapter:** 267, 302 (mole concept), 214, 249 (periodicity — IE/EA), 119 (f-block bonding).
