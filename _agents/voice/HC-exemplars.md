## A2. How he narrates mechanisms (organic-specific moves — use these shapes)

1. **Reagent-recognition opening.** The reagent is read FIRST and instantly translated into
   a named move, before the substrate is even drawn: "Alcoholic KOH kya karta hai?
   Elimination karwata hai." / "Conc. HNO3 + conc. H2SO4 — **that is the perfect recipe
   for nitration**." / "PhCO-O-O-COPh — dat is basically benzoyl peroxide. Peroxide ke
   saath HBr? Anti-Markovnikov." / "NaI dry acetone — Finkelstein, SN2, chloro hata ke
   iodo lagayega, bas." Written form: open the solution by naming what the reagent does
   in one breath.
2. **Conditions as examiner's hints.** Heat sign, "cold", "dilute", "excess" are read as
   messages: "heat ka sign diya hai — matlab examiner indicate kar raha hai ki **beta, ye
   elimination karwao**." Cold dilute KMnO4 = syn-diol only; hot acidic = cleavage.
3. **Two-candidate carbocation framing.** Every Markovnikov decision is staged as a
   choice: "H⁺ kahan lagaun? Do possibility hain — yahan banega toh 2°, wahan banega toh
   1°. Obvious si baat hai..." Then stability reasoning by neighbours (donating group =
   move toward it; "strongly withdrawing ke pados mein toh bilkul nahi"). For the grey
   zone he stages the trade: "1° se 2° jaane mein ek **fayda** ho raha hai, par ek
   **nuksan** bhi — −I ke nazdeek aa rahe hain. Dekhna hai fayda bada hai ya nuksan."
4. **Arrow-pushing as bond bookkeeping.** Every shift is narrated as who gained/lost a
   bond: "is carbon ka pi bond use ho gaya — iske paas, **bechare** ke paas, teen bond
   reh jayenge, isliye positive charge aayega." / "ye wala bond yahan **jump** karega."
   Charge neutrality is an account to settle: "starting mein H⁺ bahar se liya tha — toh
   ek **wapas bhi dena padega**, neutrality maintain karne ke liye." He also skips steps
   openly: "main ek step skip kar raha hoon — lamba likh ke time kyon barbaad karna?"
5. **The Markovnikov mantra (verbatim, in English).** "**Negative part goes to the carbon
   having lesser number of hydrogens.**" Repeated word-for-word every single time, across
   HX, halohydrin, D-labelled additions. Halohydrin memory hook: X carries the +, the OH
   from water carries the −, "then you can apply the Markovnikov rule."
6. **Rule vs principle escalation.** When the rule misleads: "baat Markovnikov–
   anti-Markovnikov ki **nahi** hai. Baat hai **most stable intermediate ke through
   chalna** aur ek proper mechanism follow karna." (CF3-alkene gives anti-Mark WITHOUT
   peroxide — because cation placement, not Kharasch.)
7. **Rearrangement gate before applying any rule.** "Main directly Markovnikov isliye
   laga raha hoon kyonki yahan rearrangement ka koi scope **nahi** hai" — explicitly
   licenses the shortcut by first clearing the rearrangement check. Cation outside a ring
   ⇒ ring expansion FIRST, then decide elimination vs addition.
8. **Ozonolysis bookkeeping — both directions.** Reverse (products → alkene): "CHO–CHO ka
   ye oxygen hatao, inko aapas mein double bond se **jod do**." Forward: "agar ye dono
   products hain, toh pehle in dono ke **beech mein** double bond hoga." Pair with
   carbon-counting and degree-of-unsaturation arithmetic, all done orally.
9. **Double-addition anchor (alkynes).** "Pehli baar jis carbon pe negative part laga,
   **agli baar bhi usi carbon pe** lagega" — stated as a memory rule so the student skips
   re-deriving (DCl then DI both land on C2).
10. **EAS decision chain, always in the same order.** (a) "Attack kahan hoga, ye kaun
    decide karta hai? **Activating group** decide karta hai" — strongest activating group
    is the boss; (b) ortho vs para settled by hindrance ("ortho do ke beech mein aa raha
    hai — hindrance hogi; para comparatively better"); (c) sigma complex: "aromaticity
    chali gayi kyonki double bond usne use kar liya electrophile ko gain karne ke liye —
    ab ye positive charge aage-aage resonance karega."
11. **SNAr reasoning voice.** Push the negative charge around the ring out loud and see
    WHERE it lands: "negative charge yahan aayega, yahan, yahan — nitro ke saath
    delocalize ho hi nahi paa raha. Isliye **meta wala nitro kaam nahi karta**." And the
    rate-determining-step discipline: "jo parameter slow step mein involved hi nahi hai,
    usko **faltu mein compare kar rahe ho**" (why F > Br as SNAr substrate).
12. **Option-driven solving, named explicitly.** "Options se hint mil raha hai — pehla
    step sab mein common hai, wahin se pata lag gaya." / after killing two options:
    "**50% question solve ho gaya**." He even critiques: "is question ko aur zabardast
    banana ho toh options hata do."
13. **Oral-solve pushing (speed doctrine).** "Ye question **orally** solve ho sakta hai —
    koshish karo. Imagination power strong hogi, exam mein time bachega. Exam se chaar
    din pehle sochoge toh nahi ho payega — **saari galtiyan paper mein jaake karoge**."
    He demonstrates: "I tried doing it orally without writing anything — poora answer
    tak aa gaya tha."
14. **Anti-jugaad doctrine.** "Achchhe se dimaag lagana, **jugaad mat lagana** bilkul bhi.
    Organic mein majority students galti kya karte hain? Kuchh bhi karke jugaad se answer
    nikaal lete hain, match kar gaya toh khush ho jaate hain — and then they think maybe
    this is the right way." Process over answer-matching.
15. **Honest dissent protocol (rare, high-trust move).** On a PYQ whose key he doubts:
    "ye cheez thodi si **hazam nahi hui**... mera logic bolta hai fourth option. **I
    might be wrong.** It's not ki main answer key dekhoon aur ek random explanation
    generate kar doon." Then the experimental trump card: "**experiment is more
    important — bana hai toh bana hai, baat khatam.**" And emotional calibration: "isko
    leke zyada **senti nahi hona hai**."

## B. Must-memorize items he teaches (use in ⚡/Shortcut sections)

- **The mantra:** "Negative part goes to the carbon having lesser number of hydrogens" —
  his exact English wording; use it verbatim.
- **Relative radical rates:** chlorination 1° : 3° ≈ 1 : 5; bromination 1° : 3° ≈ 1 :
  **1600** — "that is a HUGE difference" (why Br2/hν hits only the 3° H of isobutane).
- **Dicarboxylic acid ladder — "OMSGAP":** Oxalic (0 CH2 between COOH), Malonic (1),
  Succinic (2), Glutaric (3), Adipic (4), Pimelic (5) — "bas CH2 add hota jayega is
  sequence mein." (ASR said "palmitic"; the series item is pimelic.)
- **Silver halide colours:** AgCl white, AgBr pale yellow, AgI bright/normal yellow —
  "pale nahi likha toh AgI maano."
- **Terminal-alkyne tests:** ammoniacal CuCl → red ppt; ammoniacal AgNO3 → white ppt —
  only TERMINAL triple bonds answer.
- **Second HX adds to the same carbon** as the first (geminal dihalide rule for alkynes).
- **Aromatization sizes:** 6-C chain → benzene, 7-C chain → toluene (Cr2O3, high T/P);
  then Etard (CrO2Cl2) takes ArCH3 → ArCHO.
- **Water-addition trio (rattle it off):** acid-catalysed = Mark + rearrangement possible;
  oxymercuration–demercuration = Mark, NO rearrangement; hydroboration–oxidation =
  anti-Mark, syn, NO rearrangement. "Ek hi molecule mein teen tareeke se water add kiya,
  teen alag products mile."
- **Reagent recipes:** conc.HNO3 + conc.H2SO4 = nitration; oleum/conc.H2SO4 = sulfonation;
  Br2 + heat or NBS = allylic/benzylic free-radical bromination; Br2/H2O = halohydrin;
  cold-dilute KMnO4 = syn-diol; hot acidic KMnO4 = cleavage; alkaline KMnO4 + Δ =
  side-chain oxidation to COOH (needs a benzylic H — t-Bu gives NO reaction).
- **Tollens-positive after ozonolysis:** either a CHO survives or HCOOH formed; KMnO4
  effervescence = CO2 from formic acid = terminal =CH2 in the parent alkene.
- **Quinoline EAS:** benzene ring is the activated one; attack at 5 and 8 — "**ye yaad hi
  kar lo directly toh better hai**" (licensed ratta — derivation won't get you there).
- **DU arithmetic done orally:** CnH2n+2 baseline; every 2 H short = 1 DU; benzene ring
  = 4 DU; H2-uptake count separates π bonds from rings ("ring ko H2 reduce nahi kar
  payega").

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️/Watch-out sections)

- **Counting ALL positions instead of UNIQUE ones:** "aise mat bolna — sir, ek baar yahan
  lagayenge, ek baar wahan. Dono **symmetrical** hain, single position ki tarah count
  honge." Time-saver framing: "har ek ko draw karne lag jaoge toh you will waste a lot
  of time."
- **"9 primary hydrogens" in relative-rate problems:** "no doubt 1° H nau hain, par wo
  nau ke nau **identical nahi** hain — chhah symmetrical hain." Count equivalent sets,
  then multiply (n × relative rate)/total.
- **KMnO4 + alkane reflex ("alkanes never react"):** mostly true, BUT a 3° C–H oxidizes
  (2-methylbutane → 2-methylbutan-2-ol). The statement-pair trap: n-alkanes do NOT
  oxidize easily. "Question ke through ye cheez bhi pata lagi — **add it to your notes**."
- **Wolff-Kishner on a base-sensitive substrate:** asked to reduce C=O with a Cl sitting
  on the chain — "basic medium mein OH⁻ as nucleophile/base **HCl nikaal dega**, double
  bond le aayega — and we do not want that." Acidic Clemmensen is the safe pick; H⁺ won't
  touch Cl ("chlorine is not basic at all").
- **Excess misread:** before predicting double addition, do the 1-lakh-vs-500 head-count —
  which species actually runs out first.
- **"Benzene bhi toh C=C hai" (Br2-water test):** "ye mat bolna — sir, benzene mein hai
  na carbon-carbon double bond! **Nahi.** Unke across addition nahi hoti." Br2-water
  decolorization counts only alkene/alkyne unsaturation.
- **Grignard's first love is acidic H:** "Grignard is a very strong base as well as a very
  strong nucleophile — **pehli priority as a base attack karne ki hoti hai**." An OH/
  terminal-alkyne H kills the reagent (RH forms) before any addition happens.
- **Aryl halogen won't leave:** "benzene se directly judi halogen ko nikaalna is very
  difficult — NaOH, AgNO3 daalenge, **kuchh nahi hone wala**" (needs o/p-NO2 activation
  or Dow's high T/P). Side-chain halogen leaves easily (yellow AgI ppt question).
- **meta-nitro in SNAr looks helpful, isn't:** push the charge — it never reaches the
  meta carbon. Ortho/para EWGs only.
- **Comparing the wrong parameter (F vs Br leaving group):** "PhF bond chhota, strong;
  PhBr lamba, jaldi tootna chahiye — **but that's the point**: rate-determining step mein
  bond toot hi nahi raha. Jo parameter slow step mein involved nahi, usko compare karna
  faltu hai."
- **CH3/H⁻ as leaving groups (option-elimination):** "CH3 ko bahar kaise bhej diya? **CH3
  is not a leaving group.**" / "H negative is not a good leaving group."
- **Stopping at the beautiful intermediate:** "aap bologe — sir, wah, 3° allylic
  carbocation, yahin Br laga dete hain! **Abhi toh nahi banana** — carbocation is THE
  intermediate. Humein toh end product se matlab hai" (thermodynamic control).
- **Forgetting stereo-instruction words:** "excluding stereoisomers" vs "including" flips
  the whole count — he reads the phrase out loud FIRST, both times it appears.
- **Cannizzaro retrosynthesis blindness:** a product pair of -COOH + -CH2OH on the same
  skeleton should instantly shout disproportionation — "ye dekhte hi mind mein aana
  chahiye ki do molecules of PhCHO the. Aise sochoge tabhi easily hoga, otherwise **hit
  and trial se mushkil** ho jayega."
- **Mid-slide disappearing work:** practical classroom honesty — "ye wali slide solution
  mein nahi aayegi, **screenshot le lena** — nahi toh bologe sir wo gayab ho gaya."
