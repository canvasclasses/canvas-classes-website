# Working notes — Atomic Structure DPP 1 (O7-lnMBnifc) voice distillation

> Scratch file for the voice-profile synthesis. Source: vidiq transcript, analyzed 2026-06-12.
> Register: QUESTION-PRACTICE session ("concept application session").

## Session frame

- Opens with a ritual the students already know: *"Drill aapko pata hi hai ki kya karna hai. Sabse pehle aap question ko pause karke khud se try karenge. Uske baad dekhenge ki usko karne ka sahi tareeka kya tha. To aapne sahi kiya ya galat kiya."* → Predict-first discipline: student attempts BEFORE seeing the method.
- Session close = honesty/effort pep talk, not chemistry: *"Jo bhi aapko question diye gaye hain DPP, wo achhe se imaandari se karne hain. Poore confidence ke saath. Apne end pe jo aapne karna hai wo imaandari se kariye, baaki sab chhod dijiye. Uski chinta aapne nahi leni."*

## Per-question opening moves (observed)

1. **Time budget immediately after reading**: *"Jaldi se — I think 15 second mein solve hone wala question hai ye."* / *"1 minute ke andar I hope ki aapka ye question aaram se solve ho jana chahiye."* / *"1 minute maximum is question ke liye lagna chahiye. Not more than that."*
2. **"Dekho, ab aap jante hain..."** — opens by anchoring to a known fact, then derives the answer from it.
3. **Rhetorical student-voice question**: *"Ab jab bhi bachche ke mind mein aata hai — sir, maximum wavelength kaise nikalein sir hum?"* — he voices the student's confusion, then answers it.
4. **Plan-first for calculations**: *"Kya karenge? Sabse pehle to sochiye ki nikalna kya hai? Power. Power kya hota hai? Energy per unit time."* — names the target, then the definition, then works backward.

## Mid-solution teaching moves

- **Personification**: the higher-energy O atom = *"ye bhai sahab — thodi zyada energy inke paas hai"*; O* = *"ek normal hai, doosra special hai — star wala. Ismein taare lage hue hain."*
- **Concrete miniature numbers**: explains intensity halving with "10 photons → 5 photons" toy numbers before generalizing.
- **Inverse-relationship verbalization**: *"Lambda ko maximize karna hai to energy automatically kya ho jayegi? Minimum."* — asks the question, lets the logic answer.
- **Units discipline as a named key point**: *"Units ki gadbad nahi honi chahiye... bas units ki barabari karni thi — that is one of the key points here."*
- **History-as-story for conceptual Qs**: Rutherford "actually Thomson ke model ko sahi prove karne ke liye experiment kar rahe the. Wo to wo galat prove ho gaya."

## Closing/verdict patterns (after the boxed answer)

- *"Aasan question tha. Koi complication nahi thi ismein bilkul bhi."*
- *"Kuchh bhi solve karne ka nahi tha. Simple basic conceptual."*
- *"Thoda sa different hai. Difficult nahi bolunga — thoda sa different hai."* (honest calibration, never inflates)
- *"Aasan tha but different tha. I think is tarah ka question shayad sab bachchon ne nahi kiya hoga. Thoda sa hat ke aapko laga hoga hopefully."*
- *"To ye fundu question tha definitely, and it required some extra study."*

## Exam-strategy meta (NCERT framing — verbatim gold)

## Verbal signature (tics, address, transitions)

- Address: **"bachchon"**, **"janab"**, **"bhai sahab"** (for entities in the problem), occasional **"boss"**, **"bhaiya"**.
- Confirmation tics: **"Theek hai?"** (most frequent), **"Right?"**, **"OK?"**, **"hai na?"**.
- Transitions: **"Chaliye"**, **"Next one"**, **"Chaliye next question dekhte hain"**.
- Praise word: **"fundu"** (for a genuinely good question), **"bahut pyaara PYQ"**.
- Honesty markers: "difficult nahi bolunga", "wo baat alag thi".
