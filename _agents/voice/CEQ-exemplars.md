## B. Must-memorize relations he dictates (use in ⚡ sections)

- **Kp = Kc·(RT)^Δng** and **Kp = Kx·(P_total)^Δng** — "dono formulas yaad rakhein, ye aage kaam aayenge"
- **Δng = 0 ⇒ volume cancels** — "jab Δng zero hota hai, volume ko lene ka koi logic nahi hota — wo waise hi cancel out ho jaani hai. Moles mein hi solve kar dein"
- **log(K₂/K₁) = ΔH/2.303R · (1/T₁ − 1/T₂)** — trigger: "jab temperature ke saath equilibrium constant ki variation pe question poocha gaya hai" (two T's given, one of K₁/K₂/ΔH missing)
- Reaction manipulations: ×n ⇒ K^n; reverse ⇒ 1/K; **add reactions ⇒ multiply K's** (K₃ = K₁·K₂ pattern)
- K-size reading: K > 10³ ⇒ products dominate; K < 10⁻³ ⇒ "reaction zyada door tak gaya hi nahi"; in between ⇒ both in appreciable amounts
- **D/d = 1 + (n−1)α**, also = M(initial)/M(mix) = M(theoretical)/M(observed) — derived by conserving MASS, not moles; "sirf mass ko aapne conserve karwana hai. Derivation ek baar samajh aa jayegi, on the spot derive kar sakte ho." n here = same n as van't Hoff factor in colligative properties
- **α setup rule: start with 1 mole** — "jab bhi alpha use karna hai, shuruaat ek mol se karo to sahi rehta hai. C concentration liya hai to C-alpha kam honge"
- log K vs 1/T graph: endothermic ⇒ negative slope, exothermic ⇒ positive slope (from log K = −ΔH°/2.303RT + ΔS°/2.303R mapped onto y = mx + c)
- Kx changes with total pressure; Kp and Kc don't — "Kx na pressure pe bhi depend kar raha hai"
- Pure water concentration = 55.55 M (chammach ho ya baalti)
- The master key he repeats in EVERY numerical: "jab tak temperature change nahi kiya, equilibrium constant same rahega" — equate before-K with after-K

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **x is not the percentage:** equimolar CO + H₂O with x = 2/3 — "aap bologe sir 2/3 matlab around 67%, answer A aana chahiye. Beta, at equilibrium saare moles mila dijiye — total 2 moles mein se 2/3, dat means 1/3 hua. Jaldbaazi mein bachcha x ki value nikaal dega aur uske according answer de dega"
- **Large K ≠ fast:** "highest value first wale ki — to bahut jaldi equilibrium aa jana chahiye? Equilibrium constant kabhi bhi speed of reaction ke baare mein nahi batata. It doesn't talk about the kinetics"
- **Kp > 1 tells composition, not position:** "abhi wo kahan hai — ye to tabhi pata lagega jab aap K ko Q ke saath compare karenge." And compare with K itself, never multiples: "twice of Kp se to relate nahi karenge"
- **"Boiling = boiling" reflex:** "ye mat bolna sir paani boil hota hai. Boiling point pe liquid and vapor are in equilibrium" — freezing and boiling points ARE equilibrium phenomena
- **Δng = 0 ratta without reason:** "kai baar bachche isko ratte the ki sir Δng zero hota hai to kuch nahi hota. Kyon nahi hota? Now you should understand" — both sides scale equally (4× vs 4×), expression value unchanged ⇒ no disturbance ⇒ no shift
- **Inert gas at constant volume:** pressure rises, so the reflex is "shift!" — but n, R, T, V of the REACTING gases are all unchanged ⇒ partial pressures untouched ⇒ "equilibrium is not disturbed at all. Jab disturb hi nahi hua to kahin jaane ki zaroorat hi nahi hai." At constant pressure it silently becomes the volume-increase case
- **"Total pressure constant hai sir":** in the constant-P inert gas case — "are bhai, TOTAL pressure constant hai. Main sirf inki pressure ki baat kar raha hoon jo equilibrium system ka hissa hai"
- **Excess-liquid unity overreach:** solvent in excess = unity, but "wo liquid jo reactant hai bechara thoda sa amount mein diya tha — aap uski bhi concentration ko unity lene lag jaoge to galti kar doge"
- **Doubling a solid:** AB(s) amount doubled — "jab AB equilibrium ke expression mein hi nahi hai, to uska amount double kar dene se kya fark padega? No change"
- **Dilution symmetric-drop reflex:** halving all concentrations is NOT neutral when powers differ — Cu²⁺ drops by ½ but [Ag⁺]² drops by ¼ ⇒ Q rises ⇒ backward ⇒ final [Cu²⁺] < 0.5x, not = 0.5x
- **Graph drawn "randomly":** concentration-time curves must obey stoichiometry — drop x in A ⇒ gain 2x in C if coefficient is 2. "Aise nahi ki randomly kuch bhi draw kar diya. Graph se aap stoichiometry bhi bata sakte hain." Rate graphs MUST meet and flatten; concentration graphs only coincidentally match
- **"Low temperature" literalism (Haber):** "aisa mat bolo — sir 500° ka temperature hai, kis baat ka low hai sir? Kya mazaak kar rahe ho? Wo relatively low hai, industrial scale ke hisaab se"
- **Kx "is a constant" reflex:** "ek cheez sabke mind mein immediately aayegi — sir Kx is equilibrium constant na, us pe kyon fark padega? Actually Kp aur Kc change nahi hote, Kx change ho jaata hai" — because it must compensate P to keep Kp fixed

## D. Per-question exemplars (from inside L1/L2) — opening → key move → closing

1. **Not-a-characteristic of physical equilibria (NCERT-style multi-statement)** — Open: "Pause the video for 20 seconds and solve." Key: statement-by-statement scan; dramatizes the wrong one: "are, aisa nahi hota ki processes ruk jaate hain — jitna forward jaa rahe hain utna hi backward bhi aa rahe hain." Close: quick, no ceremony.
2. **Which reaction is reversible (by inspection)** — Open: names the oddity — "shakal se kaise idea lagayenge ki ye reversible hai?" Key: elimination by escape routes — precipitate formed ⇒ can't reverse; gas escaped ⇒ "wo to ud jayegi, backward kaise jayega?" All-aqueous survives. Close: honest hedging — "ya jiske sabse zyada chances hain ki haan, baakiyon se better chance hai."
3. **Just before attaining equilibrium?** — Key: "are bhaiya, barabar ho jaate hain — dheere-dheere nazdeek aa rahe hain aur judne wale the. They are very close to each other." Close: honest verdict — "aasan sa question hai, par phir bhi thoda sa mere dimaag ko confuse karta hai" (he admits the wording is awkward).
4. **Sealed liquid–vapor eq, volume suddenly increased** — Open: restate equilibrium as particle traffic ("jitne neeche se upar, utne upar se neeche"). Key: liquid is unaffected; vapors get room, crowd thins, condensation drops; then the toy-count recovery — "paanch upar jaa rahe hain, sirf teen neeche — har baar do naye particle upar generate ho rahe hain" until pressure rebuilds. Close: bridges forward — "yahi cheez aage Le Chatelier mein bhi padhenge" + the exam-mindset sermon (strengthen what you know, don't panic over gaps).
5. **K₃ from K₁, K₂ (CH₄ reforming)** — Open: "Maximum 30 second mein solve ho jana chahiye." Key: spot that eq-3 = eq-1 + eq-2 (CO cancels) ⇒ K₃ = K₁·K₂. Close: "is tarah ke bhi questions humein dekhne ko milte hain."
6. **Why pure solid/liquid excluded from K** — Key: the baalti-chammach + marble-density logic; "quantity se uski concentration change nahi hoti." Close: option A, done.
7. **K increases 4× when T goes 25→40 °C, find ΔH** — Open: "ab ye aa gaya question jo peechhe formula bataya." Key: K₂/K₁ = 4, both T's given ⇒ the log formula; "bas put the values and get the answer." Close: disarming honesty — "iska answer kya hai wo mujhe bhi yaad nahi. Main itna bharosa aap pe kar raha hoon ki aap ye kaam kar lenge."
8. **Equilibrium shifts to product side when…** — Key: kills each distractor against the Q-vs-K rule; "Q less than twice of Kp? Twice ki zaroorat nahi hai. K se relate karenge — uske twice-thrice se relate nahi karte." Close: "clear hai ye?"
9. **CaCO₃ heated in 0.654 L at 1000 K, mass of CaO** — Open: "Pehle isko visualize karo — ek closed container hai, usme CaCO₃ ko heat kiya." Key: solids out ⇒ Kp = p(CO₂) directly; PV = nRT gives n(CO₂); "jitne mole CO₂ ke bane the wahi to CaO ke bhi bane the" ⇒ ×molar mass. Close: difficulty calibration — "chemical equilibrium ke question hote hain bahut aasan. Par har ek variety ke upar kuch-kuch question karne padenge, tab jaake confidence aayega."
10. **NH₂COONH₄(s) ⇌ 2NH₃ + CO₂; NH₃ added till p(NH₃) = original total P; new/old total pressure?** — Open: long setup, walked slowly. Key: mole-fraction split of total P (2P/3, P/3) ⇒ Kp = 4P³/27; then THE invariant — "ab ye poora question iske upar based hai ki jab tak aap temperature nahi badloge, equilibrium constant nahi badlega" ⇒ equate old and new Kp, unknown p(CO₂) = 4P/27 ⇒ ratio 31/27. Close: re-summarizes the invariant as the chapter's master key.
11. **CO + H₂O ⇌ CO₂ + H₂, Kc = 4, equimolar start; % CO₂ at eq** — Open: "1 minute maximum lagna chahiye." Key: Δng = 0 ⇒ drop volume, work in moles; x²/(1−x)² = 4 ⇒ x = 2/3; then the trap-break (x vs % of TOTAL 2 moles ⇒ 1/3). Close: "ye cheez badi important thi" + names the rushed-student error.
12. **A(g) ⇌ B(g) + C(g): relation between Kp, α, P** — Open: "Maximum 1 minute." Key: "jab bhi alpha use karte hain, shuruaat ek mol se karo"; total moles 1+α, Kx route, Kp = α²P/(1−α²), rearrange. Close: deflates it — "bas simplify kiya, humne kuch bhi nahi kiya."
13. **Which reaction takes maximum time to reach eq (four K values)** — Open: "Bada aasan sa question hai. 10 second ke andar answer aa jana chahiye." Key: voices the bait first ("highest K — to jaldi equilibrium aa jana chahiye, right?") then breaks it: K says nothing about speed. Close: kinetics/thermodynamics boundary stated plainly.
14. **XY₂ ⇌ XY + Y; 600 → total 800 mm; Kp?** — Key: pressure-ICE table; 600 + p = 800 ⇒ p = 200; Kp = 200·200/400 = 100. Close: "ho gaya solve. Simple, very simple question."
15. **N₂O₄ observed molar mass 77.7; % dissociation** — Open: "saath-saath decode karta chalta hoon — ek particle se do generate ho rahe hain, n = 2; observed matlab mixture ka." Key: direct D/d formula, α = 0.184 ⇒ 18.4%. Close: formula-knowledge verdict — "agar formula aata hai to aasan hai. Nahi aata to aap sir maarte reh jaoge ki sir kya karna hai. Dekho derive karna bhi kitna aasan tha — sirf mass ko conserve karwana hai."
16. **N₂O₄ at P₁ (α = 33.33%) and P₂ (α = 50%); P₁/P₂** — Open: "Pause the video, 1 minute. Vichaar kar lo aaraam se." Key: temperature unchanged ⇒ same Kp at both pressures; Kp = 4α²P/(1−α²); "33.33 matlab yaar 1/3"; equate the two. Close: names the key point explicitly — "Kp ki value same hogi; total pressure badlega to alpha badlega, par the entire expression will give you the same value."
17. **N₂O₄ ⇌ 2NO₂, container volume halved; which statement?** — Key: P doubles ⇒ goes where moles are fewer ⇒ backward ⇒ α changes; then the distractor kill — "are bhaiya, Kp kyon change karega? Temperature thodi na badla hai aapne." Close: "Kp doesn't change but alpha changes."
18. **AB(s) ⇌ A(g) + B(g), AB doubled** — Key: solid absent from expression ⇒ "equilibrium is not disturbed at all" ⇒ no change in anything. One-liner question, one-liner close.
19. **H₂O(l) ⇌ H₂O(g), pressure increased** — Open: connects back — "ye kaun sa physical equilibrium hai? Boiling point is equilibrium phenomena." Key: pressure ↑ ⇒ goes to lower-volume side ⇒ condensation ↑ ⇒ to restore matching, must heat more ⇒ boiling point rises; generalizes "boiling point is directly proportional to external pressure — isliye low pressure pe cheezein jaldi boil ho jaati hain." Close: rule restated both ways.
20. **Exothermic graph: ln Kp vs 1/T** — Key: "abhi-abhi padha — exothermic ke liye slope positive aati hai." Instant recall check, no algebra repeated.
21. **10-fold pressure increase; effect on Kx** — Open: voices the reflex — "sir Kx is equilibrium constant na, us pe kyon fark padega? Sir saare options mein kuch na kuch fark diya hai." Key: Kp invariant ⇒ Kx·P^(−2) = Kx′·(10P)^(−2) ⇒ Kx′ = 100·Kx. Close: "to ye ek important point ho gaya — Kx pressure pe bhi depend kar raha hai."
22. **Cu²⁺/Ag⁺ aqueous eq, 500 mL water added; new [Cu²⁺]?** — Open: "Pehle try kar lo aaraam se dimaag laga ke. 1 minute." Key: doubling V halves every concentration; numerator drops ½, denominator (squared) drops ¼ ⇒ Q > K ⇒ backward ⇒ Cu²⁺ falls below x/2. Close: "answer will be less than 0.5x. Ye tha simple. That's it."
