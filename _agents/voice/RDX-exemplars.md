## A. Analogy inventory (concept → his actual analogy)

| Concept | His analogy (use this, don't invent) |
|---|---|
| Displacement reaction | Zinc kicks copper out of its compound — "isne usko laat maari. Bola bhai nikal ja yahan se, metal ban ke bahar chala ja" — because zinc is "zyada utaawla electron loose karne ke liye" (more impatient to lose electrons) |
| Oxidation/reduction potential | "Potential is just tendency" — strip the jargon: it's only who is more eager to lose or gain |
| Species at highest oxidation state | Personified saturation — sulfate/dichromate speaks: "bhai sahab, main to already +6 mein baitha hoon. Main to further oxidize ho nahi sakta — to main aapko karunga." CO₂ as reducing agent: "iski kahani khatam hai" |
| Law of equivalence (skips balancing) | "Jab bhi do cheezein aapas mein react karti hain, unke equivalents hamesha barabar hote hain" — stoichiometry whatever ho, 4 equivalents need exactly 4 equivalents. Balancing ki zaroorat hi nahi |
| Atoms combine in moles, not grams | The H₂O speak-aloud — "H₂O mein aap kya bolte ho? Do hydrogen ke ATOM ek oxygen ke ATOM ke saath combine kar rahe hain" — so always convert a mass % into a mole ratio first |
| Water in redox balancing | "Paani to thok mein hamare paas available hai — what is a solvent? Kitna bhi daal sakte hain" — H₂O is the free commodity you add at the END |
| KMnO₄ self-indicator endpoint | The lonely last drop — every drop loses its purple on arrival; when the analyte is finished the next drop "neeche dhoondhega — bhai C₂O₄²⁻ tum kahan ho? Wo to hai hi nahin!" — no one left to react, so suddenly the solution stays pink |
| Diphenylamine indicator (Mohr salt titration) | Stays out of the fight — "wo bolega: main tumhare beech mein nahi padta, tum aapas mein reaction karte raho" — only when Mohr salt is exhausted does it step forward, get oxidized, and flash violet-blue |
| Primary standard | What you weighed is what you still have — leave it 2 hours, come back, koi side-reaction nahi, koi moisture nahi; that trustworthiness IS the definition |
| Disproportionation eligibility | Three rungs needed — the existing state, one higher, one lower. "Kam se kam teen oxidation states to us element ko show karni hi padengi." Fluorine has only two (0, −1) — disqualified |
| Aluminium "free state" paradox | Your laptop/soda-can aluminium wears an invisible oxide jacket — the moment it meets air the Al₂O₃ layer forms and seals the metal off |
| Gold in the activity series | Gold jewellery never rusts, never gets a layer — that's LOW reactivity made visible |
| Interfering acid in permanganate titration | The acid's only job is to set the stage (acidify), not act in the play — HCl "bechaara reducing agent ka kaam kar jaayega, react kar jaayega" and the reading goes wrong |

## B. Must-memorize values he teaches (use in ⚡/Shortcut lines)

- **KMnO₄ three media** — "ye teen scenarios yaad hone chahiye": acidic 7→+2 (n = 5);
  neutral / faintly alkaline 7→+4, MnO₂ (n = 3); strongly alkaline 7→+6 (n = 1).
- **Dichromate** acidic: per molecule 2 Cr, +12→+6 ⇒ n = 6 (never 3 — count per molecule).
- **H₂O₂ n = 2** (both as OA and RA, and in its disproportionation) — "exam mein baith ke
  calculate karoge to that will waste a lot of time. Yaad hona chahiye: n-factor two hai, bas."
- Oxalate C₂O₄²⁻ → CO₂: n = 2. Thiosulfate → tetrathionate: n = 1 (2S: +4→+5).
- Equivalent weight = (at./mol. weight)/n; equivalents = N×V; **N = M × n-factor**.
- Eq-weight definition anchors: combines with/displaces **1 g H, 8 g O, 35.5 g Cl**.
- **Primary standards list:** oxalic acid, K₂Cr₂O₇, AgNO₃, CuSO₄, ferrous ammonium
  sulphate (Mohr salt), hypo. **Secondary:** NaOH, KOH, HCl, H₂SO₄, I₂, KMnO₄.
- **Disproportionation set to memorize** (asked directly as products/balancing): H₂O₂ →
  H₂O + O₂; P₄/OH⁻ → PH₃ + H₂PO₂⁻; S₈/OH⁻ → S²⁻ + S₂O₃²⁻; X₂ in dilute alkali → X⁻ + XO⁻,
  in concentrated alkali → X⁻ + XO₃⁻. Never F₂.
- Oxyacid structures decide n: H₃PO₃ n = 2, H₃PO₄ n = 3, H₃PO₂ n = 1 (only O–H hydrogens
  are acidic); H₃BO₃ gives NO H⁺ — it gains OH⁻ → B(OH)₄⁻.
- FeS₂ mein sulfur is **−1** ("pata hai to pata hai, nahi pata to nahi pata" — knowledge item).
- Unequal-molecule disproportionation: overall n = n₁n₂/(n₁+n₂).
- Balancing order (his minimum-steps method): mark changing ONs → equalize atoms of the
  changing element FIRST → balance electrons (cross-multiply) → balance charge with
  H⁺/OH⁻ → water LAST.

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️/Watch-out)

- **n-factor of an acid ≠ count of H's in the formula:** H₂SO₄ → NaHSO₄ has n = 1.
  Na₂CO₃ first step: "normally kaafi bachche bolenge sir two hota hai... but n-factor
  reaction pe depend karta hai" — to NaHCO₃ it is 1, so molar mass = equivalent weight.
- **Per-molecule comparison:** student objects "do kyon compare kar rahe ho? Sir wahan
  to chaar hain!" — because n-factor is PER MOLECULE of the species you're computing for;
  2 S compares with 2 S, 2 Cr with 2 Cr.
- **Adding loss AND gain:** "aisa mat karna ki sir chheh loose ho rahe hain, chheh gain ho
  rahe hain — 12. 12 is NOT the n-factor there. Loss aur gain to hamesha barabar hota hai" —
  count only one side (intramolecular cases like (NH₄)₂Cr₂O₇, FeC₂O₄, Cu₂S, FeS₂).
- **n-factor belongs to a species, not the reaction:** NO₃⁻ → N₂H₄ exchanges 14 e⁻ for
  2 NO₃⁻ ⇒ n(NO₃⁻) = 7 but n(N₂H₄) = 14. State WHOSE n-factor you mean.
- **Chained equivalents trap:** "one must never equate equivalents in a sequence which
  involves the same element in more than two oxidation states" — IO₃⁻ + I⁻ → I₂ then
  I₂ + hypo: apply equivalence within each reaction separately, never across the chain.
- **HCl in KMnO₄ titration:** the reflex "sir wo bhi to acid hai" — but its Cl⁻ gets
  oxidized, extra KMnO₄ is consumed, "value aapki jo calculation mein aayegi wo galat aa
  jaayegi." Rule: the acid must acidify, not participate. (HNO₃ out too — itself an OA.)
- **ON beyond max valency alarm:** H₂SO₅ naïve calc gives S = +8, CrO₅ gives +10 —
  "agar dikh raha hai ki sir yahan to aa raha hai eight — that means something is fishy."
  Look for the peroxy linkage (those O are −1); S and Cr cap at +6.
- **Two atoms of the same element ≠ same ON:** NH₄NO₃ — split into NH₄⁺ (N = −3) and
  NO₃⁻ (N = +5); "zaroori nahi ki same element ke atoms ka oxidation number same hoga."
- **"Alkaline" without weakly/strongly:** he openly flags the PYQ as under-specified —
  weakly alkaline ⇒ 3 e⁻, strongly ⇒ 1 e⁻; know both and read the examiner's wording.
- **Mohr salt disguise:** "kabhi Fe²⁺ directly bol denge, kabhi bolenge Mohr salt liya hua
  hai" — Mohr salt is just a source of ferrous ions; sulfate and NH₄⁺ sit out (S already +6).
- **Atoms before electrons in balancing:** "do nitrogen ko aap do se hi compare karoge —
  wo aisa mat bolna sir balancing ke baad do karenge. Pehle se hi karne hain."
- **Big-coefficient panic:** 68 H⁺, n = 61 for [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻ — "ghabrana nahi hai kitna bada
  number aa raha hai." His own honesty: "maine bhi pehli baar solve kiya to I was like —
  galti to nahi ho rahi? Kahin galti nahi ho rahi. Ye 61 hai."
- **Mental-math shortcut warning:** he shows the ratio trick (2.68/1.61 = 5/3 ⇒ n = 3)
  then warns — "mentally solve karte-karte hum kuchh cheezein skip kar jaate hain...
  ye cheez nahi karni hai unless until you are very sure."
- **Equivalent concept misuse:** "jab tak aapse mole concept aaraam se lag raha hai, do
  not go into the equivalent waale domain" — equivalents shine only when balancing is
  impossible (missing intermediate steps); applied blindly, "poora question galat ho gaya."

## D. Per-question exemplars (from DPP 1–2) — opening → key move → closing

**DPP 1**
1. **Xe oxidation state in Ba₂XeO₆** — Open: "20 second ke andar answer aa jaana chahiye
   — aasan question hai kaafi." Key: Ba is s-block group 2 ⇒ +2 each; ledger the charges
   (+4, −12) and Xe must pay the difference ⇒ +8. Close: "simple saral question" + NCERT
   tip: fractional-ON examples me se directly questions poochhe gaye hain.
2. **Lowest Fe oxidation state** — Open: "30 second... bada aasan question." Key: CO is a
   neutral molecule ⇒ in Fe(CO)₅ iron is ZERO ("the entire molecule is neutral"). Close:
   "simple question tha ye. Ye mostly PYQs hi hain."
3. **Iron oxide, 30% O by mass** — Open: "thoda sa pyaara question — calculation pe
   dimaag lagana padega. 1 minute maximum." Key: decode the statement (30 g O + 70 g Fe),
   then the moles-not-grams sermon → 30/16 : 70/56 → divide by smaller → ×2 → Fe₂O₃ ⇒ +3.
   Offers the backward route too: only FeO and Fe₂O₃ are plausible — test %O. "Backward
   bhi chal sakte ho."
4. **H₂O₂ with HOCl and I₂** — Open: "30 to 40 seconds max." Key: track Cl +1→−1 and
   I 0→−1 — both substrates reduced ⇒ "ye bhai sahab dono mein hi reducing agent ka kaam
   kar rahe hain." Close: "fairly simple question tha ye."
5. **N in aniline vs nitrobenzene** — Open: "30–35 second maximum." Key: draw the
   structure; every bond gives −1 to the more electronegative end — N pulls from all
   three bonds ⇒ −3; in nitrobenzene the coordinate bond is treated like the double bond
   ⇒ +3. Close: "to ye bhai sahab aapke +3 mein hain. −3 and +3, simple."
6. **Which is a redox reaction** — Open: "30 seconds maximum — identify karna bhaiya kaun
   sa hamara redox hai." Key: eliminate by ON audit — ozone is an allotrope (both 0),
   AgCl is "simply precipitation," acid–base has S already at max; N₂+O₂ at ~2000 K must
   give N positive, O negative ⇒ redox. Context aside: N₂ inert, 78% of atmosphere.
7. **Redox vs acid–base indicators (S1/S2)** — Open: "15 second ka question hai — sir ye
   to obvious hai." Key: full student-voice reasoning — redox titration ⇒ indicator rides
   oxidation potential, not pH; acid–base ⇒ pH. Both statements swapped ⇒ both incorrect.
8. **Alkaline permanganate, gain of electrons (PYQ)** — Open: he refuses the usual verdict
   — "examiner thoda aur precise hote to zyada achha lagta." Key: lay out all three media
   (5/3/1 electrons); "alkaline" unqualified is ambiguous. Close: honest dissent — "ye
   bahut well-thought question nahi hai; isme confusion ka scope reh gaya hai. Aapko pata
   lag gaya reality kya hai."
9. **KClO₃ decomposition type** — Open: "30 second, jaldi se — jin bachchon ne ye reaction
   padha hua hai, they already know." Key: K untouched; Cl +5→−1 reduced AND O −2→0
   oxidized — both inside ONE molecule ⇒ intramolecular redox.
10. **15 mL Fe²⁺ vs 20 mL 0.03 M dichromate** — Open: "fairly simple — 30 second lagaiye."
    Key: equivalents of Fe²⁺ = equivalents of dichromate; dichromate personified ("main to
    already +6 mein baitha hoon — main aapko oxidize karunga"); n = 1 vs 6 per molecule;
    Mohr-salt-as-source aside. Close: "aasan question tha" + NCERT-framework sermon —
    "jitne titration ke cases NCERT ne de rakhe hain, aapko wahi karne hain. NCERT define
    the framework — stick to that."

**DPP 2**
11. **Titrating aqueous NaOH (apparatus + acid choice)** — Open: "1 minute hai, aaraam se
    soch ke answer dijiye." Key: prefer the primary standard — oxalic acid over H₂SO₄
    (impurities, moisture) — "bhaiya agar option doge to hum primary standard lenge."
    Apparatus by elimination: "dono ko flask mein le liya to phir burette mein kya rahega?"
12. **Thiosulfate's dual behaviour with I₂ vs Br₂** — Open: "30–45 second ke andar."
    Key: per-2-sulfur bookkeeping (+4→+5 vs +4→+12); both are oxidations but bromine
    pushes further ⇒ stronger oxidant — and cross-check with the known order F > Cl > Br
    > I ("ye waise bhi true hai, aur yahan reaction se bhi evident ho raha hai").
13. **Count disproportionation-capable species** — Open: "45 seconds, jaldi se — aasan
    question hai ye." Key: apply the three-rungs rule species by species; F and K⁺ and Ag
    disqualified (no lower rung), Cu⁺ qualifies both ways. Close: "total chheh."
14. **Ca(OH)₂ / unknown H₂SO₄ back-titration** — Open: "dhyaan se padhna pehle poori
    statement ko" + honest critique: "ye statement thodi si confusing lag rahi hai... aapko
    focus itne pe karna hai ki 10 mL ka difference aaya." Key: solve part 2 first
    (milli-equivalents, HCl n = 1), feed molarity into part 1 via M₁V₁ = M₂V₂. Close:
    "question do part mein diya tha — second part se equation mili, first mein put kiya.
    Do practice such questions also."
15. **Primary standard — incorrect statements** — Open: "information-based question hai
    ye." Key: walk the property list (pure dry form, no chemical change in air, NOT
    hygroscopic, readily soluble); KMnO₄ and NaOH are NOT primary standards (NaCl/Na₂CO₃
    impurities, moisture). Close: the lab-manual sermon — "bahut saare question aap
    poochhte hain: sir, ye NCERT mein kahan hai? Ye lab manual mein milega. Do read lab
    manuals also."
16. **MnO₄⁻ + thiosulfate, ΔON(Mn) = 3 → which medium** — Open: "kuchh-kuchh yaad aaya?
    Last lecture mein aisa question kiya tha." Key: Δ3 ⇒ MnO₂ ⇒ neutral or faintly
    alkaline; kill the option that says alkaline AND acidic together. "Acidic to hata
    dijiye."
17. **Cu²⁺ → I₂ → hypo chain** — Open: "pyaara question hai — 1 minute. Mazze ki baat:
    reaction already diya hua hai, otherwise ye reactions yaad karne hote hain." Key: the
    equivalents chain x = x = x = x (Cu²⁺ = I⁻ = I₂ = hypo), then n(Cu²⁺) = 1, n(hypo) = 1.
    Close: "do-teen example solve kar liye, then you will know — is flow mein solve kiya
    jaata hai."
18. **Assertion–reason: no HCl in permanganate titrations** — Open: he skips writing the
    options — "wo likh ke main time waste nahi karna chahta tha." Key: chloride gets
    oxidized by KMnO₄ ⇒ extra consumption ⇒ wrong reading; safeguard rule — "aisa acid lo
    jo acidic medium de par reaction mein participate na kare." Close: both correct, reason
    explains assertion.
19. **1 mol each of FeC₂O₄ / Fe₂(C₂O₄)₃ / FeSO₄ / Fe₂(SO₄)₃ vs KMnO₄** — Open: "ye bhi
    bada pyaara question hai. 1 minute ke andar ho jaana chahiye — agar pata hai kya karna
    hai." Key: per-species n-factor audit (3, 6, 1, 0 — Fe³⁺ and sulfate sit out), sum
    equivalents, divide by 5. Close: "aasan question tha" — answer 2.
20. **NaOH + Na₂CO₃ + impurity, two indicators** — Open: "ye pyaara question hai, lamba
    bhi hai — isme aapko kuchh theory bhi explain karunga." Key: two indicators ⇒ the
    two-step carbonate story (phenolphthalein = first end point, methyl orange = second);
    NaOH, the strong base, "to immediately react karega — pehle step mein hi nipat gaya";
    the Na₂CO₃ n = 1 trap (above). Close: "is poore explanation ke baad is tarah ke
    question aap aasaani se kar paoge."

## E. Register notes

- Redox time budgets run TIGHT: 15s / 20s / 30s / 30–45s / "1 minute maximum" — he says
  the budget before nearly every DPP question; a 1-minute budget here already signals
  "thoda pyaara/calculation wala."
- Verdict vocabulary in this set: "simple saral question" · "bada aasan" · "fairly simple"
  · "pyaara question" (used for two-step/insight questions) · "information-based question"
  (for fact-recall) — and the honest pan: "ye bahut well-thought question nahi hai."
- Examiner-critique register is STRONGER here than in ATOM: he openly dissents on the
  ambiguous alkaline-KMnO₄ PYQ and on a confusing back-titration statement, models reading
  the examiner critically, and refuses to bless a flawed answer key.
- Doctrine lines unique to this chapter: "NCERT define the framework — stick to that"
  (titration cases) and the lab-manual extension ("whatever is not in the textbook is in
  the lab manual — do read lab manuals also").
- "Bhai sahab" personification density is highest of any chapter — dichromate, chlorine,
  sulfate, PbO₂, the lonely KMnO₄ drop all get speaking parts. In writing, keep it to ONE
  per solution (profile §2 limit).
- Meta-strategy lines worth reusing in ⚡/Shortcut: "balancing is the most important part
  of redox — uske bina bahut samasya hogi zindagi mein"; "is topic mein bahut deep mat
  jaana — time barbaad hoga sirf" (equivalent concept depth control); mole-vs-equivalent
  routing rule (C-bank last item).
- On-air self-correction appears twice (mis-stated answer, then "achha nahi nahi, galat
  bol diya maine") — honesty marker; never fabricate this in writing, but matter-of-fact
  correction tone is in-voice.
- Session closers here: "padhte rahiye, padhte rahiye" · "keep smiling" · "confidence
  loose nahi karna hai" — sessions only, never solutions.
