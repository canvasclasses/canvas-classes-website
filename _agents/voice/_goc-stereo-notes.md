## Must-memorize (his explicit ratta list for this chapter)

- **DU formula + the two riders:** DU = (C+1) + (X−H−N... as he runs it: nC+1, −H, +X, −N, /2); "Oxygen, sulphur — they do not affect the degree of unsaturation." Nitrogen brings one extra H (remove both). Halogen counts like hydrogen. DU can never be fractional or negative — "agar hai, matlab wo compound exist nahi karega."
- **No formula for counting isomers:** "Sir, koi jugaad hai, shortcut, formula? Nahin. Number of isomers nikaalne ka formula nahi hota. Likh ke, draw karke karna padta hai" — and common sense, because silly mistakes happen.
- **Tautomer pairs to memorize:** keto–enol, nitroso–oxime, imine–enamine, nitro–aci-nitro. "Yaad kar lo, pause kar lo, likh lo ek baar achhe se."
- **Enol % ladder (1,3-diketone family):** acetylacetone enol ≈ 76%; one ester → ~8%; both esters → ~10⁻³ (negligible — "ester ke saath enol nahi banta"); both Ph → 100% (extended conjugation). Stabilizers of enol: H-bonding, conjugation, aromaticity, hyperconjugation.
- **GI counting formulas:** dissimilar ends → 2ⁿ. Similar ends, even n → 2^(n−1) + 2^(n/2 − 1). Similar ends, odd n → 2^(n−1) + 2^((n−1)/2). His memory hook: "ye n−2/2 hai, ye n−1/2 hai — wo aapke liye convenient ho jayega." But he derives the n=2 case from sis-trans = trans-sis identity first.
- **2ⁿ stereoisomer formula scope:** "Jab symmetry hoti hai toh 2^n sahi answer nahi deta." Symmetry check FIRST, formula second.
- **E2 requirement:** leaving group and H must be **anti-coplanar**; convert Fischer → Newman directly (junction trick) before eliminating.
- **Fischer rules (absolute, drilled):** never flip a Fischer in/out of plane ("flip karne se enantiomer generate hota hai"); in-plane 180° rotation is allowed (top↔bottom, others shift diagonally); **never check centre of symmetry in a Fischer** — "never ever check the Fischer projection for centre of symmetry. Only check for plane of symmetry"; even number of exchanges = same compound, odd = mirror image; both exchanges must be **on the same chiral centre**.
- **R/S chant:** priorities 1-2-3-4 by atomic number → take least priority to the back (or do one exchange and invert the answer) → walk 1→2→3 → clockwise R, anticlockwise S. Fischer shortcut: least priority on horizontal → flip the answer; on vertical → keep it.
- **D/L naming:** relative nomenclature w.r.t. glyceraldehyde — vertical chain, most oxidized carbon on top, lowest chiral centre decides; "is D ka dextro–levo se koi relation nahi hai."
- **Specific rotation:** α = θ/(l·c), l in **decimeter**, c in g/mL; α is constant — change length/concentration and θ compensates. Enantiomers rotate equal and opposite.
- **Decalins:** "Decalin mein bachche yaad hi rakh lena hai... paper mein on the spot draw karke compare nahi karoge. Yaad hi karna padega." Linear arrangement = trans decalin (more stable); the 90°-twisted one = cis.
- **Allenes:** even number of C=C → terminal groups in perpendicular planes → optical isomerism possible; odd number → terminal groups coplanar → geometrical isomerism possible. Both ends need two different groups.
- **Atropisomers:** tetra-ortho-substituted biphenyls; the two ortho groups on each ring must differ.

## Traps (the reflex he dramatizes → his correction)

- **Angle strain in OPEN chains:** "Are, jab open chain hai toh ring toh wahan hai nahi — actual angle aur ideal angle mein difference kahan se aayega? Angle strain kahan se aa jayega open chain mein?" (Factor-elimination question.)
- **"Aromatic enol must dominate" (triketone/phloroglucinol):** expectation vs experiment. "Hum sochte hain ye bahut zyada stable hoga... par ek carbonyl nahi, teen hain. Wo difference of energy three se multiply ho gaya. No doubt aromaticity ka fayda hoga, par aisa nahi ki jameen-aasmaan ka fark aa jayega." He cites the 2,4-DNP test coming positive as the experimental giveaway. Never assert from theory alone.
- **Assertion-reason percentage trap (acetylacetone enol "15%"):** "Jaldbaazi mein hum statement ko padhte hain toh lagta hai sahi toh bol raha hai — isme zyada hoti hai, usme kam. Par percentage ka dhyaan nahi rakha toh galti kar sakte ho. And that will be a silly mistake." (Actual ≈ 76%.)
- **Counting isomers when not all carbons match:** "Jab bhi question solve karo, pehle number of carbon check kar lo. Pata laga isomer hai hi nahin, aur tum soche ja rahe ho ki sir kaun sa isomer hoga ye."
- **1°/2°/3° amines look like chain isomers:** "Hai toh amine teenon, par ye functional isomer consider kiye jaate hain. Ye point logically fit nahi hota dimaag mein — sir, amine toh amine hai. But bas, they are considered. Isme zyada discussion ka scope nahi hai." (Licensed ratta for a convention.)
- **Amide vs amine+aldehyde (condensed formulas):** "Kai baar jaldbaazi mein jaise hum usko sochte hain aur jaisa wo hota hai, usme difference aa jaata hai. Doubt ho toh expand karke likh lo." N-ethyl formamide vs 2-aminopropanal. "Ek-do baar silly mistakes karoge, next time se wo yaad rahega."
- **C–H "hydrogen bonding" in gauche questions:** "Aap bologe — sir uske paas bhi H hai. Ye wala H H-bonding ke liye capable nahi hai. Kyon? Kyonki ye carbon se juda hua hai. Wo hydrogen participate karta hai jo electronegative atom se juda ho."
- **Eclipsed + closer ⇒ better H-bond (false):** "Aap bologe — sir, ab toh aur nazdeek aa gaye, aur achhi H-bonding hogi. Nahin — wahan jo repulsion aur torsional strain develop hoga, wo dominate kar jayega."
- **Anti is NOT always the most stable:** gauche wins when H-bonding is possible (OH with G = OH, NH₂, F, NO₂, COOH, CHO at 60°). "Aisa mat sochna ki hamesha anti hi zyada stable hota hai."
- **H-bonding is not always the priority either:** two tert-butyls adjacent — "wahan pe H-bonding hamari priority nahi hai. Wahan hai ki tert-butyl ko door rakha jaye. Tert-butyl is a very bulky group."
- **"One optically active isomer" miscount:** "Kai baar bachche bolte hain — sir, chaar mein se ek toh hai optically active, ek answer ho jayega. Are, ye ek nahi hai. Ye chiral hai, toh dextro form mein ek exist karega, ek levo hoga. Wo do hue na basically." (C4H9Br → answer 2.)
- **"Looks like a mirror image" meso trap:** "Aap bologe — sir, ye toh directly dikh rahe hain mirror image: Br ke saamne Br, H ke saamne H... But ek interchange karo, phir doosra — plane of symmetry aa gayi. Wo chiral hai hi nahi. That is a meso. Toh uska enantiomer toh hai hi nahi — ye dono identical hue." (His "pyaara question" verdict.)
- **The answer-can't-be-zero panic:** number of diastereomers of a single-chiral-centre compound = 0. "Aise question mein doubt hota hai ki zero kaise aa sakta hai answer — hum galti toh nahi kar rahe? And that takes you further: ya toh bewakoofi karwa dega high-level thinking mein, ya time waste karoge aur ultimately sahi kar doge par time waste."
- **Counting FIXED stereocentres (2018 Advanced wavy-bond PYQ):** "Jahan pe stereochemistry fix hai, wahan aap sochna nahi hai ki sir ye bhi chiral centre hai, ye bhi... Configuration is fixed. Us pure part ko ek single unit ki tarah consider karo. Possibility sirf wahan hai jahan wavy bond hai — this is where geometry is not defined." Then 2³ = 8, minus the one with a plane of symmetry → 7.
- **Ring R/S centres are COUPLED:** (IIT spiro/ring PYQ) "Aap socho ki R aur S hai, toh main isko R hi rakhoon aur usko badal doon — this is not possible. Kyonki agar ye paltega toh wo bhi paltega saath mein. Because it is all connected." 2² = 4 is the formula answer; the real answer is 2. "Formula lagaoge toh galat answer aayega. Jisne dhyaan se samjha hai concept ko, wahi ye cheez kar paayega."
- **sp2 substituent on a ring can't make cis/trans:** "Sir, abhi-abhi aapne bola do methyl up/down ho sakte hain? Dikkat ye hai — ye wala carbon sp2 hai. Us pe jo group laga hai wo ring ke plane mein hai. Upar nahi, neeche nahi." (Mobile-phone plane demo.)
- **Ring-internal double bonds:** no GI unless ring ≥ 8 members — "ring rigid hai, trans banaoge toh itna twist aa jayega, bahut unstable."
- **"All three up" vs "all three down" double-count:** "Ye mat bolna — ek baar teenon up, ek baar teenon down. Wo same cheez hai. Molecule ko rotate karne se wo badal nahi jaata. Neeche se dekhoge toh teenon down dikhenge." Likewise up-down-up = up-up-down on a C3 ring. "Is tareeke se likhoge toh tumhare bahut saare structure ban jayenge — par wo actually same honge. Draw karke hi pata lagega."
- **D ≠ d (capital-D/L vs dextro/levo):** "Is D ka dextro–levo se koi relation nahi hai. Capital D-L glyceraldehyde ki respect mein define kiya jaata hai." And D-glyceraldehyde is the reference, not D-glucose.
- **50% rotation ⇒ 50:50 mixture (false):** "Mind mein aayega — agar S 50% hai toh baaki 50% R hoga? Nahin. 50-50 loge toh net rotation zero ho jayegi. 50% S ka matlab hai ye SURPLUS hai. Overall 75% S, 25% R — 25-25 cancel, effectively 50% pure S bacha. Isiliye rotation bhi exactly 50% aayi."
- **Specific vs observed rotation:** "Wo bada kuch kar raha hai — concentration half, length change... Beta, alpha ki value same rehti hai. Length ya concentration mein change karoge toh THETA change hoga, alpha nahi. Unhone specific rotation diya hai, observed nahi."
- **Chiral centre necessary for chirality (false):** "Allenes mein koi chiral centre nahi hota, phir bhi wo chiral hain. Non-superimposable hona is THE essential condition." (Axial chirality, atropisomers.)
- **Visualization without proof:** "Visualization ka error ye hai — aapke paas proof toh hai nahi; jo aap soch rahe ho 'aise karke ho jayega, ho jayega', usme galti hone ke chance hote hain. Doosra tareeka: R/S naming se usko confirm kar lo."
- **E2 stereochemistry last-step fumble:** "Is tarah ke question mein bachche kaafi phanste hain — elimination karwaane ke baad kaun sa group kidhar jayega, wahan galti kar dete hain. Last tak theek hota hai, last mein bas orientation mein galti ho jaati hai, aur wahin pe poora score zero ho jaata hai."

## 3D-visualization narration moves (how he makes students "see")

1. **The double-flip protocol (compare two drawn 3D structures):** flip vertically, then horizontally, tracking ONE bond's front/back fate out loud: "Jab isko maine aise palta, toh jo front mein tha wo back mein chala gaya. Back mein jaane ke baad phir 180° pe palta — ab wo phir front mein aa gaya, par is taraf ko. Horizontal flip karoge toh upar ki cheez upar rahegi, neeche ki neeche — sirf left-right ka difference aayega." Always offers R/S naming as the error-proof alternative.
2. **Sense-of-rotation matching (Newman/wedge identity):** "Pehle connectivity check karo. Phir is carbon pe clockwise chalo — CH3, Cl, H. Udhar bhi CH3, Cl, H? Rotation ka sense match kar raha hai → identical. Ek match kare, ek na kare → not identical" (and later: that's a diastereomer).
3. **Rotation-is-free doctrine (vs flip):** "Kisi bhi object ko rotate karne se it doesn't change — jaise abhi main aapko dikh raha hoon, ghoom jaoon toh main abhi bhi wahi rahoonga." Used to kill double-counting (R-cis-S vs S-cis-R; teenon-up vs teenon-down).
4. **Fischer → Newman junction trick (his named shortcut):** "Ye upar ek junction hai, ye neeche ek junction. Upar wale ko as-it-is saamne bana do; neeche wale junction ke horizontal groups ki adla-badli kar do — left wale ko right, right wale ko left." For eclipsed: keep horizontals same-side, bring up/down COOH both up.
5. **The V-flattening move (E2 product geometry):** after HX leaves the Newman, "ye jo V shape hai na — isko bas horizontal kar dena hai, flat. Ye tetrahedral tha, ab trigonal planar mein aa jayega. Front wale CH3 ke exactly peechhe kya aa raha hai? Wo wala Ph. Toh trans." Alternative voice: "front aur back carbon ke beech double bond aa gaya — ye dono same side, ye dono same side. Simple like this."
6. **Exchange-counting engine (Fischer/wedge comparisons):** even number of exchanges = same compound; odd = mirror image; BOTH exchanges on the same junction ("ye mat karna ki ek exchange upar wale pe, ek neeche wale pe — same chiral centre pe even number of exchange karwaane hote hain").
7. **Implicit-H-at-back shortcut:** the undrawn fourth bond is H at the back — skip drawing, go straight to 1→2→3.
8. **Live props, always:** ball-and-stick models for eclipsed/staggered rotation, restricted C=C rotation ("rotate karoonga, bond toot jayega"), chirality non-superimposition ("blue-blue match kar rahe, par red aur green nahi — kaise bhi ghuma lo, superimpose nahi hone wale"); the mobile phone as a ring plane; the pen for 180° flips; his own body for conformations; an improvised 4-membered ring model when a question needed it ("pause karke dekhte hain... yes, I found something").
9. **Plane-cutting narration (POS/COS hunt):** always physical: "Isko beech mein se cut karo — idhar ko white aa gaya, idhar ko blue. Symmetry aayi? Nahin." COS as a line-extension test: "ek direction mein gaya toh CH3, wahi line doosri direction mein — CH3. Peechhe ko H, aage ko Cl — match nahi kiya, toh centre of symmetry nahi."
10. **Perpendicular-planes lemma (allene/atropisomer):** "Do double bond jab ikatthe hote hain, they are mutually perpendicular. Pehla is plane mein, agla perpendicular plane mein." Odd count → ends coplanar → treat as one long C=C. Biphenyl: "ek benzene is plane mein, doosri perpendicular — taaki ortho groups aamne-saamne na hon."
11. **Fixed-unit collapse (complex PYQs):** freeze everything whose stereochemistry is drawn ("configuration is fixed — us pure part ko ek single unit ki tarah consider karo, bhool jao ki ye chiral centre hai"), vary only wavy bonds; shrink rings for convenience.
12. **sp2-in-plane test:** substituent on sp2 ring carbon lies IN the ring plane (phone demo) — cannot be up/down compared with an sp3 substituent.
