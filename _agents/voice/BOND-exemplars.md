## B. Procedures + must-memorize items he teaches (use in ⚡ sections)

- **Shape-prediction procedure (his fixed thought-chain, narrate in this order):** "Central
  atom pakdo → uski valency dekho → kitne bond ban rahe hain → bache electron = lone
  pairs → steric factor = bonds + lone pairs → geometry table." Ion charge move: "jo bhi
  negative/positive charge aata hai, usko central atom ke upar rakh ke imagine karo."
- **Steric factor counting:** a bond is a bond — "single ho, double ho, triple ho — wo EK
  hi position hai." Bonds + lone pairs = positions; arrange for minimum repulsion.
- **His hybridisation shortcut (he flags it as his own):** "Mera apna tareeka, thoda
  shortcut bhi lagta hai — sigma bonds + FREE lone pairs gino" (free = not delocalised in
  resonance). 2→sp, 3→sp², 4→sp³, 5→sp³d, 6→sp³d², 7→sp³d³.
- **Fixed bonding facts he drills:** fluorine ALWAYS single bond; oxygen either two
  singles or one double ("isiliye Xe=O double banega — ek hi taraf se jud raha hai").
- **TBP placement rules:** lone pairs + multiple bonds → equatorial; more electronegative
  atom → axial. Axial bonds slightly longer & weaker (PCl₅ breaks axially on heating).
- **Shape vs geometry:** geometry counts the lone pair, shape forgets it ("lone pair ko
  bhool jao").
- **Repulsion order:** lp–lp > lp–bp > bp–bp. Angle deviation = "deviation hai to koi
  additional unbalanced repulsion hai" — H₂O 104.5°, NH₃ 107°, PH₃ ~94°.
- **Drago rule:** central atom below period 2 + terminal EN < 2.5 → no hybridisation,
  pure p orbitals, angles near 90° ("angle se bhi confirm hua — 90° ke zyada close hai").
- **Bond order from resonance — count, don't memorise:** total bonds ÷ equivalent
  positions. ClO₄⁻ = 7/4 = 1.75; ClO₃⁻ = 5/3; CO₃²⁻ = 4/3. Count out loud.
- **MOT bond-order shortcut (he stresses it's legitimate):** electrons 8–14 → (n−8)/2;
  15–20 → (20−n)/2. "Ye koi jugaad nahi hai — it's properly published in a research paper."
- **Isoelectronic ⇒ same bond order:** CO/NO⁺/CN⁻ ~ N₂ (BO 3); O₂²⁻ ~ F₂ (BO 1).
- **sp mixing memory hook:** up to N₂ → σ2p and π2p interchange ("bas, sirf ye ek cheez
  different hai — baaki sab same"). Reason: 2s–2p energies close in light elements;
  "orbitals of same symmetry repel each other." From O₂ onward, normal order.
- **Nodal-plane count (direct question bank):** σ bonding 0, σ* 1, π bonding 1, π* 2.
- **LCAO conditions mnemonic:** **EOS** — comparable **E**nergy, effective **O**verlap,
  proper **S**ymmetry. (Same-shell s and p only: "3s ke saath 3p; 3p + 4s nahi chalega.")
- **Equal bond order tiebreak:** compare antibonding electrons — fewer antibonding
  electrons = more stable (antibonding destabilizes more than bonding stabilizes).
- **HOMO/LUMO/SOMO:** "bhare hue mein highest = HOMO; khaali dabbon mein sabse neeche =
  LUMO; half-filled = SOMO." N₂/CO HOMO = σ2p; NO/O₂ outermost = π*.
- **Values he anchors to NCERT:** NH₃ 1.47 D vs NF₃ 0.23 D, H₂O 1.85 D; CH₃Cl > CH₃F
  dipole (NCERT haloalkanes, asked in JEE Main); HF chain = zigzag, H at the bend, F at
  corners, 120° ("dhyaan se likh ke ek baar practice karna, nahi to yaad nahi rahega");
  PCl₅ axial/equatorial bond lengths in p-block; ClF₅ colorless liquid in p-block table 7.11.
- **Master structures, not question counts:** "Once you understand the geometry, chahe
  dipole pooch le, hybridisation pooch le, shape pooch le, lone pair pooch le — if you know
  the structure properly you can answer all of those questions."

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **SiO₂ ≠ CO₂ structure:** "Kaafi bachche galti karte hain — sir, carbon dioxide jaisa
  structure banayega kya? Nahi. Ye network solid banata hai" — Si bonded to FOUR O ⇒ sp³.
- **KCl vs NaCl melting point:** Fajans reflex says KCl more ionic ⇒ higher MP. Wrong —
  lattice energy (q₁q₂/r², smaller r) controls MP: NaCl 801° > KCl 770°. "Sirf Fajans rule
  nahi lagana wahan."
- **"HCl ionizes, so it must be ionic":** "Are, paani mein jaake tootta hai — hydration ki
  wajah se. Bottle mein wo molecule hai." Never argue ionic character from aqueous behavior.
- **BF₃ acidity order reversal:** reflex — F strongest withdrawing ⇒ BF₃ most acidic.
  "Expected tha. Hua nahi. Bilkul reverse ho gaya" — 2p–2p back bonding feeds boron.
- **PF₃ vs PH₃ angle reversal:** the "expected vs reality" frame — lone-pair push logic
  predicts PH₃ bigger; back bonding makes PF₃ (98°) bigger. Same frame for Cl₂O at 111°
  (the common lone-pair-on-Cl explanation is "less accurate" — back bonding is the real one).
- **Steric factor 4 ⇒ 90°? :** "Aap usko 2D mein assume kar rahe ho. Molecule poora 3D
  mein hota hai" — 109.5° beats 90°.
- **CH₄ vs CCl₄ angle:** "bachche kuch high-fi level ka dimaag lagayenge" — both are
  perfect tetrahedra, 109.5° each; no unbalanced forces, nothing to compare.
- **CH₃F vs CH₃Cl dipole:** reflex — F more electronegative ⇒ CH₃F higher. μ = q×d; C–F
  bond is shorter; experimentally CH₃Cl slightly higher. NCERT-listed exception.
- **Lone pair dipole contribution:** only a HYBRID-orbital lone pair contributes; pure s
  (spherical) and pure p (two opposite lobes) contribute nothing.
- **Inter vs intra H-bonding:** ortho-nitrophenol is INTRA ("within the same molecule");
  HF–HF is inter. Statement questions swap these deliberately.
- **OCH₃ can't donate H-bond:** "Oxygen to hai, H nahi hai — saamne ek aur OCH₃ rakh do,
  H-bonding nahi hogi." NH₂ has both N and a capable H.
- **PCl₅ axial bonds:** the incorrect-statement bait is "axial stronger than equatorial" —
  axial are LONGER hence WEAKER ("isiliye heat karne pe axial hi tootte hain").
- **PCl₅ in solid state:** it's ionic — [PCl₄]⁺ (tetrahedral) [PCl₆]⁻ (octahedral).
  "Hybridisation badal jaayegi based on solid state poocha hai ya gaseous."
- **I₃⁺ vs I₃⁻:** two-electron difference — reflex carries the linear I₃⁻ picture over;
  I₃⁺ is bent (two lone pairs, steric factor 4).
- **BrF₄⁻ square planar, not pyramidal:** Br⁻ = 8 valence electrons, "just like XeF₄."
  Square pyramidal needs 5σ + 1 lp (IF₅, BrF₅, XeOF₄, ClF₅).
- **He₂ "gaps cancel" fallacy:** if stabilization = destabilization, He₂ would exist. The
  gaps are NOT equal — NCERT draws them unequal, "kai books mein same dikhaya hai, wo galat hai."
- **OF₂ oxidation state:** reflex writes O as −2; F is more electronegative, O is +2 here.
- **N₂ vs CN⁻ reactivity:** both triple bond, isoelectronic — the difference is POLARITY
  (electrophilic C centre), not bond order. "IIT ne ek baar poocha hai. Bada pyara question."
- **H₂S vs H₂O dipole, anticipated objection:** "Aapke mind mein aa sakta hai — par sir,
  bond length bhi to matter karti hai. Yes. Par bond length ka difference utna nahi hai
  jitna electronegativity ka" — name the half-right objection, then weigh the factors.
- **Examiner-wording critique (license to flag bad questions):** on a vague NTA statement
  — "ye mere ko badi kharab cheez lagti hai NTA ki — they are not very precise. Vague si
  statement denge, aap apna dimaag lagaate raho." On a sloppy count question: "question
  thoda achhe se design hota — 'ONLY one unpaired' likh dete to clear hota."

## D. Per-question exemplars — opening → key move → closing

1. **No covalent bond (KCl) [L1]** — Open: "30 seconds for this simple question." Key:
   H₂SO₄ trap — ionic between H⁺/SO₄²⁻ AND covalent inside sulfate, "dono tarah ki bonding
   hai." Close: precision note — "predominantly" matters, some % always exists.
2. **Increasing ionic character: LiF/K₂O/N₂/SO₂/ClF₃ [L1+L4]** — Open: kill the easy ends
   first ("N₂ purely covalent, sabse peeche — do option wahin out"). Key: K₂O vs LiF is "a
   close call — kaafi bachche yahan confuse honge" (honest difficulty flag); SO₂ vs ClF₃
   via Fajans on oxidation states (+4/−2 vs +3/−1). Close: option elimination did the work.
3. **Lattice energy CaO = 4U of NaCl [L1]** — Open: "ye cheez aapko karwai thodi der
   pehle." Key: r same ⇒ only q₁q₂ left; 1×1 vs 2×2. Close: instant — no calculation.
4. **Max covalent character FeCl₂/SnCl₂/AlCl₃/MgCl₂ [L1]** — Key move he repeats all
   chapter: "anion sab mein same hai — jo common hai usko compare MAT karo." Then highest
   charge/smallest cation wins.
5. **CuSO₄·5H₂O bond types [L1]** — Key: walks all four bond types; mid-solve honesty —
   "mujhe exact formula yaad nahi aa raha abhi" — flags it, still answers what's certain.
   Close: "saare ke saare type ke bonds yahan bante hain."
6. **Max resonating structures [L1]** — On-air verification gate: counts give a tie —
   "isme kuch gadbad to nahi hai? Abhi ke liye skip karte hain, next lecture mein discuss
   karenge." Models refusing to force an answer.
7. **Highest melting point (NaCl vs KCl + BaO/MgO) [L1]** — Open: "30 seconds." Key: THE
   Fajans-vs-lattice-energy collision (trap C above), then verifies against the data table
   shown earlier — "main just confirm kar raha hoon, values table mein thi."
8. **Bond order 1.75 [L1]** — Key: draw, count bonds out loud ("2 4 5... 7 ko 4 mein
   divide karna hai"), ClO₄⁻ = 7/4. Close: ClO₃⁻ = 5/3 rejected by the same chant.
9. **Linear + no d-orbital count [L2]** — Open: "1 minute, jaldi se." Key: pair conditions
   — must be linear AND d-free; N₃⁻/N₂O/NO₂⁺/BeCl₂ in, XeF₂ linear but d-using, out.
   Close: "fast-fast karna hai — is pe practice sabse zyada."
10. **Bond angle nearly 90° (H₂S) [L2]** — Key: Drago recall — "abhi humne padha hai" —
    no hybridisation, pure p, ~90°. Close: one-line, no ceremony.
11. **Increasing angle BF₃/PF₃/ClF₃ [L2]** — Key: classify by geometry first (120° /
    <109.5 / 90°); side-note correction — ClF₃ axial angle is NOT 180° (lone-pair asymmetry
    pushes it in, like SF₄ at 178°): "ye dhyaan mujhe aaya, aapko pehle nahi bataya."
12. **Charged comb deflection (IIT PYQ) [L2]** — Open: story setup (comb, flowing
    streams). Key: one logic line — "polar cheez polar ko attract karegi" — then a pure
    polarity census, including H₂O₂'s open-book structure ("thoda sa effort karo is pe").
13. **Max dipole NH₃ vs NX₃ [L2]** — Key: "same wahi logic jo NH₃/NF₃ ka tha" — lone pair
    and bond dipoles aligned only in NH₃; NCl₃ near-zero EN difference dramatized.
14. **N₂ less reactive than CN⁻ [L2]** — Open: "bada pyara question. IIT ne ek baar
    poocha hai." Key: eliminate look-alike reasons (both triple, isoelectronic) before the
    real one — polarity creates an electrophilic centre. Close: if N₂ reacted, "duniya
    bhar ke reactions hona start ho jaate" (78% of atmosphere).
15. **PH₅/BiCl₅ don't exist + pπ–dπ in SO₂ [L2]** — Key: voiced student push-back — "sir,
    PCl₅ banta hai to PH₅ kyon nahi?" — answered via orbital-size shrinking by
    electronegative Cl; then p-orbital bookkeeping for SO₂ ("teen p the, do hybridisation
    mein, teesra is π mein — to doosra π d se aayega").
16. **Pyramidal species, OSF₂ [L2]** — Key: central-atom selection rule first ("wo jiski
    valency zyada / jo octet exceed kar sake — oxygen nahi, sulfur"), then steric count.
    Close: "just like NH₃."
17. **Two nodal planes (π*) [L3]** — Open: "abhi-abhi humne padha." Key: recite the 0/1/1/2
    table. Close: instant.
18. **NO → NO⁺, electron from where [L3]** — Key: outermost is π* ("panauti gayi" relief
    framing). Close: ties to why NO⁺ bond is stronger (BO 2.5 → 3).
19. **Stabilized by anion formation (C₂) [L3]** — Open: "1 minute for this question." Key:
    TWO checks, not one — bond order rises AND the new electron lands in σ2p BONDING

20. **Li₂ vs Li₂⁻ stability [L3]** — Key: the tiebreak rule born from a question — bond

21. **Cl₂ + hot conc. NaOH, bond order in Y [L3]** — Open: "30 seconds. Aasaan question."

23. **sp³ count (SiO₂ trap) [L4]** — Open: "30 seconds for this simple question." Key: the

## E. Register notes

- **Crash-course cadence:** every question gets a pause prompt + budget — "You can pause
  the video here and try it for 1 minute." Budgets are 30 s for "simple," 1 min for
  counting/multi-statement. A repeated "30 seconds for this simple question" IS the
  difficulty verdict.
- **Lecture meta-framing he does at open/close:** L1 "thoda lightweight rahega aaj ka,
  warm-up"; L2 promised as "heavy-weight... zyada mazedaar"; close of L2 justifies the

  kiya"). Session-management talk stays in sessions, NOT in solutions.
- **On-air honesty is constant currency:** forgets CuSO₄·5H₂O exact formula and says so;
  parks a possibly-buggy question for next lecture; corrects himself mid-sentence ("sorry,
  ulta bol diya"); critiques NTA wording; near-miss humor — "aaj slide nahi daali thi.
  Bach gaye. Baal-baal bach gaye."

  smile hi bana de — aankhein to laga di humne"), the jhoola, the awara lone pair,
  "panauti gayi." One per solution max, per profile hard limits.

- **Closings rotate:** "bahut simple question tha" · "aasaan question tha ye" · "bada
  pyara question" · "done. that's it" · pattern-lesson closes. Session signoffs ("padhte
  rahiye" family / "bane raho, baithe raho, padhte raho" / "all the best, bye") are
  session-only — never in solutions.
