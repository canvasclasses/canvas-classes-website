## B. Must-memorize items he teaches (use in ⚡ sections)

- **Pauling formula** — χA − χB = 0.208·√[E(A−B) − √(E(A−A)·E(B−B))] (bond energies in kcal/mol): "yaad kar lo — iske upar DIRECT question aata hai." Three bond-energy data points needed (A₂, B₂, AB).
- **Mulliken** — EN = (IE + EA)/2: "Mulliken ne kaha boss, electronegativity kya hai? Ionization energy aur electron affinity ka average."
- **Allred–Rochow** — 3590·Zeff/r²cov + 0.744 (r in pm; 0.359 if other units — "aise pareshan nahi hona hai"). Fundamental basis = electrostatic force between valence electron and Zeff.
- **% ionic character = 16Δ + 3.5Δ²** (Δ = EN difference). Rule of thumb: Δ > 1.7 predominantly ionic, ≈1.7 fifty-fifty, < 1.7 covalent — but "patthar ki lakeer nahi hai" (HF: Δ > 1.7 yet only 45%).
- **EN values (Pauling):** F 3.98 ≈ 4, O 3.44, Cl 3.16; N ≈ Cl (comparable); alkalis < 1. Lanthanides all ≈ 1.1 — "identical." Groups 10/11/12 increase DOWN the group (anomaly worth noting from the table).
- **Heteroatomic bond length:** d(A−B) = rA + rB − 0.09·ΔEN — only for hetero-atomic molecules.
- **nth-period filling order:** ns → (n−2)f → (n−1)d → np. Period counts: 2, 8, 8, 18, 18, 32, 32 — and period number = highest principal quantum number in the configuration.
- **IUPAC naming digits** (un/nil/bi… + ium) + the named ones worth ratta: Mendelevium 101, Rutherfordium 104, Seaborgium 106, Bohrium 107.
- **Oxide nature lists** — amphoteric (BeO, ZnO, SnO, SnO₂, Al₂O₃, PbO…), neutral (CO, N₂O, NO, MnO₂); Mn ladder MnO basic → Mn₂O₃ → MnO₂ neutral → MnO₃/Mn₂O₇ acidic ("saari variety mil gayi aapko manganese ke oxides mein"). His instruction: "yahan pause karke likh lo, note down kar lo — jis speed se main dikha raha hoon usi speed se yaad to nahi hoga."
- **Oxyacid → anhydride:** remove H₂O (H₂SO₃→SO₂, HNO₃→N₂O₅, HClO₄→Cl₂O₇ …). Integer-type questions: "10 oxide de diye — kitne amphoteric, kitne acidic, kitne basic."
- **NCERT data points he flags as direct questions:** EA pairs Rb/Cs = 47/46 ("very close"), Ar/Kr = 96/96 ("exactly identical"); Zn IE 906 vs Ga 579; Eu neutral radius 199 pm (the spike); Gd = 4f⁷5d¹6s² (Z 64), Eu = 4f⁷ (Z 63); Ga & Cs melting points 303/302 K → "garmiyon mein room temperature pe they will be in liquid state."
- **Locate any Z via nearest noble gas** — "noble gas ke saath relate karna sabse achha rehta hai" (36→33 As; 54→53 I; 83 from 53+32→Bi). This replaces memorising the table.
- **Group fingerprints from IE jumps:** huge I1→I2 gap = alkali; huge I2→I3 gap = group 2. I1 < I2 < I3 < I4 always.

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **HI is a strong acid ⇒ ionic? NO.** "Sochne mein lagta hai sir HI to strong acid hai, ekdam se ionise ho jata hai... That's a separate reason — bond lamba hai to weak hai, jaldi tootta hai. Pure HI ionic form mein nahi tha. Paani mein daalne ke BAAD toota." Only 4% ionic character.
- **Δ > 1.7 ⇒ >50% ionic — not a law.** HF: "sir iska difference to 1.7 se zyada aa gaya, 50% se zyada hona chahiye. Par aa raha hai 45%. Isiliye maine kaha — patthar ki lakeer nahi hai."
- **EN of an isolated atom doesn't exist.** "Sir akela atom ho to kya EN define ki ja sakti hai? Nahi. Uske liye electron affinity define karte ho. EN tabhi aayegi jab atom bonded hai."
- **EN is not a constant of the element.** Environment (CH₄ vs CHF₃), hybridisation (sp > sp² > sp³, s-character), and charge all change it.
- **Equal EN ⇒ same H-bonding? NO (N vs Cl).** "Aap bologe sir EN to barabar hai, phir kya funda denge?... Size also is an important factor. Sirf EN values pe answer nahi de sakte H-bonding ka."
- **"Net pe value different thi."** "Ye mat bolna sir maine net ke upar search kiya tha, wahan value different thi — Pauling scale ki baat kar rahe hain. There are different scales."
- **Poor shielding — students memorise the phrase, misplace the element.** "Pata laga bachche ne yaad kar li 'poor shielding of d electrons'. Kisme sir? Aluminium mein. Aise kaam nahi karna. Ye bhi pata hona chahiye KAHAN ho rahi hai — Gallium ke andar ho rahi hai."
- **IE exceptions come in pairs and recycle:** Be > B, N > O — "same logic Mg–Al ke liye bhi lagega, P–S ke liye bhi." Don't relearn; transfer.
- **Group 13 IE dip:** Ga (579) < Zn (906) — "lagega ki sir along the period ja rahe hain na, zyada kyon nahi hai?" — s and d both filled in Zn = stable; Ga's lone p¹ "easily removed." The 13th-group members break the increasing trend — "sudden deep maarta hai wo."
- **Second IE ≠ removing two electrons.** "I2 ka matlab do electron nikalna nahi hota. Ek nikal chuke the — ab M⁺ se doosra nikal raha hai. Total do nikalne hain to IE1 + IE2 deni padegi."
- **Second EA endothermic even when noble-gas config results (O²⁻).** "Noble gas configuration to mili, par itni repulsion create ho jayegi... repulsion outweighs the stability gain." Oxygen "chahta to hai" — wanting isn't enough.
- **F vs Cl, O vs S:** third-period member wins EA — small size = inter-electronic repulsion. "Chahta bhi hai aur uske paas jagah bhi hai — dono to chahne se nahi hota na."
- **Noble gas EA:** all endothermic; MOST endothermic = Ne, LEAST = He — "ye question aa sakta hai aapko" (both flagged as potential questions from the NCERT table).
- **Lanthanum is NOT a lanthanoid.** "Bachche galti kya karte hain — lanthanum ko lanthanoids mein sochte hain. Nahi. Lanthanoid Cerium se start hote hain. Properties milti hain isliye ikatthe padhte hain — that doesn't mean member hai."
- **Group 12 is d-block but NOT transition; Be is group 2 but NOT alkaline earth.** Two membership traps he states explicitly.
- **Radius decreases along the period — but in UNEQUAL gaps.** "Pehle do members ke beech ka gap bada hai, agle do mein kam, agle mein aur kam — C–N mein utna gap nahi, N–O mein phir zyada. Ye question aa sakta hai: difference of radius, which is greater?" His meta-rule: "har ek diagram mein socho — kya ban sakta hai isme?"
- **Eu radius spike (199 pm).** "Pehle to mujhe laga shayad NCERT ne misprint kiya... check kiya — misprint bhi nahi tha. To ye apne aap mein potential question hai." (Honest verification on-air; neutral-atom trend ≠ M³⁺ trend.)
- **Gaseous vs hydrated ion size REVERSES.** Li⁺ smallest in gas, largest hydrated ("jitna chhota cation, hydration utni achhi — water molecules zyada gherte hain") → mobility order flips. Asked "sabse chhota kaun" in water: it's Cs⁺.
- **Why oxidation states drop in steps of 2 (13th: +3/+1; 14th: +4/+2).** "Kai baar bachche poochhte hain — do ka drop kyon? Wo do ka gap exactly us s² electrons ki wajah se aata hai."
- **B(OH)₃ looks like a base.** "Lag raha hai jaise Ca(OH)₂ — base ho sakta hai. Base NAHI hai. Ye boric acid hai — H₃BO₃, very mild acid; antiseptic, eye-wash mein use hota hai."
- **Oxidation state ≠ actual charge.** "CO₂ mein carbon +4 hai — kya carbon ke upar +4 charge hai? Usne chaar electron lose kiye hue hain? Nahi." A positive *centre* built by EN difference, not an absolute charge.
- **Assertion-reason spotting habit.** "Kisi statement pe lag raha hai — pehli line mein bola 'aisa hota hai', agli line mein 'aisa KYON hota hai' — wo apne aap mein assertion-reason ka perfect material hai." (He models examiner-anticipation while reading NCERT lines, e.g. actinoid chemistry more complicated due to more oxidation states.)
- **He drops bad questions honestly.** On a 3d-series vs group-2 IE assertion: "mujhe ye bada achha question nahi laga... group two vertical ja rahe ho, 3d horizontal — kaise compare karoge? Lets leave it. Chhodo usko, faltu mein dimaag." Honest dissent is in-voice; never force a bad comparison.

### His comparison-ordering grammar (the chapter's core move)

Every trend is taught in the same fixed sequence — reuse it for any PERI order/comparison solution:
1. **Anchor to radius first.** "Agar aapne atomic radius ki variation achhe se samajh li na, to bahut saari properties aap samajh paoge — they are directly or indirectly related with atomic radii."
2. **State the general trend with its one-line cause** (Zeff along the period; new shell down the group).
3. **Then flag the exception AND its exact location** ("kahan ho rahi hai" — Ga not Al; N not O).
4. **Close with the anti-ratta line:** "isko dekho, ratta mat maarna. Samajh gaye to likhne ki zaroorat nahi padegi; nahi samjhe to likh ke kuch hoga nahi."

## D. Per-question exemplars (worked questions inside Crash Course L1/L2) — opening → key move → closing

1. **True statement about long-form periodic table** — Open: "30 second mein solve kariye." Key: kill each option by self-question ("sp, d, f aise karke to nahi bharte na hum"). Close: table predicts physical/chemical property trends.
2. **Period number equals what?** — Key: re-derive from the filling rule ("nth period ns se start hota hai...") → highest principal quantum number IS the period number. No ratta.
3. **(n−2)f⁷ ns² element** — Open: "information-based question hai, pause kar lo." Key: TWO routes offered — memorise half-filled milestones (Gd 4f⁷5d¹) OR count from the previous noble gas (54 + 10 = 64). Close: "dono tareeke — dat is up to you."
4. **X, Y, Z with Z = 33, 53, 83** — Open: "1 minute mein aaram se." Key: nearest-noble-gas walking ("36 pe noble gas; 35 halogen; 34 oxygen family; 33 nitrogen family, fourth period — arsenic"). Close: generalises the technique as "sabse aasan tareeka."
5. **Third period, group 17** — Open: "bahut aasan hai ye." Key: halogen family + third period = Cl, Z = 17. Two lines, done.
6. **Na→Cl radii trend** — Open: "15 second ka question, jaldi se." Key: decreases — but he then opens the NCERT graph to confirm no mid-period rise. Close: "NCERT ke graphs ko thoda dhyan dena hai."
7. **Largest atomic radius (O/Al/Ca/Mg)** — Key: position logic only — Ca is leftmost AND lowest. "Simple answer aa gaya."
8. **Radii order O²⁻, F⁻, O, F** — Key: charge logic ("negative species neutral se bada hi hoga; 2-negative aur bada"), then period logic for neutrals.
