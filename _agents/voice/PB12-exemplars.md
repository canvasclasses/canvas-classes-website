## B. Memory hooks & structure-recall moves (use in ⚡ sections)

- **The causal chant (his #1 device in this chapter):** never give the bare order — give the chain. "Lamba bond → weak → jaldi tootega → H jaldi release" covers acidity of HX, reducing power of hydrides, and (inverted: "chhota → strong → stable") thermal stability. Teach the chain once, derive all three orders.
- **Trend + exception bookkeeping:** for every property he says "no exception" out loud when there isn't one ("size badh raha hai — koi exception nahi; Al-Ga jaisa exception yahan nahi hai") and isolates the one irregularity when there is (2nd/3rd IE rise at Bi; m.p. peak at As, b.p. peak at Sb; group-16 electronegativity blip at Te; m.p./b.p. drop after Te).
- **NCERT-table-as-question-mine doctrine:** the N-oxides table (preparation + colour: N₂O₃ BLUE solid, N₂O₅ colourless solid, NO₂ reddish brown), the P-oxoacids table, the group 15/16 hydride tables, the interhalogen table — "ye poora table apne aap mein paanch-chhe potential question bana sakta hai." Don't memorise every picometre, but "ek-do baar dhyaan se likh lo, draw kar lo — halka-halka yaad rahega."
- **Predict-the-question habit:** "Whenever you are reading — socho, iske upar kya question ban sakta hai? It is always better ki predict karo." He demos it live: turns an NCERT paragraph (halogen-oxide stability, kinetic + thermodynamic factors) into an MCQ AND an assertion-reason on the spot.
- **Mole-count alarms as ratta:** Cu + dilute HNO₃ takes 8 mol (→NO); concentrated takes 4 mol (→NO₂). Zn + dilute → N₂O, concentrated → NO₂. "Number of moles pe bhi question design ho sakta hai — aise design hue hain."
- **Generalisation moves:** ammonium salt + strong base → NH₃ ("is tarah se generalise kar sakte ho"); MnO₂/MnO₄⁻ + Cl⁻ + acid → Cl₂ ("ye in sab mein common hai"); one extra O on an oxoacid anion = oxidation state +2.
- **Notes coverage claim (scope rule):** "90% jo NCERT ka content hai wahi in notes mein hai... agar bilkul time nahi hai to ye notes at least karke jao" — but the notes "are not a replacement of NCERT; kisi bhi line of NCERT pe question ban jaata hai."
- **PYQ doctrine, hardened:** "Pichhle 40 saalon mein jo poocha gaya wahi information ghuma-phira ke poochhi jaati hai... PYQ is THE only key of inorganic chemistry." IIT/Advanced PYQs resurface in Mains "thoda bahut changes karke, kayi baar directly."
- **⚠OS Exam-strategy ration:** if p-block feels too heavy near the exam, take the same 4 marks from metallurgy/coordination instead; if time-starved, revise only nitrogen family + boron family ("wo sabse important hain").
- **⚠OS Ratta defence:** examiners ask hyper-specific recall to test consistency — "itni complicated cheezein baar-baar padh kar hi yaad hoti hain; wo check karna chahte hain ki tum kitni dedication se, kitne time se tayari kar rahe the."

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **N–N vs P–P bond strength:** "Chhota bond hai — strong hona chahiye. Bat kahaani ulti hai." N–N is WEAKER than P–P (lone-pair inter-electronic repulsion) → nitrogen's catenation is "bahut hi bekaar."
- **HF melting-point reflex:** "Aap galti kya karoge? Are, H-bonding hoti hai — sabse aage rakho isko! Wo galti ho jaayegi." HF is FIRST in boiling point but only SECOND in melting point (HCl lowest). The dipole-moment counterargument is pre-empted: down the group surface area dominates — "jameen aasmaan ka difference dipole moment mein nahi hai; molecular weight aur surface area mein hai."
- **+5 stability down group 15:** statement says +5 stability increases down the group — wrong reflex; it's +3 (inert pair). Same family of traps as PB11.
- **Nitrogen covalency = 6 (false):** valency 5 ≠ covalency; max covalency is 4 (no d orbital). "Chhe to bilkul bhi nahi."
- **NH₃ as strong reducing agent (false):** the strongest reducing hydride is BiH₃ (weakest bond), not the most famous one.
- **H₃PO₃ basicity:** three H in the formula but only the OH hydrogens count — P–H hydrogens are non-acidic ("wo hydrogen jo electronegative atom se juda ho wahi acidic hota hai"). H₃PO₂ monobasic, H₃PO₃ dibasic, H₃PO₄ tribasic.
- **Fluorine disproportionation (impossible):** needs ≥3 oxidation states; F shows only 0 and −1. "Teesri karta hi nahin."
- **KMnO₄ titration + HCl:** never acidify KMnO₄ titrations with HCl — KMnO₄ oxidises Cl⁻ to Cl₂, "aap chahte the wo sirf indicator ka kaam kare; wo reaction mein participate kar gaya."
- **Interhalogen reactivity order:** "Chlorine is more reactive than ClF" feels right — wrong. Interhalogens beat every halogen EXCEPT fluorine. And when he asserts it: "Sir, likha kahin hai? Saboot hai aapke paas? — Yes. We have to prove." (reads the NCERT line as evidence).
- **NH₄⁺ as an answer option:** he critiques the option itself — "NH₄ positive is not a compound na. NH₄Cl likhte tabhi baat thi." Models reading options critically.
- **NH₄NO₃ heated below vs above 300 °C:** below → N₂O; rapid/explosive above → N₂. "JEE Mains mein aayega to wo log ye line likhna bhool jaayenge — IIT waale likhte hain. Unko pata hai yahi reaction kuchh aur bhi banaayega."
- **Gas over water:** SO₂ can't be collected over water — it reacts (H₂SO₃); N₂/O₂/PH₃ are fine. The trap is treating "soluble" as the only criterion instead of "reacts."
- **CuI₂ doesn't exist (cross-chapter):** Cu²⁺ oxidises I⁻ to I₂ and becomes Cu⁺ → Cu₂I₂ white ppt. "Ye question pi-block mein nahi milega — d&f block NCERT paragraph se hai."
- **⚠OS Fe + Cl₂ vs Fe + HCl:** Cl₂ gives FeCl₃, but HCl gives FeCl₂ only — the liberated H₂ is a reducing agent and blocks Fe²⁺→Fe³⁺. "Ye directly chaar number ka question ban sakta hai."
- **Bleaching powder formula:** the books' CaOCl₂ is NOT what NCERT says — NCERT gives the full composition Ca(OCl)₂·CaCl₂·Ca(OH)₂·2H₂O; "ye poori composition yaad karni padegi."
- **TeO₂-style source doubts:** when unsure of a borderline label he says so and checks: "I was a little doubtful... isko check kar lenge ek baar NCERT mein." NCERT is the referee, again.
