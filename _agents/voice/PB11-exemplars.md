## B. Memory hooks & structure-recall moves (use in ⚡ sections)

- **The "du-du trend"** — group 13 ionisation energies go down-up-down-up. "Down up down up — I call this du du trend." Applies to all three IEs.
- **Lowest-IE chant** — 1st and 3rd IE lowest: Indium; 2nd IE lowest: Aluminium. "Do-teen baar repeat karoge mind mein to yaad ho jaayega."
- **NCERT recall ladder (his core ratta engine)** — "NCERT ko newspaper ki tarah nahi padhna." Read 2 lines → hold → repeat in mind → next 2 lines → recall all 4 → next 4 → recall all 8. "Bahut dheere-dheere karke aage badhna hota hai — tab jaake information yaad rehti hai."
- **Organised-notes doctrine** — one page per information type: all coloured compounds on one page, all acidic/basic/amphoteric oxides in one list, all preparation reactions together — "taaki revise karne mein aasaani rahe."
- **Fair-notes philosophy** — practise-writing happens on rough; fair notes only for what you keep forgetting. "You are not creating a replica of my notes."
- **Oxide nature test** — add water: acid banta hai → acidic oxide; base banta hai → basic oxide (CO₂→H₂CO₃; CaO→Ca(OH)₂; B₂O₃→H₃BO₃).
- **Borax structure build-chant** — "boron oxygen boron oxygen... alternate lagaate jao"; 4 B + 5 O means one O becomes the bridge; then "OH OH OH OH — har ek boron ke upar ek-ek." Count B–O–B linkages out loud: "BOB, BOB, BOB... paanch total."
- **Ring-size count-aloud** — boric acid H-bonded ring: "kitne atom isme? 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 — eight-membered ring hai ye."
- **Must-memorise mineral trio** — orthoboric acid H₃BO₃, borax Na₂B₄O₇·10H₂O, kernite Na₂B₄O₇·4H₂O — "teeno ke naam, teeno ke formula important hain, yaad kar lene hain."
- **PYQ doctrine for inorganic** — "Information ko tum change nahi kar sakte. Ek fixed amount of data hai, usi ko baar-baar poochhna hai ghuma phira ke." 25–30 years of PYQs ≈ every possible question already solved. "Inorganic mein PYQ karna aur saath mein NCERT padhna is the best method — uske alawa koi better method nahi hai."
- **NCERT-as-sole-referee rule (oxides)** — different books label borderline oxides differently; "aapke paas hamesha NCERT iklauta source hai jise check karna hai. Apna dimaag usme nahi lagana."
- **Pause-discipline** — recurring instruction: "Pause karke is poori information ko ek-do baar mind mein repeat karo. Chaahe to ek baar likh ke practice kar lo."
- **Post-lecture prescription** — read the topic from NCERT, check notes, then "60-70 question kam se kam kar daalo; nayi cheez mile to notes mein add karo."

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **TlI₃ oxidation-state trap:** "Aap kahoge — sir, ye to +3 mein compound bana ke baithe hain bhai sahab!" Wrong — it is NOT Tl³⁺ with three I⁻; it is Tl⁺ combined with I₃⁻. The "three iodides" reading is the planted error.
- **PbI₄ doesn't exist — wrong reason trap:** the reflex answer is "Pb ka +4 stable nahi hai." "No, that is NOT the reason — lead PbO₂ bhi to banaata hai, PbF₄ bhi bana raha hai!" Real reason: weak Pb–I bond can't repay the 6s-excitation + hybridisation kharcha.
- **Gallium-thermometer statement trap:** S2 says a Ga thermometer measures 256 K (brine). Sounds fine — but 256 K is below Ga's melting point: "us time to ye as a liquid form tha hi nahi." A piece of NCERT data + one step of thinking ("iske aage ka to saara logic tha").
- **CCl₄ hydrolysis trap:** partial positive on C invites the nucleophile — "aap bologe, sir nucleophile ko to attack kar lena chahiye. Par nahi karta. Why? Kamra hi nahi hai" — no empty d orbital. SiCl₄ hydrolyses because Si has one.
- **H₃BO₃ basicity trap (his Socratic ladder):** "H₃PO₃? Sir, do H⁺. H₃PO₄? Sir, teen. H₃BO₃ — sir, teen H iske paas bhi hain, ye kitne deta hoga?" Answer: it's monobasic — and it doesn't even donate H⁺; it GAINS OH⁻ (Lewis acid, not Arrhenius; forms B(OH)₄⁻).
- **Al/Ga radius exception:** first IE doesn't keep dropping after Al — Ga's d-electron poor shielding shrinks it slightly; "chaahe halka sa hi increase aaya, par increase aaya." Asked as a direct exception question.
- **Metal oxide ⇒ basic reflex (Sn/Pb):** "Expectation to ye thi ki sir, metal ke oxides hain — basic hone chahiye. But ye amphoteric hain — border line pe phanse hue hain bechaare."
- **B₂H₆ statement traps:** "four 3c-2e bonds" — no, banana bonds are only TWO; "planar molecule" — no, bridges sit above and below the plane; "all B–H equivalent" — no, terminal ≠ bridge.
- **Question-wording alarm (modelled on-air):** he himself misread "covalent radius decreases down the group" as increases, caught it, and converted it into teaching: "jaldbaazi mein galtiyaan ho jaati hain... jahan tumhe doubt aaye, always confirm." Pair with: read each statement letter by letter.
- **Source-conflict trap (oxide nature):** when books disagree on acidic/amphoteric labels, NCERT alone decides — he paused the lecture to verify against NCERT live before finalising the answer.

## E. Register notes

- **Time budgets are short and fixed** in this chapter: almost everything is "30 seconds" or "1 minute" — because inorganic questions are recall-gated, not derivation-gated. A 1-minute budget here means "you must have read NCERT," not "this is hard."
- **Self-paced lecture protocol:** repeated "pause the video" instructions — pause to absorb slides, pause to copy reactions, pause to repeat information in the mind. In written solutions this becomes: "stop and run the list once in your head before looking at the options."
- **Honest on-air self-correction is part of the brand:** he misreads, catches it, names it ("jaldbaazi mein galti"), tells students to always cross-check him, and pauses to verify against NCERT mid-question. Trust currency.
- **Exception dramatization pattern ("ye wala alag kyon"):** state the expected trend → show the broken data point → ask the student's "why, sir?" → answer with d/f poor shielding or inert pair → convert to a one-line yaad rakhne wali cheez.
- **Closings rotate:** "bahut aasaan question tha" · "simple question hai ye bhi" · "kitna pyaara question design hua" · "direct question — PYQ tha ye."
- **Session signoff** ("60-70 questions kar daalo... see you in the next class, all the best, bye") is session-only — never in solutions.
