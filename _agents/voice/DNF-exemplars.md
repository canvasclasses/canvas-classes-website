## B. Must-memorize items he teaches (use in shortcut / "yaad rakhna" lines)

- **3D series order** must be cold (Sc→Zn); also Zn/Cd/Hg atomic numbers via the +18/+32 trick.
- **Unexpected (anomalous) configurations** to memorize on ONE summary page: Cr (3d⁵4s¹),
  Cu (3d¹⁰4s¹); in 4d/5d the frequently-asked ones — **Mo, Pd, Ag, Pt, Au** (plus Nb).
- **Lanthanide contraction** = poor shielding by 4f electrons → 4d and 5d sizes "almost
  equal"; Zr/Hf the standard example pair. f shields even more poorly than d ("f electron
  d se bhi zyada naalaayak hai").
- **MP peaks at d⁵** in each row; anomalies = **Mn and Tc** (sudden dip).
- **Most stable oxidation state:** Sc→Cr → **+3**; Mn→Ni → **+2**; (Cr commonly +3 or +6,
  most stable +3).
- **Only-one-oxidation-state elements in 3D:** **Sc (+3) and Zn (+2)**.
- **Highest MP:** W, Mo, Cr (per series). **Lowest MP:** Ag, Cu, Au.
- **Density:** max = Ni, Cu; min = Sc (in 3D).
- **Atomization enthalpy 3D:** max = V; min = Zn.
- **IE highs:** 1st & 3rd IE max = Zn; 2nd IE max = Cu (removing 2nd e⁻ from Cu disturbs
  the d¹⁰).
- **Oxygen stabilizes high oxidation states better than fluorine** — highest Mn–F is MnF₄,
  but Mn goes to +7 with oxygen (MnO₄⁻).
- **Higher oxidation states more stable DOWN the group** in d-block (opposite of p-block's
  inert-pair effect): Mo⁶⁺, W⁶⁺ more stable than Cr⁶⁺ ⇒ CrO₃ oxidizing but MoO₃/WO₃ not.
- **Spin-only magnetic moment** μ = √(n(n+2)) BM — "bas value put karni hai."
- Alloys form when metallic radii are within **~15% of each other**.
- Brass = Cu+Zn; Bronze = Cu+Sn; German silver = Cu+Zn+Ni.

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for "watch out" lines)

- **d-block ≠ transition element:** reflex "saare d-block transition hain." Break it: Zn/Cd/Hg
  are d-block but NOT transition (d¹⁰ in ground state AND in every oxidation state). Student
  voice he answers: *"sir Cu bhi to s¹d¹⁰ hai?"* — yes in ground state, BUT Cu²⁺ is d⁹
  (partially filled) so Cu IS a transition element. The test is **"kisi bhi oxidation state
  mein unpaired d electron hai kya?"**
- **Sc³⁺ / Zn²⁺ colourless:** d⁰ and d¹⁰ → no d-d transition possible ("upar jagah hi
  nahi / niche electron hi nahi"). Reflex "transition metal ion to coloured hota hai" — not
  if d is empty or full.
- **Higher-OS-down-the-group is OPPOSITE of inert pair effect:** students carry the p-block
  reflex (lower OS more stable down the group). In d-block it flips.
- **SRP irregular trend:** don't try to explain every dip — *"itni close values ka exact
  explanation possible nahi hota"* (e.g. V/Cr/Mn 1st IE 656/650/653). Only the Mn/Zn anomaly
  has a clean reason (half/fully-filled stability).
- **Mn anomaly is half-filled stability, NOT a random dip:** Mn²⁺ is d⁵, so it "doesn't want
  to get reduced" → E° more negative than expected. Cr²⁺ is d⁴, less happy.
- **Cu positive SRP (the outlier):** don't give Cu "special VIP treatment" without the
  reason — high I₁+I₂ not compensated by ΔH(hydration).
- **Cu⁺ disproportionates in water:** 2Cu⁺ → Cu²⁺ + Cu; reason is the much more negative
  ΔH(hydration) of Cu²⁺, which more than compensates the 2nd IE. (Already a past PYQ — "ek
  baar aa chuka, dobara bhi aa sakta hai.")
- **Strongest oxidizing agent = use SRP, not gut:** for M³⁺→M²⁺, highest positive E° wins.
  Co³⁺ (E° +1.97) is strongest; if Co³⁺ isn't in the options, Mn³⁺ (+1.57) next.
- **Half-fill / full-fill jump prediction is NOT guaranteed:** the s→d jump happens only
  *"jab usko fayda ho raha ho"* (exchange-energy / overall stability gain) — you cannot
  promise s fills fully before d starts. That's why the general config is written ns¹⁻².
- **Incomplete subshell ignored in counting** (carry-over from ATOM dabba logic).

## D. Per-question / worked-statement exemplars — opening → key move → closing

### L1 (Physical Properties) — mostly NCERT statement-mining + direct PYQs

1. **d-block vs transition element definition** — Open: "saare d-block ko transition nahi
   bolte — jaise Zn d-block hai par transition nahi." Key: the "partially filled d in ground
   state OR in some oxidation state" test; answers the Cu doubt (Cu²⁺ = d⁹). Close:
   "All transition are d-block but reverse is not true."
2. **General electronic configuration (why ns¹⁻²?)** — Key: S must fill before d starts
   (4s² then 3d¹...), BUT Cr (s¹d⁵) / Cu (s¹d¹⁰) jump for exactly-half/full stability — so
   you "can't guarantee" → hence the 1-to-2 range in the formula.
3. **Crystal-structure table — find the trend** — Open: "pause the video, dhoondo koi trend
   dikhta hai?" Key: first two groups HCP, next two BCC, then the Co/Ni/Cu groups CCP; Mn/Zn/Cd/Hg
   marked X (no simple HCP/BCC). "Jahan bhi table ho, find out koi trend dikhta hai ki nahi."
4. **Melting point graph (3D/4D/5D)** — Key: expected "pahaadi jaisa" curve peaking mid-series
   at d⁵; sudden dips at **Mn and Tc** are the anomalies → "ye poochha jaayega." Highs W/Mo/Cr,
   lows Ag/Cu/Au. Reason: more valence (ns + (n-1)d) electrons → stronger metallic bonding;
   unpaired d electrons useful, pairing after d⁵ drops it.
5. **Assertion–Reason: M–M bonding in heavy transition metals** — Key: 4d/5d have greater
   ΔH(atomization) ⇒ stronger metallic bond ⇒ "frequent metal–metal bonding in compounds of
   heavy transition metals." Close: "Assertion true, Reason true, Reason is the correct
   explanation."
6. **Lanthanide contraction (4d≈5d sizes)** — Key: new shell should increase size, but 5d
   elements have 4f electrons whose poor shielding makes Z_eff stronger → size "utna hi reh
   gaya." "f electron d se bhi zyada naalaayak hai — usse bhi zyada poor shielding."
7. **Atomization enthalpy 3D — min/max PYQ** — Key: irregular (dips), so they won't ask the
   trend, only min (Zn) / max (V). "Ye question ban gaya."
8. **Ionization-enthalpy irregularity** — Open: NCERT says it is "of little chemical
   significance — zyada tension nahi leni." Key: removing one e⁻ alters the 4s/3d gap, the
   s electron jumps to d (reorganization energy + exchange-energy gain) → M⁺ has dⁿ with no
   4s. So predicting *when* it jumps is hard. 1st & 3rd IE max = Zn; 2nd IE max = Cu.
9. **Density-order PYQ (atomic no. 24, etc.)** — Key: just know lowest (Sc) and highest
   (Ni, Cu); they only give the V-onward members so Zn lands last, Cu first. "Direct
   question, recent PYQ."
10. **Common oxidation states of element atomic-no. 24** — Open: "30 seconds, jaldi karo."
    Key: 24 = Cr → +2 to +6. "Direct question."
11. **Variable OS differ by unity (vs p-block by 2)** — Key: p-block gaps are 2 (B/Al 3&1,
    halogens 1-3-5-7); d-block's incompletely-filled d lets you pull one e⁻ at a time, so OS
    differ "by unity." This single property explains colour, catalysis, complex formation.
12. **Higher OS more stable down the group (Cr/Mo/W +6)** — Key: opposite of p-block inert
    pair. CrO₃ is oxidizing ("Cr says I don't want to stay at +6, reduce me"); MoO₃/WO₃ are
    not ("hum khush hain, hume nahi jaana"). MnO₄⁻ +7 with O but only MnF₄ with F → "oxygen
    stabilizes high OS better than fluorine."
13. **SRP = sublimation + ionization + hydration (3-step framing)** — Key: M(s)→M(g)→Mⁿ⁺(g)→
    Mⁿ⁺(aq); first two cost energy, hydration releases it; whether overall exo/endo decides
    SRP. The Li-vs-Cs "exam topper" analogy explains why IE alone doesn't decide.
14. **M²⁺→M SRP trend with Mn/Cu/Zn exceptions** — Key: the "khush/dukhi" personification +
    "head poking above the zero line" graph picture. Mn²⁺ (d⁵) and Zn²⁺ (d¹⁰) more negative
    than expected = "I am happy, don't reduce me"; Cu positive = "reduce me first." Negative-SRP
    metals displace H₂ from acid; Cu doesn't.
15. **Why Cu has positive E° (assertion–reason)** — Key: I₁+I₂ "not balanced by ΔH(hydration)"
    (the ₹100 ka hisaab line) → Cu stays as metal, can't liberate H₂ from acid.
16. **Cu⁺ disproportionation** — Key: Cu²⁺(aq) more stable than Cu⁺(aq) due to "much more
    negative ΔH(hydration)" which "more than compensates the 2nd ionization enthalpy." The
    profitable-deal analogy. "Question already asked — doesn't mean it can't come again."
17. **M³⁺→M²⁺ trend (which is more stable / strongest oxidizing agent)** — Key: positive E°
    ⇒ M²⁺ more stable. Ti/V/Cr happy at +3; Mn wants +2 (d⁵ over d⁴); Co³⁺ highest E° (+1.97)
    ⇒ strongest oxidizing agent. Sc³⁺ stable (noble-gas config); Mn²⁺ stable (d⁵).
18. **Frost diagram (not in NCERT, "pyaara diagram")** — Open: "NCERT mein nahi hai, par padha
    raha hoon kyunki bahut cheezein clear karta hai." Key: ΔG vs oxidation number; lower =
    more stable. Bigger the drop (e.g. Mn⁷⁺→Mn²⁺ vs Cr⁶⁺→Cr³⁺), better oxidizing agent ⇒
    KMnO₄ > K₂Cr₂O₇. Close: "pyaara diagram hai na?"
19. **Colourless-pair / coloured+paramagnetic-set PYQs** — Key: d⁰ (Sc³⁺, Mn⁷⁺) and d¹⁰
    (Zn²⁺) eliminate; partially-filled d ⇒ coloured AND paramagnetic (Cu²⁺ d⁹, Cr³⁺, Sc¹⁺ d¹).
20. **Spin-only μ of strongest oxidizing agent (the "pyaara question")** — Open: "do steps
    hai — pehle figure out strongest oxidizing agent, phir uski config se μ." Key: Co³⁺ (E°
    +1.97) → 3d⁶ → 4 unpaired → μ = √(4·6) ≈ 4.9 ≈ 5 BM (nearest integer). "Aasan tha."
21. **Complex-compound / catalytic-activity assertion–reasons** — Key: many complexes due to
    small ion size + high charge + available d orbitals; catalysis due to multiple OS + complex
    formation (NOT adsorption strength — common wrong "reason" in A-R). Fe³⁺ catalyzes
    iodide–persulphate (Fe³⁺ oxidizes I⁻, Fe²⁺ reduces persulphate).
22. **Interstitial compounds** — Key: high MP (> pure metal), very hard (some borides approach
    diamond), retain metallic conductivity, chemically inert. "Very good combination."
23. **Alloys (NCERT direct)** — Key: metallic radii within ~15%; identify components (brass
    Cu+Zn, bronze Cu+Sn, German silver Cu+Zn+Ni). Incorrect-statement PYQ: "brass = Cu+Ni"
    is wrong (it's Cu+Zn).
24. **Amphoteric metal giving H₂ with both acid & base** — Key: Zn (negative SRP + amphoteric);
    Mg strongly basic, Fe doesn't release H₂ with base, Hg "bilkul nahi."

### NCERT-reading doctrine (the chapter's defining sermon — L1 close)
- "NCERT ki har ek line mein ek question chhupa hua hai." Read each line asking *"is pe
  question ban sakta hai kya?"* — that alertness makes it stick. "Jab main padhta hoon aur
  lagta hai is pe question ban sakta hai, mind automatically usko zyada focus karta hai."
- "Sirf utna hi chahiye hota hai" — you don't need to memorize everything, just read with
  question-hunting attention. This is the explicit method for the whole trends-and-exceptions
  chapter.
- He deliberately goes SLOW in L1: "majority of students struggle in d & f block... I don't
  assume you know anything, I don't even assume you passed 12th-level d&f properly."

## E. Register notes

- **Dominant device:** personifying oxidation-state stability as a person who is *khush /
  dukhi / khush nahi hoon, main nahi jaunga*. Use this freely in SRP / stability solutions.
- **PYQ-spotting voice:** "ye question ban gaya," "ye poochha jaayega," "direct question,"

## B. (L2) Must-memorize items

- **Oxide acid/base trend (Cr):** CrO basic → Cr₂O₃ amphoteric → CrO₃ acidic. (V: V₂O₃ basic →
  V₂O₄ less basic → V₂O₅ amphoteric, slightly more acidic.) "Higher oxidation number ⇒ ionic
  character of oxide decreases" (Fajan's-rule logic).
- **Mn₂O₇ is a covalent green oil** (memory point); CrO₃ & V₂O₅ have low MP; higher oxides are
  predominantly acidic. Mn₂O₇→HMnO₄, CrO₃→H₂CrO₄ (and H₂Cr₂O₇).
- **O stabilizes high OS better than F** (carries over): highest Mn–F = MnF₄, but MnO₄⁻ = +7.
- **K₂Cr₂O₇ prep chain (say as a story):** FeCr₂O₄ (chromite) + Na₂CO₃ + O₂(oxidant) → Na₂CrO₄
  (yellow) → [acidify H₂SO₄] → Na₂Cr₂O₇ (orange) → [KCl] → K₂Cr₂O₇ (less soluble ⇒ crystallizes).
- **Chromate ↔ dichromate** interconvert by changing pH (acidic → dichromate; alkaline →
  chromate).
- **K₂Cr₂O₇ preferred over Na₂Cr₂O₇ as primary standard** — Na salt is hygroscopic.
- **Thermal decomposition gives O₂:** K₂Cr₂O₇ → K₂CrO₄ + Cr₂O₃ + 3/2 O₂; KMnO₄ → K₂MnO₄ + MnO₂
  + O₂ (NOT disproportionation — Mn goes highest→lower only, just reduced).
- **CrO₅** (deep blue, from acidified K₂Cr₂O₇ + H₂O₂; lab manual): butterfly structure; 4 O in
  peroxide (−1), 1 O as oxide (−2) ⇒ Cr is +6 (not +10).
- **Dichromate structure:** both Cr sp³/tetrahedral, O bridge; bridge Cr–O bond 179 pm (longer)
  > terminal Cr–O 163 pm (shorter); bridge angle ~126°.
- **Chromyl chloride test:** K₂Cr₂O₇ + conc. H₂SO₄ + soluble chloride → orange-red CrO₂Cl₂
  vapours (confirms Cl⁻).
- **KMnO₄ prep:** MnO₂ + KOH + O₂(or KNO₃) → K₂MnO₄ (manganate MnO₄²⁻, green, Mn⁺⁶, d¹,
  paramagnetic) → electrolytic oxidation (alkaline) → KMnO₄ (permanganate MnO₄⁻, purple, Mn⁺⁷,
  d⁰, diamagnetic). Lab: Mn²⁺ oxidized by S₂O₈²⁻ → permanganate.
- **n-factor of MnO₄⁻:** acidic → Mn²⁺ (n=5); neutral/weakly basic → MnO₂ (n=3); strongly basic →
  MnO₄²⁻ (n=1). Key acid-vs-basic product difference: I⁻ → I₂ (acidic) but I⁻ → IO₃⁻ (basic).
- **Colour of K₂Cr₂O₇ (orange) & KMnO₄ (purple) is LMCT** (ligand-to-metal charge transfer),
  NOT d-d (both metals are d⁰).
- **KMnO₄ never used with HCl** (oxidizes Cl⁻ to Cl₂). Alkaline KMnO₄ = Baeyer's reagent.
- **Lanthanides:** 14 elements **between La and Hf** (Ce 58 → Lu 71); **La is NOT a lanthanide**
  (studied with them due to similar properties). General config: 4f¹⁻¹⁴ 5d⁰⁻¹ 6s².
- **Most common OS of lanthanides = +3** ("wapas 3+ mein hi jaana hai"). Other OS stable only at
  f⁰/f⁷/f¹⁴.
- **Ce⁴⁺** (f⁰, noble-gas) strong oxidant, E°(Ce⁴⁺/Ce³⁺) = +1.74 V; **Pr, Nd, Tb, Dy** show +4
  **only in oxides**. **Eu²⁺** (f⁷) strong reducing agent.
- **Eu has the largest size (199 pm)** — the size-trend outlier; ionic radii decrease steadily
  Ce→Lu (lanthanide contraction; Zr≈Hf the standard consequence example).
- **Colourless lanthanide M³⁺ ions (memorize 5):** La³⁺ (f⁰), Lu³⁺ (f¹⁴), Gd³⁺ (f⁷), plus Yb³⁺
  and Nd³⁺ — note NCERT only says La³⁺/Lu³⁺ colourless ("rest do so"), but research adds Gd³⁺/
  Yb³⁺/Nd³⁺. Colour comes from f-f transitions (impossible at f⁰/f¹⁴).
- **Nd has max paramagnetism** in the series.
- **Earlier lanthanides ~ Ca; later ~ Al** (a deliberately "weak/vague statement — bas rat
  lena, dimaag mat kharchna").
- **Mish metal** ≈ 95% lanthanide + ~5% Fe + traces of S/C/Ca/Al (lanthanoid + transition).
- **Actinides:** config 7s² + variable 5f/6d; 14 e⁻ formally added to 5f **but NOT in Th**;
  irregularities tied to f⁰/f⁷/f¹⁴ stability. **Np and Pu show the maximum number of oxidation
  states** (3–7). 5f orbitals are **less buried/more diffuse than 4f** ⇒ participate in bonding
  to a far greater extent. Actinides HNO₃-passivated (protective oxide layer, like Al).

## C. (L2) Additional traps

- **"KMnO₄ disproportionates" reflex:** Mn⁷⁺→Mn⁶⁺+Mn⁴⁺ on heating is NOT disproportionation
  (it starts from the highest OS and only goes down). But **K₂MnO₄ DOES disproportionate** in
  neutral/acidic medium: 3MnO₄²⁻ + 4H⁺ → 2MnO₄⁻ + MnO₂ + 2H₂O. Don't swap the two.
- **CrO₅ Cr = +10 reflex:** naive formula gives +10 (impossible, Cr max is +6). Fix: 4 peroxide
  O's → Cr is actually +6.
- **Chromate "square planar" reflex:** it's tetrahedral (sp³).
- **La counted as a lanthanide:** the classic mistake — "Lanthanum se shuru hota hoga, isiliye
  naam Lanthanoid rakh diya" — NO, it starts at Ce.
- **f⁷ / f¹⁴ "stays in that OS" reflex:** Eu²⁺ (f⁷) and Tb²⁺ (f¹⁴) are stable configs but the
  ion STILL goes to +3 (Eu²⁺→Eu³⁺ etc.); Ce⁴⁺ (f⁰ noble gas) still reverts to Ce³⁺. "Bana lega
  thodi der ke liye tumhari khushi ke liye, par wapas 3+ mein jaayega."
- **NCERT-answer conflict (the meta-trap he teaches):** some PYQs conflict with NCERT (e.g. the
  "coloured lanthanide ion" question where NCERT says only La³⁺/Lu³⁺ are colourless, so Gd³⁺
  "should" be coloured, but research shows Gd³⁺ is colourless via a large band gap absorbing
  outside visible). He flags these "with proof" so the student doesn't loop on the internet:
  "jo NTA ne answer diya, usko haan karni hai bas."

## D. (L2) Per-question / worked-statement exemplars

25. **Nature of V₂O₃ and CrO (X/Y type)** — Key: direct recall, both basic. "Direct question, PYQ."
26. **Sum of spin-only μ of basic & amphoteric oxides among CrO/Cr₂O₃/CrO₃** — Key: CrO basic
    (Cr²⁺), Cr₂O₃ amphoteric (Cr³⁺), CrO₃ acidic (excluded). Count unpaired e⁻ in each, μ=√(n(n+2)),
    add the two. "≈ 8.77 — check once."
27. **K₂Cr₂O₇ preparation reactions** — Key: the story chain + by-products (Fe₂O₃, CO₂); oxidant
    is **O₂/air** (Cr³⁺→Cr⁶⁺). Possible Qs: which medium converts chromate→dichromate (acidic);
    solubility (Na salt more soluble, K salt crystallizes).
28. **Why K₂Cr₂O₇ preferred as primary standard** — Key: Na₂Cr₂O₇ hygroscopic (the chai analogy).
29. **K₂Cr₂O₇ as oxidizing agent** — Key: Cr⁶⁺ unstable (wants Cr³⁺, Frost-diagram drop); oxidizes
    I⁻→I₂, Sn²⁺→Sn⁴⁺, H₂S→S, Fe²⁺→Fe³⁺, HX→X₂ (Cl/Br/I only, not F).
30. **CrO₅ structure / Cr oxidation state** — Key: +10 impossible; 4 peroxide O ⇒ Cr +6; butterfly.
31. **Dichromate bond-length (L1 vs L2)** — Key: bridge Cr–O (179) > terminal Cr–O (163). "Potential
    question — pooch sakta hai L1>L2 ya L2>L1."
32. **KMnO₄ preparation (lab vs commercial)** — Key: both write & practice; manganate (green, Mn⁺⁶,
    d¹ paramagnetic) → permanganate (purple, Mn⁺⁷, d⁰ diamagnetic).
33. **Why KMnO₄/K₂Cr₂O₇ are coloured** — Key: **LMCT** (both d⁰, so no d-d). PYQ: "ds-d transition?"
    → CuSO₄·5H₂O (Cu²⁺ d⁹), not the d⁰ ones.
34. **Assertion–Reason: Cr²⁺ reducing, Mn³⁺ oxidizing (both d⁴)** — Key: NCERT solved example 8.4;
    Cr²⁺(d⁴)→Cr³⁺(d³, half-filled t₂g); Mn³⁺(d⁴)→Mn²⁺(d⁵ half-filled). "Reason is correct
    explanation. Khud ke dimaag se nahi soch sakte the — NCERT solved examples bhi important hain."
35. **Incorrect statement about interstitial compounds** — Key: "chemically reactive" is wrong
    (they are inert); high MP, very hard, metallic conductivity all correct. (revision from L1)
36. **AgNO₃ precipitate colours** — "Nothing to explain — memory aid." AgCl/AgBr/AgI must be cold;
    Ag₂S black (frequent in salt analysis); the rest as a reference list to save effort.
37. **"Last electron in 5d or 4f?" (lanthanide/actinide config)** — Key: the examiner self-contradiction
    rant; energy levels too close; "pareshaan mat hona, spoiler alert de diya."
38. **Which lanthanide ion shows f⁷ / which config** — Key: memorize the few — Eu²⁺ (f⁷), Tb²⁺(f¹⁴),
    Gd³⁺(f⁷), Lu³⁺(f¹⁴); "preferably they ask exactly-half/full-filled, noble-gas, or ones with
    2-3 lines written about them in theory."
39. **Ce⁴⁺/Eu²⁺ oxidizing/reducing behaviour (A-R PYQ)** — Key: Ce⁴⁺ noble-gas but reverts to Ce³⁺
    (E°+1.74 ⇒ oxidant, can oxidize water but slow ⇒ good analytical reagent); Eu²⁺ f⁷ but reverts
    to Eu³⁺ ⇒ reducing agent. "Both statements true."
40. **Correct order of atomic radii (lanthanides)** — Key: Eu is largest (199 pm) — knowing just
    that eliminates most options; note they slip a p-block element (N) into the list, "tables ko
    aaram se study karo."
41. **Number of coloured lanthanide ions** — Key: La³⁺ (f⁰) and Lu³⁺ (f¹⁴) colourless; per NCERT
    "rest do so" → answer 3. (But see the Gd³⁺ conflict note in C.)
42. **Config of bivalent Eu & trivalent Ce** — Key: Ce³⁺ = f¹ (Ce⁴⁺ noble gas); Eu²⁺ = f⁷.
    "Theory mein baar-baar mention kiya hai, yaad rehna chahiye."
43. **Max oxidation states among actinides** — Key: Np and Pu (3–7).
44. **A-R: 5f participates in bonding far more than 4f** — Key: 5f less buried/more diffuse than 4f
    (similar angular wave-function part). "Reason correct."
45. **Lab-manual Qs (NOT in textbook):** e.g. "why add dil. H₂SO₄ when preparing ferrous-ammonium
    sulphate / potash alum?" → to **prevent hydrolysis** of Fe²⁺/Al³⁺ salts (Expt 9.1). And:
    "avoid prolonged heating — may oxidize Fe²⁺→Fe³⁺ and change crystal stoichiometry" (a
    precaution that became a PYQ). "Precautions tak padhni padti hai."

# L3 ADDITIONS (Problem-Practice Session — PYQs)

> L3 is the **DPP-style solve session**: same fixed arc as ATOM DPPs — "pause, 30–40 sec, kya
> yaad aa raha hai?" Most answers are the L1/L2 statements turned into questions. New value here
> is the **calculated-vs-observed magnetic-moment trap**, the **"you didn't search NCERT
> (hypothetical scenario)" joke**, and a clean set of worked PYQs with his "khush/SRP" reasoning.

## C. (L3) Additional traps

- **Calculated vs OBSERVED magnetic moment:** when a question says "observed," check NCERT
  Table 8.7. μ(observed) for **Co²⁺ is 4.4–5.2** (vs calculated 3.87), NOT V²⁺ (V²⁺ is ~3.87 both
  observed and calculated). The trap plants the Co²⁺ observed range on V²⁺. "Table tak padhna
  padega."
- **"d-subshell full ⇒ high atomization enthalpy" reflex (Zn/Cd/Hg):** WRONG — ΔH(atomization)
  depends on unpaired valence electrons; Zn/Cd/Hg have the LEAST in their period (which is why
  they're soft). 
- **"Compounds of Zn/Cd/Hg are paramagnetic" reflex:** generalized over all compounds it's FALSE
  — Zn²⁺ is d¹⁰ diamagnetic; most relevant anions are diamagnetic too, so it's the cation that
  decides.
- **Sc shows +4 reflex:** NO — Sc only goes to +3 (then 4s⁰3d⁰ noble gas, won't lose another).
- **Lu last-electron-in-4f reflex:** the headline L2/L3 trap — Lu (Z=71) looks like "last 4f
  electron," but Yb (Z=70) is already [Xe]4f¹⁴6s², so Lu's added electron goes into **5d¹**.
  Answer = 5d (per the configs in the book), despite the genuine 4f/5d energy-closeness caveat.

## D. (L3) Worked PYQs — opening → key move → closing

46. **Correct statements about Zn/Cd/Hg** — Key: (a) high ΔH-atomization FALSE (d full ≠ many
    unpaired e⁻; theirs is least → soft); (b) Zn/Cd no variable OS, Hg shows +1 & +2 TRUE;
    (c) compounds paramagnetic FALSE (Zn²⁺ d¹⁰ diamagnetic); (d) soft metals TRUE. → answer (a)
    is the wrong one / (b)(d) correct.
47. **M³⁺→M²⁺ E°: Fe vs Mn** — Key: Fe +0.77 < Mn +1.57 (Mn²⁺ is d⁵, strong tendency to revert);
    so "Fe > Mn" is FALSE. "Exact value yaad nahi karni — bas pattern."
48. **Cr²⁺ liberates H₂ from dilute acid** — Key: M²⁺→M E° negative for all 3D except Cu; all
    negative-E° metals liberate H₂. TRUE. "Itna toh yaad rakh hi sakte hain."
49. **Co³⁺ can't liberate H₂ (positive E°) + its μ** — Key: positive E° ⇒ no H₂; Co³⁺ = 3d⁶ →
    4 unpaired → μ = √(4·6) ≈ 4.9 BM.
50. **H₂ + CuO →** — Key: H₂ is reducing, Cu wants reduction (E°(Cu²⁺/Cu)=+0.34) → gives **Cu**.
51. **Chromite + Na₂CO₃ fuse → A & B; sum of μ** — Key: products Na₂CrO₄ (Cr⁶⁺ d⁰, diamagnetic;
    Na⁺ also diamagnetic) + Fe₂O₃ (Fe³⁺ d⁵, 5 unpaired). So μ comes only from Fe³⁺. "Aasan question."
52. **Lab-manual: chromyl chloride + NaOH → A; then A + acid + H₂O₂ → B (blue)** — Key: A =
    Na₂CrO₄; B = **CrO₅** (dissolves in amyl alcohol → blue). "JEE Main mein yeh blue-colour
    question aa chuka hai. The 'you didn't search NCERT — let's assume a hypothetical scenario
    where you did' joke."
53. **Why avoid prolonged heating in Mohr-salt prep** — Key: to **prevent oxidation** of Fe²⁺→Fe³⁺
    (would change crystal stoichiometry). "Bola tha na pichle lecture mein."
54. **KMnO₄ + oxalic acid (acidic): ΔON of carbon** — Key: oxalate C is +3 → CO₂ C is +4 →
    change = **1**.
55. **MnO₄⁻ + thiosulphate (neutral/faintly alkaline)** — Key: S₂O₃²⁻→SO₄²⁻; Mn⁷⁺→MnO₂ (Mn⁴⁺) →
    3-electron change. "Direct from theory."
56. **Good oxidizing agents among lanthanides** — Key: **Ce⁴⁺** (f⁰ noble gas but reverts to
    Ce³⁺, E°+1.74) AND **Tb⁴⁺** (half-filled f⁷ but still reverts to +3) → both. "Dekho question
    ka fayda — yeh do information ab zyada yaad rahegi than reading theory. Isliye zyada PYQ karo."
57. **Statement about Ce⁴⁺ (noble gas config)** — Key: prefers to gain electron, acts as
    oxidizing agent (→ Ce³⁺).
58. **First-row metal with highest ΔH-atomization → its amphoteric oxide's μ** — Key: highest =
    **V** (Cr dipped); V oxides V₂O₃(basic)/V₂O₄(less basic)/V₂O₅(amphoteric); in V₂O₅ V is +5 →
    3d⁰ → μ = **0**.
59. **Set of correct OS statements** — Key: Mn +7 in Mn₂O₇ (green covalent oil) TRUE; Ru/Os +8
    in oxides TRUE; Sc +4 FALSE (only +3); Cr +6 oxidizing TRUE.
60. **K₂Cr₂O₇ primary standard vs solubility** — Key: statement-1 (preferred as primary standard)
    TRUE; statement-2 (K₂Cr₂O₇ higher solubility) FALSE (Na salt more soluble & hygroscopic).
61. **Incorrect statement (W⁶⁺ vs Cr⁶⁺ / KMnO₄ with HCl / lanthanide oxide phosphors)** — Key:
    W⁶⁺ more stable than Cr⁶⁺ TRUE (down-group); KMnO₄ titration with HCl FALSE (oxidizes Cl⁻);
    some lanthanide oxides as phosphors in TV screens TRUE. → incorrect = the HCl one.
62. **Thermal decomposition of Mn compound at 513 K** — Key: it's **KMnO₄** (513 K value in book)
    → K₂MnO₄ + MnO₂ + O₂ (gaseous = O₂, Y = MnO₂); then MnO₂ + NaCl + conc. H₂SO₄ → Cl₂ (pungent
    gas Z). "Dono reactions important — note kar lo."
63. **Highest OS of U and Pu** — Key: U = +6, Pu = +7. "2019 mein aaya, baad mein bhi — OS bhi
    favourite hai."
64. **Last electron of Z=71 (Lu) enters which subshell** — Key: naive logic says 4f (Lu is the
    last lanthanide), but Yb (70) is already 4f¹⁴ → Lu's electron goes to **5d**. The 4f/5d
    closeness caveat applies but answer follows the book configs.
65. **Transition element with least ΔH-atomization** — Key: **Zn** (graph rises then falls; last
    member = minimum). "Direct yaad hona chahiye."
66. **Lab-manual: sodium oxalate sequence** — Key: Na₂C₂O₄ + conc. H₂SO₄ → CO₂ + CO (effervescence
    → oxalate present); + acidified CaCl₂ → white CaC₂O₄ ppt; which decolourizes acidic KMnO₄
    (oxalate reduces it → Mn²⁺). "Ek hi page se poora question — without the page it looks random."
67. **I⁻ oxidized by permanganate in ALKALINE medium (IIT 2004)** — Key: acidic → I₂, but alkaline
    → **IO₃⁻**. "Yahi ek point hai jo main difference hai — yaad rakhna."
68. **Ammonium dichromate firework (green powder)** — Key: (NH₄)₂Cr₂O₇ → N₂ + Cr₂O₃ + H₂O; green
    powder = **Cr₂O₃** (also the N₂-preparation reaction).
