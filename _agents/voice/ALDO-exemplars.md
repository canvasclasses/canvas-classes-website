## B. Must-memorize items & decision rules he licenses (use in ⚡ sections)

- **Name reactions = pure ratta, no mechanism ever asked:** Rosenmund (COCl → CHO, Pd/BaSO₄), Stephen (RCN → RCHO via SnCl₂/HCl), Etard (toluene CH₃ → CHO, CrO₂Cl₂), Gattermann–Koch (benzene → benzaldehyde, CO/HCl/AlCl₃). "Ye saare important name reactions hain. Inke upar koi mechanism nahin poochhega — bas kabhi naam poochh liya, kabhi match karwa liya, kabhi reaction sequence ke beech mein use kar liya."
- **DIBAL-H** = the ester/nitrile → aldehyde reagent ("moderately strong — all the way alcohol tak nahin le jayega"). Always followed by aqueous workup.
- **LiAlH₄ + amide → AMINE (RCH₂NH₂), not alcohol** — "ye baakiyon se different hai, iska dhyaan rakhna" (he repeats this across two lectures).
- **Reducing/oxidizing agent conversion table** — "chair laga ke, copy leke, achhe se padho. Ye table aapko bata raha hai kaun reagent kisko kis mein convert karta hai."
- **Carbonyl reactivity order (his two-factor chant):** HCHO > RCHO > RCOR > PhCOR. Reasoning he always voices: alkyl = donation + steric hindrance; phenyl = resonance donation kills the electrophilic centre. Same logic powers Grignard equivalents and protection strategy.
- **Acid-derivative reactivity:** RCOCl > anhydride > ester > amide — because the leaving group quality decides; "Cl is a very pyaara leaving group, badi jaldi bahar nikalta hai."
- **Grignard base-first doctrine:** "First wo hamesha as a base kaam karna chahta hai. Acidic hydrogen mil jaye to as a base; nahin mile to phir as a nucleophile." pKa anchors he quotes: H₂O 15.7, ROH ~16.5, terminal alkyne ~25, alkane ~45 — product acid is far weaker, so the acid-base step always wins.
- **Esters eat TWO equivalents of Grignard** (Z leaves → ketone → second attack). One equivalent = reaction stops halfway.
- **1,2 vs 1,4 addition:** RLi and RMgX → 1,2-addition on α,β-unsaturated carbonyl; R₂CuLi → 1,4 (conjugate). "Bas yahi difference yaad rakhna hai."
- **Aldol recognition both ways:** product is β-hydroxy carbonyl (NEVER α-hydroxy, NEVER 1,2-dicarbonyl); with heat → α,β-unsaturated carbonyl. HCHO crossed-aldol shortcut: "alpha position pe H hata ke CH₂OH laga do. As simple as that."
- **Cannizzaro gate:** no α-H on BOTH partners; in crossed Cannizzaro the MORE electrophilic carbonyl gets oxidized, "aur ye bhai sahab ho jayenge reduce."
- **Test recognition pack (ratta licensed):** iodoform needs COCH₃ or CH(OH)CH₃ (halogen first oxidizes the alcohol — "isiliye wo de pata hai"); Tollens also positive for formic acid, terminal alkynes, 1,2-dicarbonyls, α-hydroxy ketones, hemiacetals ("abhi yaad kar lo, abhi samjhane ka samay nahin hai"); **aromatic aldehydes do NOT give Fehling**; 2,4-DNP (Brady) = any carbonyl; sodium nitroprusside blood-red = N + S both present; neutral FeCl₃ = phenol; ceric ammonium nitrate red = any OH; NaHCO₃ effervescence = COOH (or anything more acidic).
- **Popoff's rule:** in harsh ketone oxidation, carbonyl stays with the SMALLER alkyl group; chain breaks on the more-substituted side.
- **Hofmann bromamide recognition cue:** Br₂/KOH + a carbon LOST in the molecular formula ⇒ the precursor was an amide. "Ek carbon kam hua — ab dimaag mein kuchh yaad aaya?"
- **RCN hydrolysis stages:** partial (1 eq H₂O) → RCONH₂; complete → RCOOH.
- **RMgX + CO₂ → RCOOH** ("seedha pata hona chahiye"); RX + Mg/ether, then D₂O = the standard way to plant deuterium.
- **OMSGAP** for diacids (see analogy table) + malic/fumaric (geometrical isomers) + phthalic.
- **Ethanenitrile = CH₃CN** — two carbons TOTAL; nitrile carbon counts in the chain. "C₂H₅CN mat likh dena."
- **−78 °C demystified:** "aisa nahin ki exactly −78 pe kuchh hota hai — wo bas us solvent ke cold bath ka temperature hai."

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **Grignard vs acidic H (the chapter's #1 trap):** PhMgBr + a substrate carrying OH — the rushed student does nucleophilic addition at C=O. He dramatizes the whole wrong chain: "Aapne directly socha hoga — sir, PhMgBr yahan attack karega, CHOH-Ph banega, ek OH wahan, ek OH neeche... do answer aa jayega. Right? WRONG." Acid-base happens first: benzene gas, O⁻MgBr, carbonyl untouched. Answer: ZERO hydroxy groups. "Tha na aasan question — aur isme silly mistake ke poore scope the."
- **Strong oxidant won't stop at aldehyde:** Na₂Cr₂O₇/KMnO₄ on 1° alcohol — "aldehyde pe rukega nahin, carboxylic tak chala jayega." Mild (PCC, Tollens-as-reagent) stops.
- **α,β-unsaturated carbonyl + CN⁻ (conjugate-addition trap):** student builds the cyanohydrin at the carbonyl and then EVERY option starts looking true — he voices the full spiral ("sir, racemic bhi ban raha hai... sir, third point bhi sahi aa raha hai... karna kya hai sir?") and then breaks it: resonance puts the electrophilic centre at the β-carbon; 1,4-addition, enol → keto, no C=C left in the product.
- **Aldol product shape:** "α-hydroxy carbonyl banta hi nahin — banta hi β-hydroxy hai." And final aldol products never contain two carbonyls — option-scan kills wrong structures without solving.
- **Cannizzaro needs NO α-H** — if α-hydrogens exist, "Cannizzaro to nahin hone wala yahan pe."
- **CO₂ source in the NaHCO₃ test:** "ye mat sochna ki sir COOH tha to CO₂ wahan se nikli. Nahin nahin — gas yahan (bicarbonate) se nikal rahi hai." Sulfonic acids also give CO₂, not SO₂/SO₃.
- **Wolff-Kishner vs Clemmensen medium clash:** if the same sequence must also dehydrate an OH, alkaline Wolff-Kishner can't — "wo acidic medium ko sensitive hai, basic ko nahin." Zn/HCl (Clemmensen-type) is forced. (He also critiques: "zinc amalgam likhte to behtar lagta.")
- **"Respectively" flips answers:** two isomers A and B both match the chemistry — "wo 'respectively' poori game palat dega."
- **Alkaline medium ⇒ carboxylate, not acid:** KMnO₄/KOH gives COO⁻ ("COOH nahin banega — COO⁻ banega; acid mein dala TAB bana benzoic"); haloform gives RCOO⁻ — "dhyaan rakhna, O-negative pe jaana hai, carboxylic acid mat bana dena."
- **H₂/Pd without the poison is FULL reduction:** "kya ye Lindlar's catalyst hai? Quinoline waghaira kuchh diya hua hai? Nahin diya. To triple bond poora single bond tak jayega."
- **Aromatic vs aliphatic CHO for Fehling:** ring-attached CHO behaves like PhCHO (no Fehling); a CH₂ spacer makes it aliphatic again. The two "both look right" options split exactly here.
- **1° alcohol dehydration needs force:** catalytic H⁺ won't dehydrate Ph-ring-CH₂OH — "1° carbocation banega, bekaar carbocation. Itna poor carbocation ban raha hai to kya dehydration favourable hogi? No." Needs conc. H₂SO₄ + heat; ester hydrolysis, by contrast, runs on catalytic H⁺.
- **Grignard preparation restrictions:** any acidic H or electrophilic site inside RX itself kills it — it attacks its own molecule (see analogy table).
- **The ratta-fails case (ester ¹⁸O hydrolysis):** with a 3° benzylic alkyl on oxygen, alkyl–O cleavage wins (stable carbocation) and the labelled O stays put — "ye ratta-rataya har baar kaam nahin karta."
- **Examiner's sloppy framing (JEE Mains specific):** reagents half-written, slashes joining what should be sequential steps — "JEE Advanced wale mindset se Mains ka paper nahin dena. Statement clear nahin hogi — options se hint lo, thoda correction wala framework leke chalo, overthinking mein gaye to nuksaan."
- **Mind-solving discipline:** after a silly mistake in oral solving, the fix is NOT "ab mind mein solve nahin karna" — it's "un silly mistakes ko ab dhyaan mein rakhna; jahan zyada doubt lage, us step ko likh lo, baaki mind mein."

**DPP 1 — preparation + nucleophilic addition**

2. **Tollens on a keto-aldehyde** — Key: mild oxidant logic — ketone untouched, CHO → COOH; option autopsy: "inhone ulta reduction kar di — ye option out ho gayi."
3. **PhMgBr + hydroxy-substrate** — THE trap exemplar (see C, Grignard vs acidic H). Open: "Ye achha question hai aur isme silly mistake hone ke chances hain. Jaldi se solve kariye." Close: "Answer: zero. Tha na aasan question."
4. **Alkyne alkylation → Hg²⁺ hydration → NaBH₄ → conc. H₂SO₄** — Open: oral-solve flex ("ye question aaram se orally solve ho gaya; bas ek point pe dhyaan dena tha jahan rearrangement hoga"). Key: Markovnikov enol → ketone shortcut; 2° → 3° carbocation shift; double bond lands where α-H count (hyperconjugation) is max. Close: "Overall decent question tha. Tough to main nahin bolunga."
5. **Count the correct reactions** — Open: "Again very simple, 30 seconds." Key: Friedel-Crafts gives C=O not CH₂; Rosenmund gives CHO not COOH; CONH₂ + H₃O⁺ is NOT how you make amines. Close: "Sirf ek sahi hai. Aasan question tha."
6. **Match-list: name reactions** — Key: the ratta sermon (§B) — "yahan samjhane wala kuchh bhi nahin hai; ye yaad karne hain." Close: "4-3-2-1, first option. Simple."
7. **COCH₃ + HCN → heat with H₂SO₄** — Key: BOTH things happen — OH dehydrates AND CN hydrolyzes; voices the doubt: "yahan confusion aa sakta tha — sir, cyanide ko reaction karaun ya sirf dehydration karoon?" Plus the partial/complete hydrolysis staircase (RCN → RCONH₂ → RCOOH).
8. **Protect the ketone (ethylene glycol acetal)** — Open: "Hum ye concept directly class mein padh chuke hain." Key: full strategy narration — Nu⁻ prefers ketone over ester, so to hit the ester you must protect the ketone first; self-Q: "aap bologe — sir, ester ke saath kyon nahin bana acetal? Kyonki ketone ki reactivity zyada hai." Close: "Bas itni si kahani thi."
9. **Excess LiAlH₄ on a polyfunctional substrate** — Key: checklist by group — C=C untouched, COOH → CH₂OH, NO₂ → NH₂, ketone → alcohol. Eliminate options by the one group each gets wrong.
10. **Alkyne → SN2 → H₂/Pd → CrO₃** — Key: the Lindlar check (§C); CrO₃ takes 2° alcohol to ketone and STOPS. Close: "First option — kyonki Lindlar's catalyst nahin hai, ye catalytic hydrogenation hai, poora reduce hoga."
11. **Aryl allyl ether + excess HBr → alc. KOH → reductive ozonolysis** — Open: "Bachche, jab bhi ether mein ek taraf benzene ring ho, uska phenol banta hai." Key: allylic AND benzylic resonance structures both checked — "dono se aapko same hi cheez mil rahi hai, to confusion bhi nahin hai." Close: "Ye saare steps mind mein karne ki try karo — achha lagega, maza aayega."
12. **DIBAL on a lactone** — Key: honest dissent — "DIBAL is CHO ko bhi to reduce kar leta... par kya inhone kahin diya? Nahin diya. Yahi samasya hai. Option ke according hi answer dena padta hai." (Models exam pragmatism without hiding the chemistry.)
13. **NaHSO₃ addition statements** — Key: proton exchange to the more stable form; meta-aside to note-takers: "baad mein tum notes mein na bolo ki sir ye kya likha tha — ye lo, aise likh lo."

15. **C9H10O isomer count (vague PYQ)** — Open: "Ye bada pyaara question laga mujhe… mazey ki baat hai, inhone is pe khaas kahani bhi nahin di."
