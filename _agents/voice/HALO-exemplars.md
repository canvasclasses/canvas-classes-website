## B. Must-memorize rules & the decision framework (use in ⚡ sections)

**The SN1/SN2/E1/E2 decision narration (his exact order of checks):**
1. **Base/nucleophile strength FIRST.** Strong + compact (OH⁻, RO⁻) ⇒ second-order world
   (SN2/E2). Weak/neutral (H₂O, ROH) ⇒ first-order world (SN1+E1, always together).
2. **Substrate degree.** 1°: SN2 (E2 only if bulky base). 3°: never SN2 — strong base ⇒ E2;
   weak base ⇒ SN1+E1. 2°: "sab kuch kar lete hain — yahin saara confusion hai" → check
   everything: solvent, Nu strength, neighbouring bulk.
3. **Solvent.** Polar aprotic (DMF, DMSO, acetone) ⇒ SN2 signal from the examiner. Protic
   (water, alcohol) ⇒ stabilizes carbocation ⇒ SN1/E1.
4. **Temperature.** Heat ⇒ elimination percentage rises; low T ⇒ substitution. Reason he
   gives: elimination breaks two σ bonds and makes one weaker π — "overall reaction
   demands input of energy."
5. **Special attackers.** S⁻/CN⁻/I⁻ = great nucleophile, weak base ⇒ SN2. Bulky t-BuO⁻ =
   great base, useless nucleophile ⇒ E2 (and Hofmann product). For E2 prefer RO⁻ over
   RS⁻ — "RS⁻ achhe base ki jagah zyada achha nucleophile hai, wo reaction ko SN2 ki
   taraf le jayega."
6. **Silver salts.** AgNO₃ "jabardasti ka carbocation mechanism follow karwa deti hai" —
   can force SN1 even on 1° halides (then rearrangements follow).

**Hard anchors he drills:**
- E2 geometry: anti-periplanar / trans-diaxial. "Agar nahi hue to E2 nahi hoga — kisi aur
  mechanism se karwa lo." Syn-periplanar fails because base and X (both electron-rich)
  repel in the TS. On chairs: ring-flip if X is equatorial. On Fischer projections:
  "Fischer ko Newman mein convert karo, anti-coplanar mein rakho leaving groups ko."
- Why 3° > 2° > 1° in E2 (no carbocation!): the TS has alkene character — "jo factor
  alkene ko stabilize karte hain wahi TS ko bhi karenge" (hypercongugation count).
- Saytzeff = energy, not decree: "aisa nahi ki Saytzeff ne bola isliye hota hai — it's
  all energy based" (lower-Ea TS for the more substituted alkene).
- Hofmann triggers (any one): bulky base; β-H in a hindered spot; quaternary ammonium /
  sulfonium / phosphonium salt; poor LG like F⁻. Base bulk ↑ ⇒ Hofmann % ↑ (70/30 →
  28/72 → 12/88); LG poorer (I→F) ⇒ Hofmann % ↑.
- E1cB requirements: β-carbon carries a strong EWG (makes α... his words: the H on the
  carbon NEXT to the EWG turns acidic) + poor LG (F, OH, NR₃⁺). First step fast (conjugate
  base forms), second step is RDS — rate = k[conjugate base]¹.
- Stereo outcomes table: SN2 = always inversion (Walden), stereospecific — but "R ka S
  banega iski guarantee nahi" (priority order may change). SN1 = inversion > retention,
  never 100% racemization (ion pair). SOCl₂ alone = retention (SNi); SOCl₂ + pyridine =
  inversion (SN2); PCl₅/PBr₃ = inversion.
- Leaving-group logic: best LG = weakest base ("simple acid-base concept lag jayega").
  OH⁻ is never displaced directly — "OH ko nikalte hain OH₂⁺ bana ke, ya OTs bana ke.
  OTs is a wonderful leaving group — bahut pyaara." Better LG speeds BOTH SN1 and E1
  (RCl + NaI → RI first, then fast hydrolysis).
- Finkelstein: RX + NaI in DRY acetone (NaI soluble; NaCl/NaBr precipitate, so no back
  attack). SN2, inversion. Components of a name reaction are fixed.
- BUT vicinal dibromide + NaI/acetone = anti elimination of Br₂ (E2-type), NOT
  Finkelstein — "blindly mat chale jaana."
- Swarts: metallic fluorides (AgF, Hg₂F₂, CuF₂) for fluorination — direct F₂ is violent.
- SOCl₂ = best route to RCl (SO₂ + HCl escape as gases — no separation effort).
- KCN (ionic) → RCN via C-attack (C less electronegative = better Nu centre); AgCN
  (covalent) → RNC via N-attack (C is locked in bonding).
- Vinyl/aryl halides: no SN1 (terrible carbocation), no SN2 (sp², back attack blocked;
  +M of halogen gives the C–X bond double-bond character). Need drastic conditions or
  strong EWGs for nucleophilic aromatic substitution.
- Hunsdiecker shortens the chain by one carbon — "jaldbaazi mein bachcha paanch carbon
  ka bromide bana deta hai."
- Dipole moment order: CH₃Cl > CH₃F > CH₃Br > CH₃I — "NCERT mein directly table mein
  values ke saath de rakha hai"; charge × length, the length factor dominates.
- NGP: backside internal attack (NG and LG must be anti-coplanar) → 3-/5-membered ring
  intermediate → external Nu attacks either ring carbon (two products possible) →
  double inversion = net retention. π-bonds (benzene ring) also count as NG.

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **"Benzylic ⇒ SN1 only":** "Maximum bachche bolte the — sir SN1, kyunki benzyl hai,
  achha carbocation banega. Par benzyl mein SN2 BHI achha hota hai." Tie-breaker: EDG on
  ring ⇒ SN1; EWG on ring ⇒ SN2 (EWG stabilizes the partial-negative TS). He shows the
  exact Clayden page this JEE question was copied from.
- **"SN1 gives 50-50 racemization":** "Aap bologe — sir carbocation planar hai, 50%
  retention 50% inversion. Aisa nahi hota." Ion pair: front side stays occupied,
  inversion always > retention.
- **Hydride vs methyl shift:** "Hydride shift karwate to zyada stable carbocation banta —
  methyl kyon shift karwa diya?" Always check which shift gives the more stable cation.
- **Octet-completion beats degree:** with α-OCH₃, "kai bachchon ke mind mein aayega — sir
  hydride shift karwa ke 2° benzylic bana lein. Nahi. OCH₃ ke resonance se octet complete
  ho raha hai — aisa carbocation 3° se bhi zyada stable hai." Cation stays put.
- **Repeated-PYQ autopilot:** the Wurtz question looks like the old IIT one — "are sir,
  ye to IIT ka repeat hai — khatak answer laga diya, dekha hi nahi. Aisa nahi." The Mg in
  the next step proves A must still contain a halogen (Br reacts before Cl in Wurtz).
- **Keto-enol blindness in alkaline medium:** cyclohexenone eliminations — students get
  "B = C, A different" and mark it; real answer D because alkaline medium tautomerizes
  all three to phenol ("enol aapko aromaticity provide kar raha hai — reaction wahan
  rukega hi nahi").
- **E2 without the anti check:** "Jaldbaazi mein bachcha Saytzeff product bana dega. Wo H
  to anti-coplanar hai hi nahi — wo wala H aap E2 mein nikal hi nahi sakte." And on rings:
  "mat bol dena — sir isme cis-trans bhi hoga. Ring ke andar double bond ko cis-trans
  ke liye at least 8-membered ring chahiye."
- **Finkelstein reflex on a vicinal dibromide:** NaI/acetone with TWO adjacent Br ⇒ Br₂
  eliminates (anti), no iodide product.
- **"Beautifully worded wrong reason" (assertion-reason):** vinyl halide question — reason
  says the carbocation is "stabilized by loosely held π electrons." "Bade sundarta se
  likha hai — lagta hai sahi hai. Par vinyl carbocation is VERY unstable, bahut last
  mein aata hai bechara stability order mein. Majority people will make a mistake."
- **Bridgehead halide:** "Aapko lagega — sir 3° carbocation banega. No. Bridgehead
  position pe carbocation nahi bante. Wo wala Br nahi niklega."
- **Sandmeyer scope:** only Cl and Br via CuX (plus CN, SO₃H as extra-NCERT additions) —
  never F, I, At from Sandmeyer.
- **Diazonium endgame:** to REMOVE the N₂⁺ and put H, it's H₃PO₂ — "CuBr le liya to wahan
  bromo lag jayega. Mere ko yahan bromo thodi na lagana hai." And the giveaway: "NO₂ jab
  bhi last mein nahi hota — samajh jao: NO₂ → NH₂ → N₂⁺ → H₃PO₂ se bahar."
- **Mechanism step-skipping:** water attack on a carbocation gives OH₂⁺ first, then loses
  H⁺ — "jaldbaazi mein bachche sochte hain water attack karega, OH lag jayega.
  Directly nahi lagta." (Energy-profile question: count the hills = count the steps; the
  FIRST hill must be tallest — carbocation formation is the RDS.)
- **Excess vs limited reagent:** excess NH₃ ⇒ both C–Cl ends substituted; "agar limited
  diya hota to ek pe attack hota aur cyclization wala case kar sakte the."
- **Competing-nucleophile misread:** the Br⁻ formed DURING Finkelstein is not a competing
  nucleophile — "jab reaction start hua tha us samay bromide tha hi nahi" (and it
  precipitates anyway). A competing Nu is like water present alongside I⁻.
- **"Alcoholic KOH banayega triple bond":** no — single elimination only. Second HX
  removal needs NaNH₂. But also the reverse trap: with 3 equiv alc. KOH on a vicinal
  dibromide, you don't need a triple bond — pull one H from each side, get a conjugated
  diene ("teesra alcoholic KOH ka koi kaam hi nahi bacha, koi baat nahi").
- **E1cB deuterium puzzle:** PhCH₂CH₂Br + EtO⁻/EtOD gives PhCD=CH₂ — "E2 single step mein
  hota hai, ye D wahan aa hi nahi sakta tha. Sirf E1cB se possible hai" (carbanion picks
  D from solvent, repeat, then eliminate).
- **His honesty markers (model these, never fake certainty):** "Mere according answer
  fourth option hi hona chahiye — it doesn't make sense at all" (disputes the given key
  on a ring-expansion question); "Maybe maine kahin galti kar di ho — I will check it
  after the session" (cis-2-butene stereo outcome he promises to re-verify).
