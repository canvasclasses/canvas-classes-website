## B. Must-memorize constants he teaches (use in ⚡ sections)

- **hc = 1240 eV·nm** (his standard for photon problems) and hc = 2×10⁻²⁵ J·m

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **PE vs TE:** "Poochha PE hai, bachche TE likh aate hain — PE = 2×TE (magnitude). Do se multiply karna na bhoolein." Variation he himself offers: KE question → PE variant, double the magnitude.
- **Matter wave vs EM wave:** de Broglie λ confused with c = νλ — "isko confuse mat karna; c = νλ sirf EM waves ke liye."
- **Frequency double ⇒ KE double (false):** the ₹500/book analogy kills it — "common sense bhi lagaani hai."
- **Rydberg shortcut scope:** the 13.6(1−1/n²) family only valid falling TO n=1 — "paanch se two pe ye formula nahi laga sakte."
- **Hypothetical-atom reflex:** in modified-universe questions (only even orbits etc.), the reflex answer uses the normal universe — "aap bologe sir three se girega. Three toh hai hi nahin!"
- **"Collision" red herring:** electron-collision wording makes students reach for physics momentum conservation — "aisa kuch nahi hai; energy gain hua, level badla."
- **h/π vs h/2π:** orbital angular momentum √(l(l+1))·h/2π — "dhyaan dena, yahan h/π hai, two nahi hai" (read the given form letter by letter).
- **Incomplete subshells in counting questions:** "4p² completely filled nahi hai — isko consider nahi karenge."
- **Nucleus/infinity are not nodes.**
- **Examiner's disguise:** ground-state energy given via ionization energy instead — "kya karta hai examiner..."

## D. Per-question exemplars (from DPP 1–3) — opening → key move → closing

1. **e/m of cathode rays, H vs D** — Open: "15-second question." Key: electrons are electrons, gas doesn't matter. Close: "Kuchh solve karne ka nahi tha — simple basic conceptual."
2. **p_e/p_p after same accelerating V** — Open: anchor "electric work done = gain in KE." Key: p = √(2mqV); only mass differs. Close: "Aasan question tha."
3. **Laser power output (quanta/s)** — Open: "Nikalna kya hai? Power. Power kya hota hai? Energy per unit time." Key: E = n·hc/λ; units auto-balance. Close: "Units ki barabari — that is the key point here."
4. **Photochemical dissociation O₂ → O + O*** — Open: longer budget, "thoda effort lagega." Key: the special atom personified ("star wala — taare lage hue hain"); convert both energies to one unit, THEN add; λmax ⇒ Emin. Close: "Thoda different. Difficult nahi bolunga."
5. **Intensity halved → y and z?** — Key: toy numbers (10 photons → 5); each photon's energy unchanged ⇒ KE same, electron count halves.
6. **If Thomson were right (gold foil)** — Key: history-as-story (Rutherford was trying to CONFIRM Thomson); uniform charge ⇒ tiny uniform deflection, slight slowing.
7. **KE-vs-ν and V₀-vs-ν slope ratio** — Open: "1 minute max, not more." Key: compare both to y = mx + c; slopes h and h/e ⇒ ratio e. Close: "Aasan tha but different tha — pehli baar dekha hoga."
8. **Black-body multi-statement (PYQ)** — Open: "Bahut pyaara PYQ." Key: NCERT-is-the-framework sermon (4 lines → read 40). Close: "Fundu question — required some extra study."
9. **TE vs KE in excited state** — Open: "20-second question." Key: |TE| = |KE| at ANY level — excited/ground is a distractor. Close: verdict + PE variation offer.
10. **Radius 5.3→21.2 fm after collision** — Open: trap-scan "kya trick ho sakta hai? Waise kuchh khaas nahi." Key: physical picture first (gained energy → farther orbit), then rₙ = n²r₁. Close: names the collision red herring.
11. **Speed → distance** — Open: strategy skeleton in one breath. Key: hidden unknown is n; get it from v, then r.
12. **Maxwell hypothetical (ν_emitted = ν_revolution)** — Open: flags novelty + 1–1.5 min budget; "tough nahi, figure out karne mein time lagega." Key: justify WHY c = νλ applies (it's Maxwell's EM claim); "janab apni ground state mein hain" ⇒ r = 0.529 Å.
13. **Even-orbits hypothetical → λmax visible** — Key: Socratic reflex-break ("three toh hai hi nahin — four se girega").
14. **Binding energy from 3rd Balmer line** — Open: "Dhyaan se padhna" + decode "third line = 5→2". Key: the elegant dodge — "Z se matlab hi kya hai? Poori term hi binding energy hai." Close: "Pyaara question tha na?"
15. **Same de Broglie λ: e, α, p energies** — Open: "45–50 seconds, not difficult at all." Key: strip the equation ("h hata do, two hata do, root hata do — mKE same"); α ≈ 4 protons by mass.
16. **95% boundary surface multi-statement** — Open: "Beautiful question" + why multi-statement Qs are good. Key: THE sheher/moon-trip analogy. Close: "Achchha question tha na."
17. **Quantum-number statements** — Key: critiques the question's own wording (spin "orientations" → "two quantum mechanical states"); models critical reading of examiners.
18. **Ge: filled orbitals with mₗ = 0** — Open: empathy ("kuchh bachche pareshaan hote hain — pehli baat, configuration likhni padegi properly"). Key: dabba-counting chant; excludes incomplete 4p². Close: "Answer is seven."
19. **Hypothetical l ≤ n+1** — Open: praise + honest dissent from online solutions ("I might be wrong, par mujhe gadbad lagi — 2s kahan jayega bechaara?"). Key: rebuild filling order from n+l rule under the new premise.
20. **2s energy: Li vs Na vs K** — Key: NCERT sermon with exact citation (para before 2.6.4) → became a 2019 PYQ. "History ki tarah nahi, chemistry ki tarah padhna hai."
21. **n=4, s=+½ in filled subshells** — Key: split each filled subshell into spin halves (1+3+5+7=16). Close: practice doctrine ("pareshaan nahi hona — dhyaan se padho, likho, answer saamne hai").
22. **L = 5h/π → how many orbitals** — Open: names what's unusual (L GIVEN, not asked). Key: "h/π hai, two nahi" detail alarm; l = 4 ⇒ 9 orbitals; orbit number is a red herring.
23. **Min Δv in region 2a₀** — Open: honest effort-typing ("bas calculation hai, zyada dimaag nahi"). Key: max Δx = region size (balloon logic); min Δv ⇐ max Δx.
24. **n=4 mₗ=0 / n=5 mₗ=+2 pair** — Key: dabba chant again; disqualified subshells personified ("s waala bhai sahab qualify nahi hue"). Close: confidence-builder ("ab aap saare question kar paoge").
