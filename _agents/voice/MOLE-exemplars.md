## B. Must-memorize constants/shortcuts he teaches (use in ⚡ sections)

- **Volume strength of H₂O₂: x = 5.6 × Normality = 11.2 × Molarity.** He derives it once (68 g → 22.4 L O₂), then: "agar molarity de rakhi hai, 11.2 se multiply kar do — bas itna hi karna hai."
- **CₓHᵧ + (x + y/4) O₂ → x CO₂ + (y/2) H₂O** — "ye default hai, yaad rakhna hai isko." His standard for every combustion / gas-analysis question.
- **Molality from molarity:** m = 1000M / (1000d − M·M_w). "Formula derive kar liya hai toh directly lagana hai — sab kuch de rakha hai."
- **Mole concept = one expression:** moles of species ÷ its stoichiometric coefficient, all equal. Precondition he hammers: "the equation HAS to be balanced — tabhi aap mole concept apply kar paenge."
- **Limiting reagent recipe:** convert given amounts to moles, divide each by its stoichiometric coefficient, **the smaller value is the LR** — works for ugly decimals too.
- **Molecular weight = 2 × vapour density.**
- **Ratta list of molar masses** (15-min video): H₂SO₄ = H₃PO₄ = 98, urea = acetic acid = 60, CaCO₃ = 100, HCl = 36.5, HNO₃ = 63, KClO₃ = 122.5, NaOH = 40, glucose = 180, sucrose = 342. "Ye yaad kar lena, bade important hain."
- **Common n-factors:** Cr₂O₇²⁻ → 6; MnO₄⁻ (acidic) → 5; MnO₄⁻ (neutral) → 3; MnO₄⁻ → MnO₄²⁻ → 1; H₂O₂ → 2. Salt = total cationic charge; acid = basicity; base = acidity.
- **Avogadro constant has units** — mol⁻¹ ("Avogadro constant ke units hote hain, Avogadro number ke nahi") — an old IIT fill-in-the-blank he cites.
- **1 u = 1.66×10⁻²⁷ kg** = 1/12 mass of one C-12 atom.
- **Eudiometry absorbents:** CO₂/SO₂ in KOH, O₂ in alkaline pyrogallol, CO in ammoniacal cuprous chloride — "mostly CO₂ ke baare mein hi baat aati hai."
- **Average-atomic-mass number-line shortcut:** Cl 35.5 between 35 and 37 → divide the gap into quarters; value sits at the ¼ mark ⇒ 3:1 abundance. "Average heavier part ki taraf tilt hota jaayega."

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **Irrelevant data planted to confuse:** the "27 mL of mixture" in the equal-molecules question — "ye confuse karne ke liye information add ki hai; iska koi role nahi. Directly question ko padhte attack nahi karna — pehle samjho, irrelevant information ko bypass karo."
- **Solvent vs solution in the statement:** density of SOLVENT given, student plugs it as solution density — "Sir ye kya dhokha hai? Yahi toh maine bola — statement dhyaan se padhiye. Solution ki density nahi di, solvent ki di hai." (JEE Adv PYQ; his favourite MOLE trap.)
- **Atoms vs molecules under Avogadro's law:** equal V, T, P → "khatak karke answer 1:1:1:1 de diya, aur khatak karke galat. Atoms ki baat hui hai, molecules ki nahi" → ratio 3:4:1:5.
- **Mass-ratio laws quoted on volume data:** Gay-Lussac data ≠ law of definite proportions — "law of definite proportion fixed ratio of MASS ki baat karta hai, volume ki nahi." Sometimes the answer is genuinely "none of these".
- **Assuming reactants fully consumed:** CO₂ + C → CO with 1.5 L collected — "kisne kaha ki ye poori tarah react kar gayi? Ye assume karna ki reactant poora consume ho gaya, ye bhi ek galti hoti hai."
- **Equal masses ≠ equal moles (LR questions):** "weight mein dhyaan rakhna — mole 1:1 the, weight 1:1 mat kar dena."
- **The empirical-formula reflex:** combustion data given, percentage asked — "jab bhi C-H ka aisa question aata hai, soch formula ki direction mein jaati hai. Mujhe formula poocha hi nahi — percentage nikaalni hai. That kind of thinking can waste a few seconds." (C₉.₅H₂₃.₃₃ doesn't fit a formula — and doesn't need to.)
- **"Vapour" wording:** metal chloride as 100 mL VAPOUR — "normally aadat hoti hai gas padhne ki; vapour dekhte hi lagta hai ye kya hai?" Treat it as gas, 22400 mL/mol applies.
- **Volume is not additive — but assume ideal:** "waise volume is not an additive property, but ideal scenario le ke chalna hi padega" (10 mL Br₂ + 90 mL CCl₄ = 100 mL).
- **n-factor is per-reaction, not per-compound:** H₂SO₄ → Na₂SO₄ (n=2) vs → NaHSO₄ (n=1) — "chhod SAKTA hai, 'sakta hai' waali baat nahi hoti. Reaction mein chhoda hai ki nahi chhoda? Nahi chhoda — toh n-factor one."
- **Writing before clarity:** "Randomly kuch likh doge copy ke upar, phir usi ko sochte reh jaoge. Galat cheez se start karoge toh usi mein phans ke reh jaoge. Jab tak clarity nahi hai kya karna hai, kuch karna nahi hai." And: "pehla step galat toh agla step bhi galat — time bhi waste, confidence bhi girega. Don't just write given data."
- **Incomplete JEE Main statements (vs Advanced):** alcohol giving H₂ with no alkali metal mentioned — "Advanced ka question bahut neatly ek-ek point likhta hai... ye aadhi-adhoori chhod ke chale jaate hain. Phir confusion create hota hai."
- **Examiner's mixed-concept disguise:** mole questions built on organic/inorganic reactions (haloform, NaHCO₃ + oxalic acid, Zn + HCl) — "jisko ye reaction nahi pata, woh kitna bhi expert ho mole concept mein, ye wala part pehle hai. Multiple chapters interlink ho jaate hain questions ke through."

### DPP 1 (rOjFuHsVQu4)

1. **0.01 mol organic compound (60% C) → 4.4 g CO₂; molar mass?** — Open: "1 minute hai — aur ye question main toh bolunga aap ORALLY bhi solve kar sakte ho." Key: scale 0.01→1 mol by ×100 (440 g CO₂ = 10 mol ⇒ 120 g C = 60% ⇒ 40% = 80 g) ⇒ 200. Close: "Ye poora dimaag mein solve kar sakte the. Aasaan question."
2. **Mass of CO₂ to mix with 20 g O₂ so 27 mL of mixture has equal molecules** — Key: kill the planted 27 mL ("volume hai, data mass mein hai — confuse karne ke liye"); equal molecules ⇒ equal moles ⇒ W/44 = 20/32 ⇒ 27.5 g. Close: the read-before-you-attack sermon (see trap bank).
3. **Ag coating: 0.05 cm thick, area in m², ρ = 7.9 g/cm³ → atoms** — Open: "simple sa question, just thodi si calculation." Key: unit mismatch is the whole question — convert m² → cm² (×10⁴) because density is in cm; volume → mass → moles → ×Nₐ chain. Close: "Units ka dhyaan rakhna bada important hai — isliye ye question discuss karvaya."
4. **Hemoglobin 0.34% Fe; atoms in 3.3 g** — Open: "45 second ke andar aaraam se ho jaana chahiye." Key: decode the statement first — "100 gram mein 0.34 gram Fe. 3.3 mein kitna? Bas UNITARY METHOD." Close: easy; "solution isliye le rahe hain taaki samay na barbaad karein."
5. **1.84 mg polyhydric alcohol (M=92) → 1.344 mL H₂ at STP; alcoholic H count** — Open: names why he chose it — "bachcha normally dekhega toh samajh nahi aata ye kya hai; organic ko context mein laoge toh jaldi aayega." Key: OH hydrogen is acidic; alkali metal liberates H₂; each H ⇒ ½H₂ ⇒ x/2 mol per mol alcohol; equate with 22400-mL mole count ⇒ x = 6. Mid-question rant: JEE Main's incomplete statements vs Advanced's neat ones. Close: "Jinko galti ho rahi hogi, wahi pe ho rahi hogi — aage ka aasaan hai, aap kar loge mujhe pata hai."
6. **10 mg effervescent tablet (NaHCO₃ + oxalic acid) → CO₂, molar volume 25 L; % NaHCO₃** — Open: story-frame ("ye goli hai; goli ko paani mein daalenge — mention nahi kiya par sawaal ka saar usi ke aaspaas hai"). Key: oxalic acid releases NO CO₂ in water — every mole of CO₂ traces to NaHCO₃ 1:1; "yahi key point hai question ka." 8.4%. Close: the mixed-concept doctrine — "multiple chapters interlink ho jaate hain."
7. **10 g S + 12 g O₂ → SO₂ + SO₃, nothing left over** — Open: hard time-box: "1 minute. Usse lamba kiya toh you are missing the KEY STRATEGY." Trap dramatized in student voice: "Accha sir 22 gram ho gaya, 22 gram ka kya karein? Limiting bhi nahi hai — dono khatam ho rahe hain. Kya karein?" Key: MOLE CONSERVATION of S (equation can't be balanced — "stoichiometry kya hogi? We don't know"); mass conservation gives 22 = x + (22−x); 10/32 = x/64 + (22−x)/80 ⇒ SO₂ 12 g, SO₃ 10 g. Close: "Jahan reaction balance karna mushkil ho, mole conservation is a great way."
8. **H:D = 6000:1; neutrons in 3 g** — Open: "1, max 1.5 minute." Key: physical reasoning before formula — H has NO neutrons, D has one ⇒ neutrons = D atoms; D so scarce the average atomic weight stays 1 ⇒ 3 g = 3 mol; divide by 6001 ⇒ 3.01×10²⁰. Close: the chapter sermon — "mole concept mein formulas bade limited hain, par unke upar questions bahut zyada bante hain... aapki ek apni methodology develop ho jaati hai. ANSWER IS NOT IMPORTANT. STRATEGY IS IMPORTANT."

## E. Register notes (MOLE-specific)

- **The chapter's signature move is the unitary method spoken as a chant:** "100 gram mein 0.34 hai, toh 3.3 mein kitna? ... 12 gram ko 1 mol chahiye, toh 1000 gram ko kitna?" Almost every 🗺️ step in this chapter is a two-line unitary scaling, voiced as question → answer. Solutions should keep that rhythm (ask the scaling question, then answer it) rather than writing bare proportions.
- **Conservation is his named fallback whenever balancing fails:** "jahan reaction balance karna mushkil hai, MOLE CONSERVATION is a great way" (S→SO₂+SO₃, Ag→AgCl+AgBr, dilution M₁V₁=M₂V₂ all framed as "kisi cheez ko conserve kar rahe hain"). Name the conserved species explicitly in the solution.
- **Equivalents are sold as the no-balancing luxury:** "equivalent concept ka benefit — balancing nahi karni padti. Stoichiometry jo marzi ho, equal equivalents react karte hain." Use this exact selling point when a solution switches from moles to equivalents.
- **Limiting reagent has ONE canonical recipe** (given amount → moles → ÷ stoichiometric coefficient → smallest = LR), introduced via the coffee-sachet story. Don't present alternative LR methods; he mentions they exist and moves on.
- **Time budgets are tighter here than in ATOM:** 30–40s / 45s / 1 min / "1 minute, usse lamba kiya toh you are missing the key strategy" — a long budget itself flags a hatke question (2 min for the CuBr₂ mixture). Several are flagged "orally solve ho jaana chahiye."
- **The chapter-specific sermon is STRATEGY-BEFORE-PEN:** "Visualize the process. Jab tak clarity nahi, kuch likhna nahi — galat se start karoge toh usi mein phans jaoge; time bhi waste, confidence bhi girega." And its mirror: "Answer is not important, strategy is important." This belongs in 🧠 openings as "see the route before you write."
- **Statement-decoding is dramatized as the skill being tested:** solute/solvent/solution swaps, vapour vs gas, mass-% vs mole-%, mass-% vs volume data — his refrain: "aankh aur dimaag ke dhakkan khule rakhein." MOLE ⚠️ sections should quote the exact misread, not just the fix.
- **Shortcut license is explicit:** welding-gas question solved by computing M = 26 and scanning options — "zaroori nahi hai ki question ko solve kar hi lena hai; dimaag lag raha hai, shortcut lag raha hai toh laga lo." Option-scanning is a legitimate ⚡ move in this chapter (but pair with the proper method, as he does).
- **Reassurance posture for this chapter:** "Mole concept pe command nahi hai toh pareshaan nahi hona — maximum EK question aayega, aur aajkal aasaan aa rahe hain. Basics + PYQ karke chalo." Also the balance warning: "ek chapter ko zyada time doge toh doosre mein kam padega — ek planner banaiye, us pe stick kijiye." Session-only; never in solutions.
- **Transcript quality:** DPP transcripts are clean. CC L1/L2 are clean. The 15-min revision is ASR-degraded (English-heavy Hinglish mangled: "Avogadro"→"avocado", broken formula readings) — use it only for the ratta lists, which are corroborated elsewhere.
