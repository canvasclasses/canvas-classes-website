## B. Must-memorize relations he teaches (use in ⚡ sections)

- Solubility is just a concentration term — "g per mL bol do, molarity mein, molality mein, mole fraction mein — nothing else."
- Henry's law: p = K_H·x, and **K_H is INVERSELY proportional to solubility** — derive it: fix the same p for two gases, "jiska K_H bada hoga automatically x chhota ho jayega."
- **x in p = K_H·x is NOT always mole fraction** — balance the units. K_H in atm: x unitless (mole fraction); K_H in atm·L/mol: x is molarity. "Blindly ye sochna mat ki hamesha mole fraction hai."
- Clausius-Clapeyron: ln(p₂/p₁) = (ΔH_vap/R)(1/T₁ − 1/T₂) — "dhyaan rakhna yahan natural log hai. Log base 10 loge to 2.303 se multiply karna padega."
- Raoult rearranged: P_total = P°A + x_B(P°B − P°A). His memory hook: "ek ka pure pressure le lo, plus doosre ki mole fraction — andar jo P° pehla aayega wo usi ka aayega."
- The Raoult–Dalton bridge: P_T·y_A = P°A·x_A — "ye formula kaam aayega jab vapor phase ki composition nikalni ho."
- 1/P_T = y_A/P°A + y_B/P°B. Graph signature: **P_T vs x = straight line; P_T vs y = curve.**
- Deviation ↔ azeotrope mapping, derived not memorized: minimum boiling = easy to boil = weak attractions = **positive deviation**; maximum boiling = hard to boil = strong attractions = **negative deviation**.
- Deviation example lists (water–ethanol, CHCl₃–CCl₄, ethanol–hexane, ethanol–acetone...): "ye examples zaroor yaad karna. Directly question aate hain inke upar."
- Boiling = equilibrium phenomenon: vapor pressure = external pressure. Normal BP ⇒ VP = 1 atm = 760 torr — **this is the hidden-data trick** ("100 °C diya hai" means P° = 760).
- RLVP: (P° − Ps)/P° = x_solute. **The lowering ΔP alone is NOT colligative; the RELATIVE lowering is.**
- His preferred RLVP variant: **(P° − Ps)/Ps = n₁/n₂** — "koi assumption-approximation nahi hai, ye wahi standard formula ko rearrange kiya gaya hai. Calculate karna kai baar zyada aasaan hota hai."
- Dry-air experiment: loss in weight of solution ∝ P_solution; loss in weight of solvent vessel ∝ (P° − Ps).
- ΔTb = i·Kb·m, ΔTf = i·Kf·m. **Kb/Kf are the SOLVENT's constants.** Sub-formulas Kb = RTb²·M/(1000·ΔH_vap), Kf analog with ΔH_fus — "kai baar ye formula bhi directly poochh lete hain."
- The 1000-g molality shortcut: scale the given solvent mass to 1000 g and scale the solute with it — "1 kg mein jitne number of mol of solute hote hain wahi to molality hoti hai."
- van't Hoff: i = 1 + (n − 1)α, where n = particles generated PER particle: NaCl n=2, BaCl₂ n=3, dimer n=1/2, trimer n=1/3. At α = 1, i = n.
- i = observed/theoretical colligative value = normal mass/abnormal mass = particles after/particles before dissociation.
- Non-electrolytes (urea, glucose, sucrose): i = 1 "unless until it is defined ki kuchh kar rahe hain."
- Osmotic pressure: π = iCRT; **compare i·C (effective concentration), never bare C.** Mixing: π_final = (π₁V₁ + π₂V₂)/(V₁ + V₂) — "kai baar iske upar directly question aate hain."
- NCERT anchors he assigns: the solution-types table ("NCERT mein jo table hai wo follow karna along with examples"), scuba-tank composition ("uski percentage NCERT mein hai, yaad kar lena").

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

- **"Temperature badhao, solubility badhti hai" (false generalization):** "Bachhe bolenge sir badhti hai — garam paani mein hamesha cheezein zyada dissolve hoti hain. Nahi, aisa hamesha nahi hota." Depends on ΔH — cerium sulfate DECREASES. "We cannot generalize this case."
- **x = mole fraction reflex in Henry's law:** "Dey always assume ki x is mole fraction. Aap bologe sir graph mein to yahi likha hai. Haan, but —" the units decide; it can be molarity or molality.
- **K_H side-switch:** p = K_H·x vs x = K′_H·p — "proportionality constant wahin kyon aaye? Is side bhi to aa sakta tha." Units of K′_H differ; don't panic at the flipped form.
- **Natural log vs log₁₀:** "Kai baar bachhe jaldbaazi mein natural log aur log base 10 ka galti kar dete hain calculation mein."
- **Henry's law where the gas reacts:** CO₂/SO₂/SO₃ dissolve because they REACT with water — "Henry law bolega bhai sahab, maine bola tha pressure ke basis pe. Ye to aapas mein chemically react kar gaye — iski solubility main predict nahi kar sakta."
- **Vapor pressure vs partial pressure (compressed-vessel):** compress → water re-establishes its VP (equilibrium property; only T changes it); the O₂ alone obeys Boyle and doubles. "Volume badhane-ghatane se vapor pressure ko fark nahi padta."
- **Ratta-fying the deviation tables:** "Bachhe isko ratte hain — delta H aisa, aisa. Isko ratna nahi hai, samajhna hai. Pressure zyada kab aata hai?" Then the Socratic chain: more vapor ⇒ escaped more easily ⇒ attractions WEAKER than ideal.
- **Azeotrope at any ratio (false):** "Ek specific ratio pe mix karne pe hi bana azeotrope. Uske baad bhi nahi, uske pehle bhi nahi."
- **Right-conclusion-wrong-reason options:** in the min-boiling-azeotrope MCQ he dissects "VP increases because more molecules escape" — plausible-sounding, wrong; only the weaker-forces statement can be said "with confirmation."
- **The geometry reflex (SP = PQ+QR+RS):** "Geometry padhne wala banda bolega sir ye to maths ka bilkul basic cheez hai." But the exam wants Raoult: SP = P_total = SR + SQ. "Raoult law ke according solve karna hai, geometry se nahi."
- **Solution boiling above BOTH components:** the reflex says "between the two" — that's ideal only; above both ⇒ stronger forces ⇒ negative deviation. "Agar ideal hota to dono ke beech mein aata."
- **Surface-area explanation of RLVP:** the standard picture (solute blocks surface) is "an easy explanation but NOT accurate" — the entropy argument is the real one. Don't present the surface picture as the reason.
- **Two Kb values given (IIT's favorite):** "IIT ne kai baar aisa kiya hai — solute ka Kb bhi de diya, solvent ka bhi. Bachha pareshaan: do values, kaun si loon? Elevation kiska? SOLVENT ka. Agar dono ke diye hain to aapko confuse karne ke liye diya hua hai."
- **Boiling point given instead of P°:** "Yahan vapor pressure directly nahi dete — temperature bol dete hain. Boiling point ka temperature de denge; us time vapor pressure will be equal to atmospheric pressure" (100 °C ⇒ 760 torr).
- **Counting the added reactant's particles (HgI₂ + KI):** "Ab ye mat bolna — sir HgI₂ ke bhi to particle count karo. Are bhai, wo to daalte hi react kar gaya. Uske particle count nahi karne." Count the system before vs after: 4 particles → 3.
- **"Freezing point RAISED? Kaise sir?"** Smaller depression = closer to zero: "negative mein cheez chal rahi thi — we were going towards zero. Yes, freezing point is raised."
- **The −0.2 vs −0.4 sign trap:** "Aap bologe sir iski value zyada aa rahi hai, iska zyada hona chahiye. −0.4 dekhne mein badi hai, par drop negative ki taraf ja raha hai — actually value mein chhota ho jayega. Silly mistake hai ye."
- **Ice-formation questions — solvent mass is the unknown:** "Don't take 250 g water as the solvent. Ab 250 g bacha nahi hai wo" — find W from ΔTf, then ice = initial − W.
- **i vs effective concentration:** when ONLY i is asked, "keep the concentration out of the equation. Jaldbaazi mein bachha 0.05 ko five se multiply karega — wo nahi poochha."
- **Hydrate water in n-counting:** Mohr-salt type — "·6H₂O wo to ionize nahi hote" — count only the ions.
- **Solute crossing the SPM:** "NaCl will flow..." — never; "solute particles cannot move. Solvent to dono taraf ja payega but NET flow ek side pe hoga."
- **Examiner wording he critiques on air:** "Mole fraction of toluene is 0.8" — incomplete; "in liquid ya in solution likhna padega, tab sahi bol sakte ho." Models critical reading.
