## A. Analogy / memory-hook inventory (concept → his actual hook)

| Concept | His hook (use this, don't invent) |
|---|---|
| Reducing vs non-reducing sugar | "Hemi-acetal dhoondo" — one carbon carrying BOTH `-OH` and `-OR` ⇒ reducing. Both `-OR` ⇒ acetal ⇒ non-reducing. "Jyaada rocket scientist banne ki zaroorat nahi — hemi-acetal mil gaya to reducing, nahi mila to non-reducing." |
| Aldose vs ketose from the name | The NAME is the hint — "Aldo-" ⇒ `-CHO` on C1; "Keto-" ⇒ C=O on C2; "-pentose/-hexose" ⇒ chain length 5/6. "Naam mein hi hint diya hua hai." |
| L = up, R = down (Fischer → Haworth) | The **RD rule**: "Right is Down — RD karke yaad rakh lo. To obviously Left is Up." For a capital-D sugar, the terminal `CH2OH` is drawn UP. |
| Allose→Altrose→Glucose→Mannose→Galactose recall | The **four-fingers technique** (learned from another good teacher): all four fingers out = all four `-OH` right = **Allose**; one finger in = Altrose; second finger in = **Glucose**; top two in = Mannose; proper splay = Galactose. A way to physically remember the C2/C3/C4 `-OH` pattern. |
| Anomers | "Differ ONLY at the hemi-acetal carbon" — because the attacking `-OH` could come from front OR back. α/β are the two front/back attack products. |
| Epimers | "Differ at exactly ONE chiral centre that is NOT the hemi-acetal one." Glucose & Mannose (C2), Glucose & Galactose (C4). "Sirf ek hi centre pe differ karna chahiye — do jagah aa gaya to epimer nahi bol sakte." |
| α vs β anomer (which is which) | "`CH2OH` and the anomeric `-OH` TRANS ⇒ α; same side (cis) ⇒ β." β slightly more stable because its `-OH` sits equatorial (bigger group on equatorial = less repulsion). |
| Pyranose vs furanose | Pyranose = 6-membered ring (1 O + 5 C), from pyran; furanose = 5-membered ring, from furan. Glucopyranose, fructofuranose. |
| Why glucose can't digest grass (cellulose) | "Ghaas kha ke aa jaoge khet se to body digest nahi karegi" — humans have α-glucosidase (digests starch's α-link) but not the β-link enzyme; cow/goat/buffalo can. "Ek bond ka difference." |
| Invert sugar naming | Sucrose is dextro (+); on hydrolysis it gives (+)glucose +52.5 and (−)fructose −92.4 → net negative → rotation INVERTED from + to −. "Dextro thi, ab levo ho gayi — invert ho gayi." |
| Mutarotation question shortcut | Same as reducing-sugar: anything with a hemi-acetal can make α/β anomers ⇒ shows mutarotation. Sucrose (no hemi-acetal) does NOT. "Bas hemi-acetal dhoondo." |
| Pentaacetate ⇒ counts the `-OH` groups | "Kitne acetate group bane, utne hi `-OH` the." Glucose → penta-acetate ⇒ 5 `-OH`. Reduce `-CHO` to `-CH2OH` first → 6 `-OH` → hexa-acetate. A counting tool, not memorization. |
| n-hexane from glucose + HI | Strong reducing agent HI reduces EVERYTHING to the alkane — "thok mein kaam hoga, kisi ko exception nahi milegi." n-hexane (straight) ⇒ glucose chain is LINEAR, unbranched. |
| NCERT-line doctrine | "NCERT ko history ki tarah nahi, chemistry ki tarah padho. Har statement mein ek potential question chhupa hota hai." When reading any line, ask "isme kaunsa question chhupa hai?" |
| Where the non-NCERT PYQ hides | Seliwanoff/Molisch/Barfoed tests are NOT in the NCERT chapter — they live in the **Lab Manual, Unit 11** (Tests for carbohydrates, fats, proteins). "Jo NCERT mein nahi hota, wo Lab Manual mein hota hai." |
| Zwitterion / charge at pH (proteins, L2) | Track the two charged groups: at low pH (strongly acidic, ~2) `-COO⁻`→`-COOH`, leaving `-NH₃⁺` ⇒ net **+1**; near pH 6 (isoelectric) zwitterion ⇒ net **0**; high pH (~11) `-NH₃⁺`→`-NH₂`, leaving `-COO⁻` ⇒ net **−1**. "H⁺ bahut honge to COO⁻ ko COOH bana denge; OH⁻ bahut honge to NH₃⁺ se H⁺ kheench lenge." |
| Isoelectric point recipe | "α-COOH ki pKa aur α-NH₃⁺ ki pKa ka average lo — add karo, divide by 2." Use only the α-group pKa's (NOT the side-chain `-COOH`/`-NH₂`). Neutral amino acids ~6; acidic (extra `-COOH`) ~3-4; basic (extra N) >7 (arginine ~10+). |
| Peptide linkage / counting | Amino acids join by `-CO-NH-` (water lost). **n amino acids ⇒ (n−1) peptide linkages.** "7 amino acids jude to beech mein 6 peptide linkages." The end residues use only one group; the middle one ("bechaara") uses BOTH its `-NH₂` and `-COOH`. |
| Why a peptide chain folds (2°/3° structure) | The `-C=O` and `-N-H` of the backbone H-bond when the chain coils — "jab gol-gol ghoomega to ek CO ke saamne NH aa gaya, bond ban gaya." α-helix and β-pleated sheet = secondary; fibrous/globular = tertiary; arrangement of multiple chains = quaternary. |
| Denaturation | Heat/pH break the H-bonds → coils unfold → biological activity lost; **2°+3° destroyed, 1° intact** ("amino acid jude to hain hi"). Examples: **boiling egg white (coagulation)**, **curdling of milk** (lactic acid from bacteria). |
| Arginine vs lysine basicity | Both basic, but arginine has MORE nitrogen atoms in the side chain ⇒ more strongly basic ⇒ higher pKa/isoelectric point. "Lysine mein sirf ek NH₂ extra; arginine mein zyada nitrogen." |
| Vitamins — solubility & storage | Fat-soluble **A, D, E, K** stored in liver + adipose tissue; water-soluble **B-group + C** excreted in urine, must be taken regularly — **except B₁₂** which the body stores. "Body kya store karti hai? A-D-E-K aur B₁₂." (Stored longest: A and D.) |
| Vitamin etymology hook | Originally "vitamine" (vital amine) — the trailing 'e' dropped when later vitamins were found WITHOUT an amine group. |
| Nucleotide vs nucleoside | base + pentose sugar = **nucleoside**; nucleoside + phosphate = **nucleotide**. "-tide mein phosphate laga hota hai." Nucleic acid = polymer of nucleotides. |
| DNA base pairing & H-bonds | **A pairs with T (2 H-bonds); G pairs with C (3 H-bonds).** In RNA, thymine is replaced by **uracil** (A-U, G-C). DNA = double strand (chemical basis of heredity); RNA = single strand. The DNA double helix is held purely by **hydrogen bonding**. |
| Sugar in DNA vs RNA | RNA = β-D-ribose; DNA = β-D-2-deoxyribose ("deoxy = ek oxygen kam at C2"). |
| Phosphodiester linkage | Nucleotides join through a **phosphodiester linkage** between the **3'** and **5'** carbons of the pentose sugars. Base sits at C1', phosphate at C5'. |
| RNA types & proportions | **rRNA ~80%** (in ribosomes), **tRNA ~15%**, **mRNA ~5%**. DNA stores the code; RNA does the building. Transcription = copying DNA→RNA; Translation = RNA→protein. |
| Enzyme = lock-and-key bio-catalyst | Enzymes are proteins acting as biological catalysts with well-defined **active sites**; substrate locks in, reacts, product leaves. They work at an **optimum (body) temperature** — NOT high temperature — and CAN be poisoned. |

## B. Must-memorize lists & facts he teaches (use in shortcut / recall sections)

**Carbohydrates (L1):**
- Carbohydrate definition (memorize exactly): "optically active polyhydroxy aldehydes or ketones, OR compounds which produce such units on hydrolysis." NOT the old "hydrates of carbon" CₙH₂ₙOₙ.
- Glucose open-chain has **4 chiral carbons**; sucrose has **9 chiral carbons** (count manually).
- Optical rotations: α-glucose **+112**, β-glucose **+18.7**; equilibrium mix in water = **36% α / 64% β** (β more stable → majority).
- Invert-sugar rotations: glucose **+52.5**, fructose **−92.4** → net negative.
- Glucose commercial prep: hydrolysis of starch, acidic, **393 K, 2–3 atm** (memorize the conditions — a question can hang on them).
- Glucose lab prep: acidic hydrolysis of sucrose **in presence of alcohol** (fructose more soluble in alcohol; glucose crystallizes out). The "in presence of alcohol" phrase is itself a question-trap.
- Glucose oxidation map: Br₂-water (mild) → **gluconic acid** (1 −COOH, partial); HNO₃ (strong) → **glucaric/saccharic acid** (di-COOH, top+bottom). "Gluconic = mono, glucaric = di."
- Glucose does NOT give: 2,4-DNP test, Schiff's test, NaHSO₃ addition — because `-CHO` is locked in the ring (not free).
- ATP/ADP in photosynthesis per glucose: **18 each** (memory-based, homework-level).
- Disaccharide linkages (memorize WHICH carbons): Sucrose = C1(α-glucose)–C2(β-fructose); Maltose = C1–C4 (two glucose, α-link); Lactose = galactose + glucose; Cellulose = β-glucose C1–C4; Starch amylose = α C1–C4 (unbranched, water-soluble), amylopectin = α C1–C6 (branched, insoluble).
- Polysaccharide storage: **starch** = plant storage (α-glucose); **glycogen** = animal storage (liver + muscle, highly branched); **cellulose** = plant cell wall (β-glucose).
- Tests: Molisch = all carbohydrates of every class. Reducing property = Fehling's, Benedict's, Tollens'. Seliwanoff = distinguishes ketose (red, fast) from aldose (slow); Barfoed = distinguishes monosaccharide from disaccharide.

**Proteins / amino acids / nucleic acids / vitamins (L2):**
- 20 naturally occurring amino acids — the **R groups must be memorized** ("isme koi shortcut nahi de sakta"). General structure: central C with `-COOH`, `-NH₂`, `-H`, `-R`. Chiral unless R = H ⇒ **glycine is the only achiral / non-chiral natural amino acid.** Most natural amino acids are **L-configuration.**
- Acidic amino acids (2 only): **aspartic acid, glutamic acid** (extra `-COOH`) — hook: "naam ke aage 'acid' likha hai." Basic amino acids: lysine, arginine, histidine (extra N).
- His essential-amino-acid **mnemonic: "Lalit MP VV TH"** → Leucine, (Lysine), Arginine, Isoleucine, Threonine, Methionine, Phenylalanine, Valine, Tryptophan, Histidine. (He's self-deprecating: "main bahut bad hoon at banane mein — apna alag banao to bhi chalega.")
- Amino acid properties: colourless crystalline solids, water-soluble, high-melting, behave like salts (zwitterion polarity).
- Fibrous protein: polypeptide chains run **parallel**, held by **hydrogen + disulphide bonds**, insoluble in water (e.g. keratin in hair/wool/silk, myosin in muscle). Globular protein: chains **coil into spherical shape**, water-soluble (e.g. insulin, albumin).
- Forces stabilizing 2°/3° structure: hydrogen bonds, disulphide linkages, van der Waals, electrostatic forces of attraction.
- His enzyme **mnemonic-song** (offered, with permission to discard): Invertase (yeast): sucrose→glucose+fructose; Zymase (yeast): glucose→ethanol+CO₂; Diastase (malt): starch→maltose; Maltase (yeast): maltose→glucose.
- Lab-manual tests (Unit 11): **Molisch** (all carbohydrates; conc. H₂SO₄ + α/1-naphthol → deep violet ring at junction of two liquids); **Barfoed** (cupric acetate in acetic acid; monosaccharide → brick-red Cu₂O ppt — distinguishes mono from disaccharide); **Fehling/Benedict** (reducing sugar → orange-red Cu₂O; Fehling uses tartrate, Benedict uses citrate complex of Cu²⁺ — complexed to keep Cu²⁺ below Ksp so no Cu(OH)₂ ppt forms); **Seliwanoff** (ketose, red, fast); **acrolein test** (oils/fats/glycerol + KHSO₄ → acrolein, pungent smell); **starch + iodine → blue** (starch-iodine complex); **Biuret** (alkaline CuSO₄ + ≥2 peptide bonds → violet — needs ≥3 amino acids joined); **Ninhydrin** (proteins/amino acids/1°-amine/ammonia → blue-violet "Ruhemann's purple"); **Xanthoproteic** (aromatic amino acids/proteins + conc. HNO₃ → orange).

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for "watch out" sections)

- **Up/Down starts at C2, not C1:** "Bachcha galti kya karta hai? Pehle carbon ko bhi up/down kar deta hai — bhool jaata hai ki C1 to CHO tha, ab hemi-acetal ban gaya. Up/down second carbon se count karo." The C1 (anomeric) position is set by α/β, not by the Fischer L/R rule.
- **`CH2OH` on the wrong carbon (name-decode trap):** in pentafuranose/hexopyranose questions, students accept a structure with `CH2OH` ON the hemi-acetal carbon — "aisa to nahi hota tha; CH2OH hemi-acetal ke NEXT wali position pe hota hai." Also count carbons against the penta/hexa prefix — a 6-C structure labelled "pentose" is out.
- **Reducing sugar ≠ "one ring vs two rings":** "Aisa mat sochna ki ek ring hona chahiye, do ring." A disaccharide (lactose, maltose) CAN be reducing if it still carries a free hemi-acetal. Count the hemi-acetal, not the rings.
- **Gluconic vs glucaric mix-up:** "Gluconic acid di-carboxylic hai" is FALSE — gluconic is mono (Br₂-water, partial oxidation); glucaric/saccharic is di (HNO₃). And gluconic (a −COOH) can't form acetal — only the carbonyl could.
- **Sucrose shows mutarotation (FALSE):** sucrose has no hemi-acetal (both anomeric carbons locked in the glycosidic bond) ⇒ non-reducing ⇒ NO mutarotation. The other di/mono-saccharides do.
- **Which glucose/fructose form makes sucrose:** "α-D-glucose + β-D-fructose" — examiner swaps to "β-glucose + α-fructose" to trip you. Memorize C1(α-glucose)–C2(β-fructose).
- **Assertion-Reason on test wording:** Seliwanoff statements ("ketoses dehydrate very rapidly under acidic conditions to give furfural") get flipped — "very slowly" or "under basic conditions" — read EVERY word. Reason often: "β-elimination is required before dehydration to furfural" for aldoses (why aldoses are slow).
- **`-CHO` locked in ring:** glucose / glucose pentaacetate don't give Schiff / 2,4-DNP / NaHSO₃ because the aldehyde is consumed in the cyclic hemiacetal — "free CHO available nahi hai."
- **Disaccharide linkage carbons get forgotten mid-revision:** "Kiska C1–C2 tha, kiska C1–C4 tha — beech mein bhool jaoge." Drill each disaccharide's specific linkage carbons.
- **Isoelectric-point pKa selection (L2):** use ONLY the α-COOH and α-NH₃⁺ pKa's — "jo R group ke andar wala COOH/NH₂ hai uska nahi." Acidic/basic side chains shift the pI but you average the α-pair.
- **Net charge at a given pH (L2 PYQ):** don't forget phenolic `-OH` also ionizes at high pH. At pH 2 count `-NH₃⁺` (+); at pH 11 count `-COO⁻` and phenoxide (−). Take the ABSOLUTE value and ADD across the scenarios.
- **Enzymes at "very high temperature" (FALSE):** enzymes work at an optimum body temperature, NOT high temp; they CAN be poisoned; they are NOT necessarily heterogeneous. Only "well-defined active sites" is the correct statement.
- **Biuret needs ≥2 peptide bonds:** a single free amino acid (e.g. alanine) does NOT give Biuret — "kam se kam teen amino acids judne chahiye." Xanthoproteic/ninhydrin work on free amino acids too; Barfoed is a carbohydrate test, never for amino acids.
- **Fehling product is Cu₂O, not CuO:** "S2: gives CuO" is the false statement — it's **Cu₂O** (Cu goes +2→+1), orange-red/brick-red ppt.
- **Sucrose gives no Tollens / no reducing-sugar test:** no hemiacetal ⇒ non-reducing ⇒ negative Tollens/Fehling; glucose & fructose both positive.
- **Coagulation of egg = denaturation, NOT "secondary structure unchanged":** denaturation breaks 2°+3°, keeps 1° — so "secondary structure remains unchanged" is the wrong statement.
- **Histamine predominant form (PYQ trap):** human blood is slightly alkaline; only the MOST basic ring nitrogen gets protonated (the one whose lone pair is NOT tied up in aromaticity / not sp²-deactivated). Don't protonate the aromaticity-involved nitrogen.
- **Invert-sugar specific rotation is PER MOLE OF MIXTURE (PYQ trap):** sucrose → 1 mol glucose (+52.7) + 1 mol fructose (−92.4); the difference (~40 for 2 mol) must be HALVED to get per-mole-of-mixture (~19.8). "Yahin pe bachcha turant galti karega — bas difference le ke answer maan lega."
- **Transcription vs translation:** "RNA directs synthesis of specific protein" describes... read carefully — copying DNA→RNA is **transcription**; RNA→protein is **translation**. (He flags this as a tricky non-NCERT/info-based PYQ.)

## D. Per-question exemplars (L1) — opening → key move → closing

1. **Identify reducing vs non-reducing (3 structures, 30 s)** — Open: "Take 30 seconds." Key: hunt the hemi-acetal carbon (one `-OH` + one `-OR`); ignore ring count — even a two-ring disaccharide (lactose) can be reducing. Close: "Simple si cheez thi."
2. **β-aldopentofuranose (which structure, 1 min)** — Open: "Pehle naam ko decode karte hain — naam mein kya rakha hai." Key: furanose ⇒ 5-ring (all 4 are); aldo ⇒ `-CHO`; β ⇒ `CH2OH` and anomeric `-OH` same side; then eliminate on carbon count (penta = 5) and on `CH2OH`-misplaced-on-hemiacetal-carbon. Close: only one option survives — "itni si cheez decode kar pa rahe hain."
3. **Invert sugar — what & why named** — Open: "Direct NCERT paragraph, basic cheez." Key: sucrose (+, dextro) → glucose(+52.5) + fructose(−92.4) → net negative → rotation inverted +→−. Close: NCERT-quote framing; "isi liye invert sugar naam pada."
4. **Mutarotation (which shows it)** — Key: "Mutarotation pe simple dimaag nahi lagana — jisme hemi-acetal hai wahi α/β bana sakta hai." α-glucose +112 / β +18.7 equilibrate. Close: "Itna sa dhyaan rakhna hai, isse jyaada deep nahi jaana."
5. **Glucose reactions chain (HI, HCN, NH2OH, Br2-water, Ac2O, HNO3)** — Open: "Directly rat lo, dimaag nahi lagana." Key: HI → n-hexane (linear); Br₂-water (mild) → gluconic (1 −COOH); HNO₃ (strong) → glucaric (di-COOH); Ac₂O → penta-acetate ⇒ 5 `-OH`. Close: "Very simple, very frequently asked — ek baar dhyaan se dekh lo."
6. **X/Y/Z acetate equivalents (reduce then acetylate)** — Key: count `-OH` after each transform: cyclic glucose has 4 free `-OH` (X=4); Ni/H₂ opens `-CHO`→`-CH2OH` giving 6 `-OH` (Y=6); normal glucose penta-acetate (Z=5). "Number of `-OH` change kar do, corresponding acetate aa jaayega."
7. **Seliwanoff test (what colour, PYQ)** — Open: "NCERT mein nahi? To Lab Manual Unit 11." Key: distinguishes ketose/aldose; ketose dehydrates rapidly (acidic) → furfural → resorcinol → colour; keto-HEXose (fructose) → **red**. Close: assertion-reason framing ("β-elimination... ye dekho kaise question banta hai").
8. **Functional groups in maltose** — Key: structure recall — one carbon is acetal (`-OR`/`-OR`), the other hemi-acetal ⇒ maltose IS reducing. Answer: "one acetal + one hemi-acetal."
9. **Chiral centres in sucrose** — Key: count manually → **9**. "Structure yaad hoga to bata doge."
10. **Molisch test (which detected)** — Key: NCERT-line-style recall — "carbohydrates of ALL classes give Molisch." Not for nitro/amine/alcohol. Close: appends Fehling/Benedict/Tollens for reducing property.
11. **Methyl-D-galactopyranoside (which structure, 1 min)** — Key: "methyl" just names the `-OR` (OMe) at the old hemi-acetal position; D ⇒ `CH2OH` up; eliminate structures missing `CH2OH` or with it misplaced; then match galactose `-OH` pattern via RD (down-up-up). Close: "Aasan tha. Simple."
12. **Stereochemical relationships set (anomers/epimers/etc.)** — Key: where the difference sits decides the label — hemi-acetal carbon ⇒ anomers (or acetal ⇒ "anomers" when `-OR`); a single non-anomeric centre ⇒ epimers; functional-group swap (glucose/fructose) ⇒ functional isomers; chain-length CH₂ difference (glucose/ribose) ⇒ homologues.
13. **Correct pyranose (Haworth) for sugar X (30 s)** — Key: apply RD chant from C2 (skip C1, it closes the ring — "ye hemi-acetal banane mein chala gaya"); "up-down-down" must match. Close: warns against over-flipping.
14. **Incorrect statement about given glucose** — Key: Br₂-water gives only ONE −COOH (gluconic), not dicarboxylic ⇒ that statement is the wrong one; the rest (coexists with 2 cyclic anomers; no Schiff's despite `-CHO`; 4 asymmetric C) are all true.
15. **Correct statement re gluconic acid (30–40 s)** — Key: gluconic's `-CHO` is already oxidized to `-COOH`, so it can't form acetal/cyclic; gluconic = mono (partial oxidation), glucaric = di (HNO₃). Answer: "gluconic is a partial oxidation product."
16. **A→B via fermentation then haloform (PYQ)** — Open: "Biomolecules mein nahi milega — Alcohol chapter 11.5." Key: glucose/fructose --zymase--> ethanol (A = CH₃CH₂OH); haloform on CH₃CH(OH)- → CHI₃, leaving B with 1 carbon. Close: "NCERT se direct uthaya."
17. **Compound A: tetra-acetate + Br2-acid + HI→isopentane (40–45 s)** — Key: tetra-acetate ⇒ 4 `-OH`; Br₂-water reacts only with `-CHO` (eliminate ketose options); HI → **isopentane** ⇒ branched chain (not n-hexane). Close: "Wo information question ki form mein aa gayi."

## D2. Per-question exemplars (L2 — proteins / nucleic acids / vitamins) — opening → key move → closing

1. **Chiral carbons in a tripeptide Ile-Arg-Pro (1 min)** — Open: "Agar R-groups yaad hain to solve karoge, nahi to chupchaap answer dekh lo." Key: break each peptide bond; count the α-carbon (always chiral except glycine) PLUS any chiral centre inside the R group — Ile has one extra ⇒ total **4**. Close: "Structures yaad honge tabhi ye sab pooch sakte hain."
2. **Correct statement regarding enzymes (30 s)** — Open: "Bada basic sa question." Key: kill each wrong option (high-temp FALSE; heterogeneous FALSE; can't-be-poisoned FALSE); only "possess well-defined active sites" is correct.
3. **Detected by Molisch test (15 s)** — Key: Molisch = all sugars/carbohydrates only. Pick the carbohydrate option.
4. **Amino acid with two carboxyl groups (15 s)** — Key: "naam ke aage 'acid' likha hai" ⇒ aspartic / glutamic acid (the two acidic amino acids).
5. **Secondary structure = what pattern?** — Key: α-helix / β-pleated sheet (NOT sequence = that's primary; NOT globular/fibrous = that's tertiary).
6. **Water-soluble vitamin (15 s)** — Key: A,D,E,K fat-soluble; B,C water-soluble; pick the B/C member.
7. **Reason for DNA double helix** — Key: among electrostatic / vdW / dipole-dipole / H-bonding, the helix is held **purely by hydrogen bonding** (A-T 2, G-C 3). "Protein chain ki baat alag hai — wahan sab forces; DNA helix sirf H-bond."
8. **Heterocyclic base & phosphate ester positions** — Key: base at **C1'**, phosphate at **C5'** in both DNA and RNA. Direct answer.
9. **Glucose + Fehling: S1/S2 (statement type)** — Key: S1 (reddish-brown Cu₂O ppt, glucose is reducing) TRUE; S2 ("gives CuO") FALSE — it's **Cu₂O**.
10. **Correct sequence of amino acids in given tripeptide** — Key: break the two peptide bonds; decode each R group — isopropyl ⇒ **valine**, `-CH₂OH` ⇒ **serine**, `-CH(OH)CH₃` ⇒ **threonine**.
11. **Increasing order of pKa (4 amino acids)** — Key: higher pKa = more basic; basic amino acids (lysine, arginine) highest, **arginine > lysine** (more N); neutral (glycine) middle; acidic (aspartic) lowest.
12. **Predominant form of histamine in human blood** — Key: blood slightly alkaline ⇒ protonate only the most basic ring N (lone pair free, not aromaticity-bound / not sp²-deactivated). Flagged "definitely tricky, info we may not know — not in NCERT."
13. **Positive Tollens test pair** — Key: reducing sugars only; sucrose & acetophenone (ketone) are out; glucose/fructose & aldehydes give it.
14. **Net charge of peptide at pH 2, 6, 11 (IIT Advanced PYQ)** — Key: same as isoelectric reasoning — pH 2 ⇒ +2 (|Z₁|=2), pH 6 ⇒ 0 (zwitterion), pH 11 ⇒ −3 (two `-COO⁻` + phenoxide), |Z₃|=3 ⇒ sum **5**. Close: "Isoelectric wala part padha hai to tough nahi; warna figure out karna tricky."
15. **Specific rotation of invert sugar (30 s)** — Key: glucose +52.7, fructose −92.4 → difference ~40 for **2 mol**; HALVE for per-mole-of-mixture (~19.8). "Yahin bachcha turant galti karega."
16. **Max RNA found in (30 s)** — Key: rRNA ~80% ⇒ found in ribosomes. Answer: ribosomes.
17. **A & B carbohydrates / C protein from test grid** — Key: Molisch+ ⇒ A,B carbohydrates; Barfoed− on A ⇒ A not monosaccharide (rule out glucose); Biuret+ only on C ⇒ C is protein (albumin), alanine ruled out (single amino acid, no peptide bond).
18. **Number of peptide linkages on hydrolysis (3 Gly + 2 Leu + 2 Val, 30 s)** — Key: 7 amino acids ⇒ **6 linkages** (n−1). "Kaunse the, usse mujhe kya matlab — jab 7 judenge to beech mein 6."
19. **Vitamins stored in body / stored longest** — Key: A,D,E,K + B₁₂ are stored (total **5**); stored relatively longest = **A and D**.
