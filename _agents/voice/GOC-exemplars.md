## A2. Narration frameworks (his reusable multi-step moves)

### 1. The order-question spine (any stability/acidity/basicity ORDER)

1. State the governing principle in one line (unstable base = strong base; heat of hydrogenation ∝ 1/stability; pKa ∝ 1/acidity; "conjugate acid banao, acidity order likho").
2. **Convert every inverted scale to the direct scale out loud, first** (hydride affinity → carbocation stability; pKb → acidity; PE → stability: "decreasing PE ka matlab increasing stability"). A stated safety move.
3. Delete the common part — "Jo common part hota hai usko compare nahi karna. Ignore kar do, cancel out ho jayega."
4. Find the MOST and LEAST extreme first, eliminate options — "Most aur least agar identify kar lo to majority of trends waale questions mein badi aasaani se answer mil jaata hai. Baaki ko solve karne ki zaroorat padi hi nahi."
5. Only then fine-compare the close pair — switch to neighbourhood/surroundings, or declare honestly: "Ye itne close hain, on-the-spot predict karna possible nahi hota. Elimination method se chalna padega."
6. Anti-silly-mistake ritual for base orders: "Sabse pehle acidity order inke conjugates ke likh dete hain. Is tareeke se galti hone ka scope nahi rehta."

### 2. The who-is-more-stable escalation ladder (intermediates)

When one effect ties, climb to the next: (1) spot what's COMMON and cancel it ("benzene common hai — cancel out"); (2) inductive — if distance kills it ("teen-chaar carbon ke baad negligible") or it ties, drop it; (3) resonance / **octet completion** ("octet completion is a very important factor" — it beats electronegativity); (4) hyperconjugation by alpha-H count, counted out loud ("3... 3 aur 3 chhe... 3-6-9"); (5) if theory ties or contradicts, invoke DATA — "hydride affinity values se exact pata lagta hai. Data beats everything — always go for data" (cites Jerry March / Carey). Resonance > hyperconjugation ≈ inductive, EXCEPT halogens (inductive > mesomeric). Resonance-structure stability rules applied IN SEQUENCE with stop-condition: (1) octet completion, (2) more π bonds ("saare bond mat count karne lag jaana — sigma to change honge hi nahi"), (3) less charge separation, (4) charge on the right atom — "Agar isi pe answer aa gaya to aage kuch dekhne ki zaroorat nahi." First find the most unstable: "Sabse pehle sabse unstable ko dhoondo — jahan octet complete nahi."

### 3. The aromaticity gate-chant

Kitne π electrons?"** → 4n+2 aromatic / 4n anti-aromatic / fails a gate ⇒ non-aromatic. Conjugation detector: "Charge, phir single bond, phir CHARGE" — charge defined broadly: "It can be positive, negative, a lone pair, OR a pi bond — sigma ke electrons ko chhod ke, baaki sab charge." Gate details he voices: planarity is information, not appearance ("padh ke pata lagega"); pyridine-type lone pair NOT counted ("already conjugation khud poori hai — is lone pair ki zaroorat hi nahi"); furan-O contributes ONE lone pair; anion counts as 2 π electrons; sp³ centre breaks the chain ("teeno p kharch ho gaye"). When π-count by sight feels illegal: "Shifting karwa do, dusra resonating structure banao, tab clearly dikhega."

### 4. Arrow-pushing law + resonance-counting pedagogy

- **The one law:** "Arrow ALWAYS starts from high electron density to low electron density." Hard corollary: "Kabhi bhi positive charge ki taraf SE arrow start mat karna — wahan toh hai hi kuchh nahi, move karoge kya?" Full-head arrow = pair; fish-hook = single electron (radicals).
- **The myth he kills first:** "Bagal mein positive hai toh negative ko shift karwa dete hain — NO. A formal positive charge is IRRELEVANT." Real test = EMPTY ORBITAL + octet not exceeded. His triple example: sp² C⁺ (positive, allowed), neutral BF₃-type boron (no positive, still allowed), N⁺ next to O⁻ (positive, NOT allowed — five bonds).
- **Three validity gates:** (1) no octet expansion for 2nd-period atoms ("S ka kar sakte ho easily; N/O/C ka NAHI"); (2) net charge conserved ("we are ONLY allowed to move electrons — create or destroy a charge NAHI"); (3) participating orbitals coplanar/parallel — pyridine's lone pair is in-plane ⇒ does not resonate ("perpendicular hain toh overlap kaise karenge?").
- **Counting discipline:** move ONE step at a time, place charge on the immediately-conjugated atom first — "beech wala structure miss ho gaya" directly costs marks (JEE Advanced has asked the count). Generate structure 3 from structures 1–2, don't reinvent ("bas iss structure pe SAARI possibilities dekho"). Carbonyl π always moves toward O. At a chauraha, draw BOTH branches (he changes pen colour per branch). No-repeat check: two near-identical benzenes with shifted double-bond positions COUNT as distinct. And: "Koi formula hai kitne banenge? NAHI — properly karna padega."
- **Resonance honesty preamble:** "Resonance ek hypothetical concept hai. It exists only on paper... Teeno sahi hain, par koi accurate nahi." (Used to kill equilibrium-sign options: "Are, they are not structures at all! That is resonance.")

### 5. Carbocation rearrangement narration

1. **The WHY:** "Jab bhi carbocation rearrange karta hai — it wants to become a MORE STABLE carbocation." (Cation's own ambition: it "sees" a better version of itself.)
2. **1,2-hydride shift chant:** "Number the carbons 1 and 2. Us 2-wale carbon ka ek hydrogen, uske bond ke saath — matlab H NEGATIVE ki form mein — tod ke yahan shift karwa do. Isko bolte hain 1,2-hydride shift."
3. **Migratory aptitude as common sense:** shift H over methyl "kyunki methyl shift kar diya toh ye carbon khud 3° nahi bacha." Smaller migrates first (H > Me > iPr > tBu) BUT "iska koi ABSOLUTE order nahi hota" — never oversell the rule.
4. **No 1,3/1,4 shifts:** "Otherwise hoti toh door koi possibility dikhe toh seedha wahan shift kar dete. Bade rare cases mein hoti hai."
5. **Ring expansion fires** only when + lands on the first carbon just outside the ring; strain = pocket-money dukh; 4→5 beats 5→6 (flight upgrade).
6. **The exception:** cyclopropylmethyl cation is exceptionally stable — DON'T expand it; its three forms are RESONATING structures, not a rearrangement. "Dancing resonance" debunked: "aisa koi term poori organic chemistry literature mein hai NAHI."
7. **Multiple cations ⇒ mixture:** enumerate every possible shift, grade each (bakwas / good but not the best / the best), most stable gives major product; tie-break by alpha-H count.
8. **Closing meta-lesson (reusable opener for mechanism solutions):** "Organic mein jitni mechanisms ki practice karoge, ek COMMON SENSE develop hoti hai... likh-likh ke practice karne se INTUITION develop ho jaati hai. You can actually think about a mechanism you've never read before." Plus the step-1 doctrine: "First step hi deciding point hai. Once you start, phir aage-aage dikhta jaata hai."

1. **Double-flip protocol** (compare two drawn 3D structures): flip vertically then horizontally, tracking ONE bond's front/back fate aloud — "Jab isko maine aise palta, jo front mein tha wo back mein chala gaya... Horizontal flip karoge toh upar ki cheez upar rahegi — sirf left-right ka difference aayega." Always offer R/S naming as the error-proof alternative.
2. **Sense-of-rotation matching** (Newman/wedge identity): "Pehle connectivity check karo. Phir is carbon pe clockwise chalo — CH₃, Cl, H. Udhar bhi? Rotation ka sense match → identical. Ek match kare, ek na kare → not identical" (= diastereomer).
3. **Rotation-is-free doctrine:** "Kisi bhi object ko rotate karne se it doesn't change — jaise main ghoom jaoon, main abhi bhi wahi rahoonga." Kills double-counting (teenon-up vs teenon-down; R-cis-S vs S-cis-R).
4. **Fischer → Newman junction trick:** "Upar wale junction ko as-it-is saamne bana do; neeche wale ke horizontal groups ki adla-badli kar do — left wale ko right, right wale ko left."
5. **The V-flattening move** (E2 product geometry): after HX leaves, "ye jo V shape hai na — isko bas horizontal kar dena hai, flat. Tetrahedral tha, trigonal planar mein aa jayega. Front wale CH₃ ke exactly peechhe kya aa raha hai? Wo wala Ph. Toh trans."
6. **Exchange-counting engine:** even number of exchanges = same compound, odd = mirror image; both exchanges on the SAME chiral centre ("ye mat karna ki ek upar wale pe, ek neeche wale pe").
7. **Implicit-H-at-back shortcut:** "Fourth bond draw karne ki zaroorat nahi. Wo H hoga aur back side mein hi hoga, kyonki baaki teen mein se koi back mein nahi. Toh use bhool jao" — go straight to 1→2→3.
8. **Live props, always:** ball-and-stick for rotation/non-superimposition ("blue-blue match, par red aur green nahi — kaise bhi ghuma lo"); mobile phone = ring plane; pen = 180° flip; his own body = conformations; an improvised model when needed ("pause karke dekhte hain... yes, I found something").
9. **Plane-cutting narration (POS/COS hunt):** physical — "Isko beech mein se cut karo — idhar white, idhar blue. Symmetry aayi? Nahin." COS as line-extension test: "ek direction mein CH₃, wahi line doosri direction — CH₃. Peechhe H, aage Cl — match nahi kiya, toh centre of symmetry nahi."
10. **Perpendicular-planes lemma (allene/atropisomer):** "Do double bond jab ikatthe hote hain, they are mutually perpendicular." Odd count → ends coplanar → one long C=C. Biphenyl: "ek benzene is plane mein, doosri perpendicular — taaki ortho groups aamne-saamne na hon."
11. **Fixed-unit collapse (complex PYQs):** freeze everything drawn ("configuration is fixed — us pure part ko ek single unit ki tarah consider karo"), vary only wavy bonds; shrink rings for convenience ("five-member ki jagah four-member le lo, koi fark nahi padta").
12. **sp²-in-plane test:** substituent on an sp² ring carbon lies IN the ring plane (phone demo) — can't be up/down vs an sp³ substituent.

Supporting chants: **R/S** — priorities by atomic number → least priority to back (or one exchange + invert the answer) → walk 1→2→3 → clockwise R / anticlockwise S; Fischer shortcut: least priority horizontal ⇒ flip, vertical ⇒ keep. **D/L checklist** — vertical chain, most-oxidized carbon top, lowest chiral centre decides; "is D ka dextro–levo se koi relation nahi hai."

### 7. The IUPAC naming walkthrough (identical every time)

Identify main FG → pick the main chain THROUGH it ("functional group is the priority — FG hi main chain mein nahi hai to phir kis baat ki main chain?") → number from the FG end, then COVER unsaturation 1→2 ("ye nahi ki start kar diya aur wapas chale gaye"), then lowest locants, then alphabetical tiebreak → list branches alphabetically with locants → assemble aloud, naming each slot: "2° prefix (branches, alphabetical) → 1° prefix (cyclo) → word root → 1° suffix (ane/ene/yne) → 2° suffix (main FG)." Unwritten locant = 1. Mistake-prone cases taught by writing the WRONG name first and asking "Right? ... WRONG na?" Complex branch: parenthesis + primed numbering (1′, 2′...). N-chains: "ye chains nitrogen se judi hain — to nitrogen hi inki position hai. N-ethyl-N-methyl." Lecture opener move: name the student's resistance ("lamba topic hai, boring hai...") then reframe — "hum us boring tareeke se NAHI padhenge... to-the-point, crisp aur interesting" — with the stakes: paper gives the NAME, not the structure.

### 8. The acidity lens (fixed move)

Every acidity comparison routes through the conjugate base: "H⁺ nikaalo → jo negative charge aaya, wo kitna stabilize ho raha hai?" Numbers-first teaching: "Just saying this is more acidic than that — that is one thing. Par MORE ka matlab kitna more? Slightly ya bahut zyada?" pKa values → "calculated guesswork" in exams.

## B. Must-memorize bank (his ratta-licensed list)

### Electron effects & resonance

- **−I order:** NR₃⁺ > SR₂⁺ > NH₃⁺ > NO₂ > CN > COOH > F > Cl > Br > I. Anti-ratta framing: "Not exactly poora order ratt ke chale jao — ek idea rahega." Rule of thumb: "Jab bhi nitrogen pe positive charge dikhe, samajh jao bahut strong −I group hai." **+I order:** O⁻ > COO⁻ > CR₃ > ...
- **THE one-line master rule:** "Charge dispersal leads to stability — poore organic mein ye fundamental hai."
- Inductive range: "teen-chaar carbon ke baad bilkul negligible." Inductive = "partial AND permanent."
- Hyperconjugation needs alpha-H on an **sp³** carbon adjacent to C=C / C⁺ / radical. Overlaps (JEE Adv asked): cation σ(C–H)→empty 2p; alkene σ(C–H)→π*.
- Alkene stability on alpha-H tie: gem-disubstituted > trans > cis ("experimental data dekh ke").
- Strength: resonance > hyperconjugation ≈ inductive; halogens are the exception (−I > +M ⇒ net weakly withdrawing). Halogen +M order F > Cl > Br (2p–2p overlap).
- +M: N > O ("zyada aasaani se kaun dega? Nitrogen"). Mesomeric by sight, not list: lone pair on the attached atom ⇒ +M; C=O/C≡N attached ⇒ −M. Read the attachment atom (OCOCH₃ +M vs COOCH₃ −M).
- Resonance energies: benzene 36 kcal; naphthalene 61 (per ring 30.5); phenanthrene > anthracene. **Per-ring vs total:** "total resonance energy poochh rahe hain ya per ring — ye cheez badi important hai."
- Hybrid energy < every RS; more stable RS contributes more (equal RS ⇒ equal shares: carbonate 1/3 each).
- Heat of hydrogenation ∝ 1/stability. Benzene data story: 120 / 232 (discount 8) / 208 (discount 152) ⇒ MO housefull, not just resonance.
- Electromeric/inductomeric = skip-weight: "Pichhle 25-30-40 saalon mein mushkil se ek ya do baar PYQ — zyada mehnat ki aavashyakta nahi."
- Resonance ground rules: carbon never exceeds 4 bonds; "atoms ko move karna allowed hi nahi hai — sirf electrons move hote hain."

### Aromaticity & intermediates

- Hückel procedure: three gates FIRST (cyclic, planar, continuous conjugation), THEN 4n+2 / 4n count. Stability: aromatic > non-aromatic > anti-aromatic.
- Annulene facts: [10] NON-planar ⇒ non-aromatic; [14] planar ⇒ aromatic; [16] anti; [18] aromatic. Pyrene: count periphery only (14 π) — "Hückel rule single ring ke liye define kiya gaya tha."
- Anti-aromatic resonance ban: never draw benzene-style equalizing resonance for cyclobutadiene ("ye lengths barabar nahi hain — that causes destabilization").
- Structures you simply must know: furan/thiophene/pyridine/pyrrole, cysteine-has-sulfur, tropolone, tropylium, annulenes. "Jo cheezein yaad karne ki hain wo yaad karne ki hain, aur wo important hain."
- Carbocation ladder: trityl > diphenylmethyl > **3° > benzyl** (data beats logic — hydride affinity) > allyl > 2° > 1° > vinyl/phenyl ("bahut bakwas"). Allylic must be sub-classified 1°/2°/3°; 3° allylic > plain 3°. Aromatic cations exceptionally stable when the + ITSELF creates aromaticity (test: neutralize it — does aromaticity change?). Cyclopropylmethyl > tropylium (bent-orbital overlap).
- Carbenium vs carbonium: CH₃⁺ = carbEnium; carbOnium = CH₅⁺ (3c-2e) — "majority of books ne galti ye ki hai."
- Carbanion: EWG stabilize ⇒ CH₃⁻ > 1° > 2° > 3°; sp³ pyramidal; CCl₃⁻ > CF₃⁻ (Cl d-orbital back-bonding).
- Radical: neutral — both EDG and EWG stabilize; CH₃• planar, CF₃• pyramidal; **allylic radical > benzylic and 3°** ("ye point yaad rakhna padega — confirmed from data"). Generation: photolysis/thermolysis/redox; acetone-type → 2R• + CO; azo → 2R• + N₂. O₂ = radical inhibitor.
- Carbene: neutral, ELECTROPHILE ("lone pair hai, nucleophile hoga — no! Octet incomplete"). From CH₂N₂ + Δ/hν; CHCl₃ + base.
- Why carbocations get the most class time: "ek question se kaafi saare target poore ho jaate hain."

### Acidity & basicity

- **The starred pKa table ("reference points for calculated guesswork"):** HF 3.2; benzoic 4.2 ("420 hai ekdam"); acetic 4.76; H₂CO₃ 6.35 ("important — phenol se zyada acidic"); NH₄⁺ 9.2; phenol ≈ 10; water 15.7; ethanol 16; acetone 19.2 (ketone alpha-H ≈ 20); acetylene / ester alpha-H ≈ 25; H₂ 35; NH₃ 38; ethene 44; ethane 50. Carboxylic range ≈ 4–5. "Maine list de rakhi hai — yaad rakha karo, kaafi jagah comparison mein help karegi." 1 pKa unit = a BIG difference.
- Acidity framework (HA): bond strength, electronegativity of A, conjugate-base stabilization, solvent, H-bonding. Across period: EN dominates; down group: size; s-character: sp > sp² > sp³.
- NaHCO₃ effervescence threshold: carboxylic-range pKa or stronger (sulfonic yes, picric yes, nitrophenols pass); plain phenol fails. Starred-carbon detail: the CO₂ comes from the bicarbonate, not the COOH.
- Ortho effect: ANY ortho group on benzoic acid raises acidity; bulkier = stronger; two ortho better. Scope: benzoic acids only. Basicity variant: ortho-substituted anilines are weaker bases than aniline.
- Benzoic-derivative master summary: for CH₃/C₂H₅/F/Cl/Br/I/OH/OCH₃/Ph → **ortho > meta > para**; ONLY exceptions CN and NO₂ → **ortho > para > meta**. "Bas ye yaad kar liya to saare derivatives yahin solve ho gaye."
- Nitrophenols: para > ortho > meta (ortho's intramolecular H-bond costs effort); mesomeric does NOT weaken with distance, inductive does; −M doesn't operate at meta. Cresols: meta-cresol most acidic. Ortho-halophenols: Br > F (F's +M cancels its −I).
- Amine basicity (aqueous): **methylamines 2 > 1 > 3 > NH₃ ("213"); ethylamines 2 > 3 > 1 ("231")** — 2° on top in both. Gas phase 3° > 2° > 1°. Cyclic > acyclic of same degree. Hybridization: sp³ > sp² > sp. Enhancers: negative charge, small size, donating groups.
- Pyridine's lone pair: in-plane, perpendicular to π, NOT in resonance ⇒ available ⇒ why pyridine is everyone's base.
- Bridgehead rule (rings < 8 atoms total): no C=C / C⁺ / lone-pair donation at a bridgehead.
- Equilibrium direction: toward the weaker acid AND weaker base — "jis direction mein acid bhi weak ban raha hai aur base bhi weak, wahi direction hogi."
- Aromatic conjugate base ⇒ huge acidity boost (cyclopentadiene); symmetric flip: an aromaticity lone pair will NOT be donated ("aromaticity ka loss is a huge loss").

### Resonance drawing & rearrangement (specials)

- Arrow law + three validity gates + carbonyl-toward-O rule (see A2 §4).
- No 1,3/1,4 shifts; migratory aptitude smaller-first with no absolute order; cyclopropylmethyl exception (see A2 §5).
- CH₂N₂-type unfamiliar molecules: build by valence-electron audit (16 electrons), never memorize.

### Stereochemistry

- DoU: nC+1, −H, +X, −N, ÷2; O/S don't affect it; never fractional or negative ("agar hai, matlab compound exist nahi karega").
- No formula for counting isomers — "Likh ke, draw karke karna padta hai."
- Tautomer pairs: keto–enol, nitroso–oxime, imine–enamine, nitro–aci-nitro. "Yaad kar lo, likh lo ek baar achhe se."
- Enol % ladder: acetylacetone ≈ 76%; one ester ≈ 8%; both esters ≈ 10⁻³ ("ester ke saath enol nahi banta"); both Ph → 100%. Enol stabilizers: H-bonding, conjugation, aromaticity, hyperconjugation.
- GI counting: dissimilar ends 2ⁿ; similar even n → 2^(n−1) + 2^(n/2−1); similar odd n → 2^(n−1) + 2^((n−1)/2). 2ⁿ stereoisomer formula: symmetry check FIRST.
- Fischer absolutes: never flip in/out of plane; in-plane 180° allowed; **never check a Fischer for centre of symmetry** — plane only; even exchanges = same, odd = mirror, same centre only.
- R/S chant + Fischer horizontal-flip shortcut; D/L = relative to glyceraldehyde (see A2 §6).
- Specific rotation α = θ/(l·c), l in **decimeter**, c in g/mL; α constant — θ compensates. Enantiomers: equal and opposite.
- Decalins: memorize — linear = trans (more stable); 90°-twist = cis. "Paper mein on-the-spot draw karke compare nahi karoge."
- E2: leaving group and H **anti-coplanar**; Fischer → Newman first.
- Allenes: even C=C → optical; odd → geometrical; both ends need two different groups. Atropisomers: tetra-ortho biphenyls, each ring's ortho pair must differ.

### Purification & analysis (DPP 4–5 — reusable for POC)

- **RF = component distance ÷ solvent distance** — unitless, NOT integral, NOT constant across solvents.
- Steam distillation: steam volatile + **IMmiscible** with water. Simple distillation: b.p. gap > 20°. Vacuum distillation: decomposes before normal b.p. (glycerol — NCERT line).
- Paper chromatography: "water present in the pores of the paper forms the stationary phase — exact line hai ye NCERT mein."
- TLC and column are both adsorption chromatography ⇒ TLC order transfers to elution order. TLC spotting: iodine, UV, reagent spray (ninhydrin). Long column ⇒ near-identical components.
- Fixed pairings: chloroform+aniline → simple; aniline+water → steam; crude oil → fractional; naphthalene+NaCl → sublimation ("NaCl kabhi sublime nahi hota"); benzoic acid+naphthalene → crystallization. Purity check = sharp melting point.
- Detection: blood-red (Lassaigne) = ferric thiocyanate ⇒ N AND S; N confirmed only via CYANIDE ⇒ needs carbon; Prussian blue = N test, Fe³⁺/FeCl₃ pathway. Kjeldahl: K₂SO₄ = b.p. elevator, CuSO₄ = catalyst; FAILS for nitro, azo, N-in-ring (except spirol/indole/skatol), diazonium.
- Estimation skeletons: %S → mol S = mol S in BaSO₄ (233); %N Dumas → use N₂ (28), STP via P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂, subtract aqueous tension over water; %N Kjeldahl → 1 N : 1 NH₃, 2 NH₃ : 1 H₂SO₄.

### IUPAC & basics

- Name-assembly: 2° prefix → 1° prefix (cyclo) → word root → 1° suffix → 2° suffix. FG priority: COOH > ester > acid halide > amide > nitrile > aldehyde > ketone > alcohol > amine. Prefix forms: carboxy / alkoxycarbonyl / halocarbonyl / carbamoyl / cyano / formyl–oxo / hydroxy / amino.
- The carb- family rule: FG carbon NOT counted in root ⇒ -carbaldehyde / -carbonitrile / -carboxylic acid / -carboxamide; counted ⇒ -al / -nitrile / -oic acid / -amide. CHO = chain-terminating (formyl outside chain); ketone = oxo inside.
- Branch vocabulary: iso- stub fixed ("minimum propyl to hota hi hai"); sec-butyl; phenyl vs benzyl; vinyl vs allyl. Licensed trivial names: acetamide, acetophenone, benzophenone, benzaldehyde, methyl benzoate, phenyl acetate, cumene, phthalic acid, α-/β-naphthol.
- Numbering order: main FG → unsaturation → lowest locants → alphabetical. Double vs triple: lowest locant FIRST; only on a perfect tie does double win ("yahan bachche galti karte hain"). Ring with main FG: that carbon = C1; start from the more alkylated alkene carbon.
- Classification ladder: acyclic/cyclic → alicyclic/aromatic → homo/heterocyclic → benzenoid/non → spiro/fused/bridged/isolated. "Hydrocarbon" = C and H ONLY.
- Formal charge: no formula — compare visible electrons vs normal valency. Valence-count principle: "C⁻ ke paas paanch electrons — wo wahi bonding arrangements dega jo nitrogen neutral form mein deta hai."
- Hybridization quick count: σ + FREE lone pairs (free = not in resonance). Carboxylic OH oxygen = sp². **Melamine (PYQ): all SIX N sp².**
- Dipole stars: CH₃Cl > CH₃F (μ = q×d); NH₃ 1.47 vs NF₃ 0.2; CH₃Cl > CH₂Cl₂; BF₃/CO₂/CCl₄ zero. Polarizability: C–I beats C–Cl in SN2 despite lower polarity. Bond length: C(sp³)–H > C(sp²)–H > C(sp)–H. Boiling-point ladder: alkane < ether (no ether–ether H-bond) < aldehyde/nitrile < alcohol < water < carboxylic acid; intramolecular H-bond (o-nitrophenol) ⇒ LOWER b.p.

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️ sections)

### Effects, resonance & intermediates

- **Positive charge ≠ electrophile:** "Kya har positively charged ion electrophile hoga? NH₄⁺ pe bhi to positive charge hai — par nitrogen already chaar bond bana chuka hai. This is NOT an electrophile." Test = can it ACCEPT a pair (empty orbital / breakable π). Mirrors: "Sir carbon ke to already chaar bond hain — kaise accept karega?" → "kyonki wo pi bond toot sakta hai." And: whole molecule ≠ electrophile — carbonyl has BOTH centres.
- **Octet beats electronegativity (THE GOC trap):** "Bachche bolte hain — sir oxygen pe positive charge nahi aana chahiye. Par yahan carbon ke paas sirf 6 electrons hain. Octet completion sabse badi priority hai — uske badle oxygen pe positive aa gaya to koi baat nahi." Counter-check: O⁺ with 6 electrons is WORSE than C⁺ ("paagal sa ho jaata hai").
- **Arrow-pushing bans:** never move π toward a negative charge; never move FROM a positive charge; never move atoms or σ electrons; charge conserved; octet-exceed check before delocalizing onto 4-bonded N/O.
- **"Positive bagal mein hai toh lone pair shift" — FALSE:** formal + is irrelevant; need empty orbital + octet room (N⁺ next to O⁻ can't accept).
- **Wrong-way carbonyl push** (+ on O) — highly unstable; always push toward O.
- **Skipping the intermediate structure when counting RS** — directly loses the JEE numerical-value mark. One step at a time.
- **Two double-bond-shifted benzene structures treated as identical** — the positions differ ⇒ distinct structures.
- **Conjugation continued past an sp³ carbon** — "delocalize hi nahi hua to stability kaise milegi?"
- **Hyperconjugation eligibility:** alpha-H must sit on an sp³ carbon — vinyl alpha-H rejected ("ye waala sp2 hai"); tert-butyl-flanked cation has alpha-C but no H ("Aap bologe sir 3 × 3 = 9. Are nahi bhai! No alpha hydrogen — jaldbaazi mein bachcha galti karta hai"); alpha-O doesn't count.
- **Tert-butyl bait:** "Ekdam se answer dikhta hai — sir, tert-butyl itna achchha donating group! Par positive charge se itna door hai ki inductive negligible. Alkyl mesomeric show nahi karte. Funda hyperconjugation ka hai — mostly isme bachche khataak karke galat answer dete hain."
- **σ→π\* next to a carbocation denied too fast:** "Chaliye iska resonating structure draw karke dekh lete hain" — draw the RS before denying an overlap.
- **More resonating structures ≠ more stable** (phenol vs acetic acid): "Wo structures utne STABLE bhi hone chahiye. Phenoxide ke zyadatar mein negative charge CARBON pe aa raha hai. Acetate ke sirf do hain, par dono mein negative do ALAG-ALAG oxygen pe." Sub-trap: "Phenoxide mein bhi do baar oxygen pe hai? Wo dono SAME oxygen hain na."
- **Mesomeric ≠ benzene-only:** "Bachche sochte hain mesomeric sirf benzene aur uske rishtedaar tak limited hai. Jahan conjugation hai wahan lag jaayega."
- **"Conjugation = stability" reflex:** "Conjugation se kabhi-kabhi ulta stability ghat bhi jaati hai. Anti-aromatic mein yahi to hota hai."
- **Carbonyl beside a carbocation:** very destabilizing (adjacent ++) — "kahin bhi carbonyl carbocation ke agal-bagal laga ho, that's a very destabilizing group."
- **PE vs stability inversion:** convert before ordering — "decreasing PE ka matlab increasing stability."
- **Benzyl vs phenyl confusion:** opposite ends of the ladder. "Allylic is stable" without degree: always specify 1°/2°/3°.
- **CF₃⁻ "should be most stable":** CCl₃⁻ wins by Cl d-orbital back-bonding ("fluorine ke paas koi empty d orbital hai hi nahi").
- **Radical treated as charged:** "Bhai sahab, ye charged hai hi nahi!" Both EDG and EWG stabilize.
- **Carbene looks like a nucleophile:** lone pair visible, but octet-incomplete ⇒ electrophile.
- **Negative charge ≠ pair of electrons:** "Pair mein se ek iska apna tha, ek bahar se liya. Yahi chhoti-chhoti galtiyaan hain jinse organic kharab hoti hai."
- **Rearrangement:** shifting methyl when H is available (drops the carbon's degree); inventing 1,3/1,4 shifts; ring-expanding cyclopropylmethyl.

### Aromaticity

- **Pyridine-type lone pair counted in π:** "Galti se isko count mat kar lena — already conjugation khud poori hai." (Counting it flips the verdict.)
- **Aromaticity lone pair vs resonance lone pair (basicity):** "Aromaticity waale ko nikaal doge to structure anti-aromatic ho jaayega — bahut galat. ### Acidity & basicity

- **Ortho-effect scope:** "Benzoic mein! Cyclohexane ring pe wo concept mat laga lena." Basicity flip: "Ye to donating group hai, basicity badhaani chahiye — par ye ANILINE hai, ortho effect lagega."
