## B. Must-memorize items he licenses (use in ⚡/Shortcut sections)

- **pKa anchors** (his acidity number-line): water ≈ 15.8 · alcohol ≈ 16–17 · **phenol ≈ 10** ·
  terminal alkyne ≈ 25 · H₂ ≈ 35 · alkane ≈ 45. "Phenol ka 10 — normal alcohol ke
  comparison mein that is extremely acidic."
- **Victor Meyer colours** — 1° red, 2° blue, 3° colourless ("reaction last step tak
  pahunch hi nahi paata"). "Ek cheez to aap directly ratt sakte ho."
- **Oxo process / industrial methanol** (CO + H₂, ZnO–Cr₂O₃) — "yaad hi karna hai, koi
  dimaag nahi lagana ismein."
- **Lab prep of phenol** (aniline → diazonium 0–5°C → H₂O/Cu²⁺) — "as it is yaad karna
  padega"; 0–5°C because the diazonium salt is only stable in that range.
- **Fermentation facts**: invertase then zymase; anaerobic; zymase inhibited beyond 14%
  alcohol; air → ethanoic acid.
- **Dehydration temperature ladder**: 1° + conc. H₂SO₄ 443 K/170° → alkene; same alcohol
  at 140° → ether (intermolecular); 3° needs only 20% dilute acid. "Temperature ka
  management is very important here."
- **Oxidizing-agent map**: Cr in +6 (KMnO₄, K₂Cr₂O₇, CrO₃, H₂CrO₄) = strong — "wo 1°
  alcohol ko chhodega nahi, carboxylic acid bana ke hi rakhega." PCC/PDC stop at aldehyde
  (PDC when acid-sensitive groups present); Collins = CrO₃·2-pyridine; Jones = CrO₃ +
  dilute H₂SO₄. MPV: identify by aluminium isopropoxide — carbonyl-only reduction.
- **Reducing-agent ladder**: NaBH₄ < LiBH₄ < LiAlH₄; Bouveault–Blanc = Na/EtOH; diborane
  reduces COOH more readily than ester (NCERT's single line — "paragraph bhi nahi, just
  ek line likh rakhi hai" — and it became a JEE question).
- **Ether cleavage rule**: aryl–O bond NEVER breaks — "ek taraf benzene ring ho to ek
  product phenol banega hi banega"; with one-equivalent HI the alkyl side gives RI.
- **NCERT lab manual doctrine** (chapter-specific extension of his NCERT rule): acrolein
  test, CAN test, phthalein dye colours, FeCl₃ colours all live in the **lab manual**, not
  the textbook — "jo bhi aapko dikhta hai 'sir ye NCERT mein nahi hai' — wo NCERT mein
  hai, par lab manual mein hai. 11th ka alag hai, 12th ka alag hai."

## C. Trap bank (concept → the reflex he dramatizes, for ⚠️/Watch-out sections)

- **"2° + strong nucleophile = SN2" reflex:** "Main aapki baat se totally agree karta
  hoon — wo thought process bilkul aayega, mera bhi aaya tha. Par ye normal 2° hai ya
  hindered 2°? Bagal mein cyclohexyl bada group hai — hindrance definitely hai. To SN1
  hoga, carbocation banega, rearrangement hoga."
- **NaBH₄ tunnel vision:** given a bromo-ketone, "bachcha directly C=O ka OH bana dega aur
  A option mark kar dega — bilkul galat. Mechanism socho: H⁻ attack ke baad O⁻ bana, aur
  wahin molecule mein Br bhi hai — intramolecular attack ho sakta tha. Mechanism nahi
  padha, ratta hua hai 'C=O ka OH kar dena' — to phir sir maarte rahoge."
- **Grignard mole-counting with acidic H:** ester needs 2 mol BUT terminal alkyne H eats
  one first — "Grignard pehle as a base kaam karta hai. Jo bachcha jaldbazi mein O=C pe
  focus karega, bolega 'haan haan sir do RMgX use hote hain' — aur acidic H miss kar
  gaye. Silly mistake ka scope tha."
- **SN2 ⇒ inversion (false when attack is elsewhere):** Williamson on a chiral alcohol —
  "achchha SN2 hua hai, inversion! Are, dekho to sahi attack kiya kis pe — CH₃ pe, jo
  chiral tha hi nahi. Jis centre pe chirality hai uske bonds reaction mein participate hi
  nahi kar rahe — inversion bol hi nahi sakte."
- **SOCl₂ retention/inversion pair:** alone = SNi, **retention**; SOCl₂ + base = SN2,
  **inversion**. Carbocation intermediate only in the SNi proposal.
- **Aryl/vinyl halides never SN1/SN2:** "yahan baat ye bhi nahi hai ki elimination ho
  jata — hona hi nahi hai. Aryl position pe nucleophilic attack ke through SN1/SN2 nahi
  karwa sakte. Back attack karoge kaise? Poora benzene raaste mein aa jayega."
- **Kinetic-product temptation (allylic HBr):** "aap bologe sir 2° allylic pe lagaya,
  ye 1° allylic pe kyon? — kinetic control tab aata hai jab bahut low temperature diya
  ho, −20 wala. Room temperature hai, to thermodynamic product banega."
- **Protonate the OH or the double bond?** His decision rule: protonate whichever ejects a
  **stable molecule** (water) or makes the better carbocation — "OH₂⁺ banke water niklega
  — water is a very stable molecule, wahi driving force hai." Counter-case: allylic
  alcohol next to an O-mesomeric double bond — there protonate the alkene instead.
- **O- vs C-/S-attack (better nucleophile ≠ better base):** "oxygen is better base —
  chhota hai, electron density zyada. But sulphur is more polarizable — sulphur is a
  better nucleophile. Yahan as a base attack nahi karna, as a nucleophile karna hai."
  Same family: Kolbe — carbanion carbon attacks CO₂, not phenoxide O ("carbon being less
  electronegative, aasani se electron-pair donate karna chahega").
- **C≡N is sacred:** "normally hum triple bond ko disturb nahi karte reaction mein —
  it is a very stable bond. Unless aap usko Grignard mein CHO ya kisi form mein convert
  kar rahe ho."
- **Alkaline hydrolysis end-product:** "alkaline medium mein carboxylic acid as end
  product exist nahi karta — immediately carboxylate salt banega. Aage H⁺ ka ek aur step
  likhna chahiye tha." (He openly critiques the question for skipping it.)
- **SOCl₂ scope:** converts alcoholic OH to Cl, **not phenolic OH**.
- **Claisen star-mark trap:** "bachche jaldbazi mein sochte hain — chain ko todo, utha ke
  as-it-is ortho pe laga do. Nahi. Ye 1 se nahi judti, 3 se judti hai — double bond ki
  position flip ho jati hai (1-2-3 → 3-2-1). Ortho blocked ho to para pe jaake wapas
  1-2-3." Examiners star-mark one carbon precisely for this.
- **Cumene O-18 trap:** acetone's oxygen comes from WATER, not from the O₂/peroxide —
  "itna minutely observe karna padta hai. 'Haan sir, haan sir' karke dekh liya — itni
  minute detail poochhega to phans jaoge. Phasna nahi hai."
- **Fries vs Friedel–Crafts:** RCOCl with NO Lewis acid → O-acylation only ("RCO⁺ banega
  hi nahi to benzene pe attack nahi ho sakta"); add AlCl₃ and "ye bhai sahab chupchaap
  uth ke ortho/para pe shift kar jayega."
- **Reimer–Tiemann intermediates:** dichlorocarbene CCl₂ (not CCl₃, not CHCl); and the
  ring intermediate carries **O⁻ (phenoxide), not OH** — "yahan pe OH nahi hota,
  phenoxide hota hai. Ye dhyaan rakhna."
- **Equivalents ambiguity (HI on ether):** "rather they should have mentioned ki HI is
  one equivalent. Itni si clarity mention kar dete to inka kya jata? Jo padh ke aaya hai
  bachcha, wo to confuse nahi hota." He refuses to hard-mark the answer — models the
  protest, then gives the intended option.
- **JEE Mains ambiguity doctrine (recurring meta):** "JEE Mains ke questions ki statement
  bahut clear nahi hoti; Advanced mein wo elaborate karte hain — isko ignore karo, isko
  consider karo. Har saal kuch na kuch ambiguity dikhti hai. Uska hum kuch nahi kar
  sakte — jo best possible concept ke basis pe lag raha hai uske saath jaiye." Plus the
  paper-designer's state of mind: "NCERT mein bahut deep content nahi hai — jisne sirf
  NCERT achchhe se ki hai uske liye this is perfect. Wo state of mind samajhna padega."
