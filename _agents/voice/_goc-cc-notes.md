## Must-memorize (orders/values he dictates)

- **−I order:** NR₃⁺ > SR₂⁺ > NH₃⁺ > NO₂ > CN > COOH > F > Cl > Br > I. Anti-ratta framing: "Not exactly ye poora order ratt ke chale jao. Ek-do baar padh liya to ek idea rahega — kaun thode strong hain, kaun thode weak." Rule of thumb he gives instead: "Jab bhi nitrogen pe positive charge dikhe, samajh jao bahut strong −I group hai." Halogens: electronegativity order = −I order.
- **+I order:** O⁻ > COO⁻ > CR₃ > ... (decreasing). O⁻ best donor (extra electrons).
- **Resonance-structure stability rules — APPLY IN THIS SEQUENCE, stop when resolved:** (1) octet completion ("sabse important rule hai ye"), (2) more covalent bonds — count ONLY π ("saare bond mat count karne lag jaana — sigma to change honge hi nahi"), (3) less charge separation, (4) negative charge on more-EN atom / positive on less-EN. "Agar isi pe answer aa gaya to aage kuch dekhne ki zaroorat nahi."
- **Resonance energies:** benzene 36 kcal ("thoda sa yaad kar lena chahiye"); naphthalene 61 → per ring 30.5; anthracene 3 rings, per-ring less; phenanthrene > anthracene stability (higher RE, same 3 rings). Pyridine/furan/thiophene/pyrrole values "just compare karne ke liye — koi mandatory data nahi."
- **Hyperconjugation requirement:** alpha-H on an **sp³** carbon adjacent to (a) C=C, (b) C⁺, (c) radical. "sp2 pe nahi, sp pe bhi nahi."
- **What overlaps with what (JEE Advanced asked this):** carbocation — σ(C–H) with **empty 2p**; alkene — σ(C–H) with **π\* antibonding**. "Ye bhi dhyaan rakhna."
- **Alkene stability when alpha-H count ties:** gem-disubstituted (both alkyl on one carbon) > trans > cis (less repulsion in trans). "Experimental data dekh ke kiya jaata hai."
- **Strength hierarchy:** resonance > hyperconjugation ("resonance aapka strong factor maana jayega").
- **Hybrid energy** is lower than EVERY resonating structure; more stable RS contributes more (equal RS ⇒ equal contribution, e.g. carbonate 1/3 each, benzene 50-50).
- **Inductive effect range:** "teen-chaar carbon ke baad inductive effect bilkul negligible — hum consider nahi karte."
- **Heat of hydrogenation ∝ 1/stability.**
- Electromeric + inductomeric effects: "kisi kaam ka nahi hai" — temporary, skip.
- **Hückel procedure (his order):** first THREE gates — cyclic, planar, continuous conjugation ("tabhi p orbitals parallel honge, tabhi overlap kar payenge") — THEN count: 4n+2 π (2, 6, 10...) aromatic; 4n π (4, 8, 12...) anti-aromatic; fails a gate ⇒ non-aromatic.
- **Stability order:** aromatic > non-aromatic > anti-aromatic (the profit/zero/loss frame).
- **Conjugation-break rule:** "Agar kisi bhi path mein koi sp³ atom aa jaye to samajh lo wahan conjugation break ho gayi" — sp³ has no spare p orbital ("teeno p kharch ho gaye").
- **Benzene hydrogenation data he quotes:** 1 C=C → 120 kJ/mol; 2 conjugated → 232 (expected 240, discount 8); benzene → 208 (expected 360, discount 152) ⇒ "this kind of stabilization cannot be explained on the basis of conjugation alone" — real reason: 6 p orbitals → 6 MOs → 3 bonding completely filled.
- **Annulene facts:** 10-annulene NON-planar ⇒ non-aromatic (despite 4n+2); 14-annulene IS planar ⇒ aromatic; 16-annulene anti-aromatic; 18-annulene aromatic. Pyrene: ignore the middle double bond, count only periphery (14 π) ⇒ aromatic — "Hückel rule single ring ke liye define kiya gaya tha; yahan apply karna bhi nahi chahiye."
- **Anti-aromatic resonance ban:** never draw benzene-style equalizing resonance for cyclobutadiene — "double chhota hai, single lamba hai; ye lengths barabar nahi hain. Anti-aromatic mein is tarah ki resonance nahi karte, because that causes destabilization."
- **+M strength: N > O** (same-period lone-pair donors): "Nitrogen is less electronegative than oxygen — zyada aasaani se kaun dega? Nitrogen. +M effect hamesha nitrogen ka oxygen se better hai."
- **Lone-pair counting in π count:** count a lone pair ONLY if conjugation needs it to bridge a gap (furan: 1 of O's 2 lone pairs); if alternation is already complete (pyridine-type N), do NOT count it.
- **THE one-line master rule of organic (he says memorize this single statement):** "Charge dispersal leads to stability — poore organic mein ye fundamental hai. Chahe inductive ki baat karein, mesomeric, hyperconjugation — hum charge ko spread out hi to kar rahe hain."
- **Carbenium vs carbonium (book-error he corrects):** CH₃⁺ (trivalent) = carbEnium; carbOnium = pentavalent (CH₅⁺, from CH₄ + H⁺, octet NOT exceeded — 3c-2e). "Majority of books ne galti ye ki hai — they have mentioned it is the carbonium ion."
- **Carbocation stability ladder:** trityl (3 rings) > diphenylmethyl > 3° > benzyl > allyl > 2° > 1° > vinyl/phenyl. With the caveat: **3° is a LITTLE more stable than benzyl** — "logic to kehta hai benzyl zyada hona chahiye (resonance strong hai)... but but but, there is always a but — organic padh rahe hain to thoda to beech mein but aayega. Ye pata lagta hai hydride affinity values se. Data beats everything — always go for data" (cites Jerry March / Carey).
- **Allylic must be sub-classified:** 1°/2°/3° allylic; 3° allylic > plain 3° (inductive + hyperconj + resonance, three effects at once). Definitions drilled: allylic = carbon NEXT to the C=C carbon; benzylic = carbon outside the ring; phenyl/vinyl = + on the sp² carbon itself ("bahut bakwas, bahut hi unstable").
- **Aromatic cations are exceptionally stable** when the + charge ITSELF creates the aromaticity (cyclopropenyl, tropylium) — test: "agar positive charge neutral kar do to kya aromaticity pe fark padega?" Trityl: no (benzene was aromatic anyway); tropylium: yes.
- **Cyclopropylmethyl cation > tropylium** — reason is **bent-orbital overlap** (angle-strained σ orbitals overlap easily with the empty p). "DANCING RESONANCE jaisa koi term literature mein exist nahi karta — logon ne coin kar di thi, which is wrong actually."
- **Halogen mesomeric order:** F > Cl > Br (+M) — 2p–2p overlap matches carbon's 2p ("similar size, comparable energy — overlap achchha hoga"); 3p–2p worse, 4p–2p "aur bekaar." BUT for halogens inductive > mesomeric (exception to the usual M > I), so overall they count as weakly withdrawing.
- **Carbanion rules:** EDG destabilize, EWG stabilize ⇒ CH₃⁻ > 1° > 2° > 3° (inverted ladder); geometry: 3 single bonds + LP = sp³ pyramidal; one double = sp²; triple = sp. CCl₃⁻ > CF₃⁻ — back-bonding into Cl's empty d orbitals ("fluorine ke paas koi empty d orbital hai hi nahi").
- **Radical facts:** neutral — "charge dispersal wali baat nahi ho payegi"; stabilized by BOTH EDG and EWG; CH₃• planar sp² (odd e⁻ in p), CF₃• PYRAMIDAL; order ≈ carbocation order BUT "radical mein ek maze ki baat — allylic radical is even more stable than benzylic and 3°. Ye point yaad rakhna padega — confirmed from data."
- **Radical generation:** homolysis via photolysis / thermolysis / redox; acetone-type R–CO–R → 2R• + CO (stepwise, CO leaves); azo R–N=N–R → 2R• + N₂. Radical reactions: coupling, disproportionation, H-atom abstraction, O₂ addition (O₂ = radical inhibitor, slows chains).
- **Carbene:** CH₂ neutral, ELECTROPHILE not nucleophile ("kaafi bachche bolte hain sir lone pair hai, nucleophile hoga — no! Octet incomplete hai, isliye electrophile"). Generation: CH₂N₂ + Δ/hν → :CH₂ + N₂; CHCl₃ + base → CCl₃⁻ → :CCl₂ + Cl⁻ (the H is acidic because of 3 EWG). Singlet/triplet skipped as out of exam scope.
- **Why carbocations get the most class time:** "Carbocation poochhne mein hi resonance bhi check ho gayi, hyperconjugation bhi, inductive bhi — ek question se kaafi saare target poore ho jaate hain. Carbanion pe utne nahi aate, radicals pe aur bhi kam."
- **The starred pKa table he dictates (L3 — "reference points for calculated guesswork"):** mineral acids negative pKa (don't memorize values, just "bahut strong category"); HF 3.2; **benzoic 4.2 ("420 hai ekdam — 4.20")**; **acetic 4.76** ("repeatedly use kiya hoga, yaad hi hoga"); H₂CO₃ 6.35 ("bachche yaad nahi karte, par important — phenol se zyada acidic"); NH₄⁺ 9.2; **phenol ≈ 10**; water 15.7; ethanol 16; acetone 19.2; acetylene 25; H₂ 35; NH₃ 38; ethene 44; ethane 50. "Jab vailue thodi yaad hai to approximation se guess work bahut achchhe se kar paoge."
- **1 pKa unit = a BIG difference** (formic 3.77 vs acetic 4.76 — the first methyl matters; lengthening the chain after that barely moves it).
- **Acidity factor framework (HA):** (1) H–A bond strength, (2) electronegativity of A, (3) anything stabilizing A⁻ (conjugate base) raises acidity, (4) solvent, (5) H-bonding (can raise OR lower). Across period: electronegativity dominates; down group: size/bond length dominates; s-character: sp > sp² > sp³ acidity.
- **Ortho effect (benzoic acids):** ANY ortho group — donating or withdrawing — raises acidity; bulkier ortho group = stronger effect; two ortho groups even better (the army analogy). Scope warning: benzoic acid only.
- **Benzoic-derivative master summary (he converted the data table into this):** for G = CH₃, C₂H₅, F, Cl, Br, I, OH, OCH₃, phenyl → acidity **ortho > meta > para**. ONLY two exceptions to memorize: **CN and NO₂ → ortho > para > meta.** "Bas ye yaad kar liya to benzoic ke saare derivatives yahin solve ho gaye."
- **Nitrophenols:** para > ortho > meta acidity — ortho's OH is tied up in intramolecular H-bonding ("ye wali H-bonding bhi todni padegi — slightly more effort"); mesomeric effect does NOT weaken with distance (equal at ortho and para), inductive does; −M doesn't operate at meta. 2,4-dinitrophenol reaches carboxylic range; picric acid 1.02.
- **Cresols:** meta-cresol most acidic of the three — methyl can't hyperconjugate-donate into meta ("wahi logic lagega jo mesomeric ka lagta hai").
- **Ortho-halophenols:** bromo more acidic than fluoro — F's excellent +M (2p–2p) largely cancels its −I; in Br only inductive really operates.
- **Ortho effect, basicity variant:** ortho-substituted anilines are WEAKER bases than aniline — any ortho group, donating or withdrawing.
- **Aqueous amine basicity (NCERT trends):** gas phase 3° > 2° > 1°; aqueous = solvation (H-bond count on conjugate acid) vs inductive, opposing. **Methylamines: 2 > 1 > 3 > NH₃ ("213 yaad kar lo"); ethylamines: 2 > 3 > 1 ("231"). 2° on top in both.** "Directly na poochh ke iske peeche ka logic poochh liya jaye — assertion-reason ban jaye."
- **Cyclic amines > acyclic amines** of the same nature/degree. Pyridine's lone pair: in the ring plane, PERPENDICULAR to the π system, not in resonance ⇒ available ⇒ why pyridine is used as a base in so many reactions.
- **Basicity by hybridization:** sp³ > sp² > sp.
- **Basicity enhancers (3):** negative charge (2− > 1− > neutral), small size (denser charge), more donating groups.
- **Bridgehead rule:** rings with total atoms < 8 — no double bond / positive charge at a bridgehead (sp² needs planarity, bridgehead can't be planar) ⇒ a bridgehead N lone pair cannot donate by resonance.
- **Acid-base equilibrium direction:** "Koi bhi reaction us taraf jaata hai jahan wo zyada stable cheezein bana raha hai — jis direction mein acid bhi weak ban raha hai aur base bhi weak, wahi direction hogi reaction ki." Strong acid ⇒ weaker conjugate base.
- **Effervescence test (NaHCO₃):** works for anything with pKa in carboxylic range or stronger (sulfonic yes, picric yes); plain phenol no. "Dushmani phenol se nahi hai — baat hai ki pKa ek certain value se lower honi chahiye."
- **Aromatic conjugate base ⇒ huge acidity boost** (cyclopentadiene-type): "uske jaane se usko sirf resonance nahi mil rahi — wo aromatic ho raha hai, aur wo stabilization HUGE hota hai." Symmetric flip: a lone pair responsible for aromaticity will NOT be donated (pyrrole as base) — "aromaticity ka loss is a huge loss."
- **IUPAC name-assembly scheme (his fixed order):** 2° prefix (branches + junior FGs, ALWAYS alphabetical) → 1° prefix (only the word "cyclo") → word root (C count of main chain) → 1° suffix (ane/ene/yne, diene...) → 2° suffix (main FG). Unwritten locant = 1 ("jab locant nahi likhte, matlab wo one tha").
- **FG priority ladder he drills:** COOH > ester > carbonyl(acid) halide > amide > nitrile > aldehyde > ketone > alcohol > amine. (Sulfonic acid & anhydride deliberately skipped: "generally unke question nahi aate.") Prefix forms: carboxy / alkoxycarbonyl / halocarbonyl / carbamoyl / cyano / formyl–oxo / hydroxy / amino.
- **The carb- family rule (his counting proof):** when the FG carbon is NOT counted in the ring/chain word root → use the full form: -carbaldehyde, -carbonitrile, -carboxylic acid, -carboxamide, carbonyl halide. When it IS counted → -al, -nitrile, -oic acid, -amide.
- **Formyl vs oxo:** CHO outside the main chain = formyl; C=O counted inside = oxo. "CHO chain-TERMINATING group hai — hamesha end mein aata hai; ketone beech mein aata hai."
- **Radical/branch vocabulary drilled:** iso- = the (CH₃)₂CH– stub is FIXED ("iso mein minimum propyl to hota hi hai — ye teen carbon fix hain"); isobutyl = stub + CH₂; sec-butyl = straight butyl attached via its 2° carbon; phenyl (ring direct) vs benzyl (ring + CH₂); vinyl (C=C carbon direct) vs allyl (C=C + CH₂). "Iso mein pentyl tak use nahi hoga — propyl aur butyl pata hone chahiye."
- **Accepted trivial names he licenses:** acetamide, acetophenone ("kisi rule ke according nahi aata, par yahi accepted IUPAC hai"), benzophenone, benzaldehyde, methyl benzoate, phenyl acetate, benzenesulfonic acid, cumene (isopropylbenzene — "phenol mein padhna hota hai"), phthalic acid, α-/β-naphthol.
- **Classification ladder (asked as PYQs):** acyclic/cyclic → alicyclic vs aromatic → homo- vs heterocyclic (ring-atom property only) → benzenoid vs non-benzenoid → spiro / fused / bridged / isolated bicyclic. "Hydrocarbon" = C and H ONLY.
- **Numbering decision order he narrates:** main FG first ("functional group is the priority — FG hi main chain mein nahi hai to phir kis baat ki main chain?") → then unsaturation ("branch ko nahi, unsaturation ko next priority") → lowest locants → alphabetical tiebreak. Double vs triple bond: lowest locant FIRST; only on a perfect tie does the double bond win — "double bond ko priority TAB di kyonki locant se dono cheezein same aa rahi thi. Otherwise nahi. Yahan bachche galti karte hain."
- **Ring-specific numbering rules:** after picking C1 you must COVER the double bond going 1→2 ("ye nahi ki start kar diya aur wapas chale gaye"); when the two alkene carbons differ, start from the MORE alkylated one; in cyclic compounds with a main FG, that carbon is automatically C1.
- **Complex-branch protocol:** parenthesis at the attachment locant; give the branch its own primed numbering (1′, 2′...); branch name ends in -yl covering its own unsaturation, e.g. cyclobut-1-en-1-yl, (1-chloro-2-methyl)prop-2-en-1-yl.
- **N-locant for substituted amides/amines:** "Ye chains main chain se nahi judi — nitrogen se judi hain. To nitrogen hi inki position hai. N-ethyl-N-methyl... dono methyl hote to N,N-dimethyl."
- **Formal charge, no formula (Basics):** compare visible electrons against normal valency — one less ⇒ +1, one extra ⇒ −1. "Koi formula yaad nahi karna hai. Bilkul fundamental hai."
- **Valence-count principle (Basics):** bonding pattern depends on the electron COUNT, not the element — "C⁻ ke paas paanch electrons — ab wo wahi bonding arrangements dega jo nitrogen neutral form mein deta hai. Central atom ko bhi bhool jao."
- **Hybridization quick count (Basics):** σ bonds + FREE lone pairs (free = NOT in resonance); 2 = sp, 3 = sp², 4 = sp³. Carboxylic-acid OH oxygen = sp² (one LP in resonance). **Melamine (JEE Main PYQ): all SIX nitrogens sp², zero sp³** — the NH₂ lone pairs are in resonance.
- **Dipole pair-facts he stars (NCERT tables):** CH₃Cl > CH₃F (μ = q×d; longer C–Cl bond wins, "slightly — bahut chhota difference"); NH₃ 1.47 vs NF₃ 0.2 (lone-pair vector adds vs opposes); CH₃Cl > CH₂Cl₂ (two C–Cl vectors at >90° partially cancel); BF₃/CO₂/CCl₄ zero by symmetry.
- **Polarizability (Basics):** decreases across period, increases down group; C–I reacts faster than C–Cl in SN2 DESPITE lower polarity — "polarity bhi important hai, par polarizABILITY bhi. Wo poori picture nahi batate wahan pe."
- **Bond length by hybridization:** even C(sp³)–H > C(sp²)–H > C(sp)–H — flagged as not directly examinable but foundation: "Fundamentals ka matlab ye nahi ki hum wahi padhenge jo paper mein aayega. Bahut saari cheezein aisi hain ki unka knowledge nahi hoga to aage ki cheezein samajh nahi aayengi."
- **Boiling-point ladder (forces):** alkane (van der Waals) < ether (dipole, NO ether–ether H-bond) < aldehyde/nitrile (heavier dipoles) < alcohol < water < carboxylic acid (strongest H-bonding); intramolecular H-bond (o-nitrophenol) ⇒ LOWER bp than intermolecular (p-nitrophenol); urea solid vs acetone liquid by H-bond strength.

## Traps (the reflex he dramatizes)

- **Positive charge ≠ electrophile.** "Har bachche ko pata hai sir, electron loving matlab positive charge. Par kya har positively charged ion electrophile hoga? NH₄⁺ pe bhi to positive charge hai — par nitrogen already chaar bond bana chuka hai, octet complete hai, exceed nahi kar sakta. This is NOT an electrophile." Test = can it ACCEPT an electron pair (empty orbital / breakable π)?
- **"Sir carbon ke to already chaar bond hain — ye kaise electron pair accept karega?"** (CO₂/carbonyl) → "kyonki wo pi bond toot sakta hai."
- **Whole molecule ≠ electrophile.** Carbonyl: C is the electrophilic CENTER, O⁻ end is a nucleophilic center — "ek hi molecule mein dono present hote hain. Poora molecule electrophile nahi hota."
- **Octet beats electronegativity** (THE GOC trap): "Bachche galti se answer dete hain — sir ye zyada stable hai, kyonki sir oxygen pe positive charge nahi aana chahiye. Par yahan carbon ke paas sirf 6 electrons hain — octet incomplete. Wahan sabke octet complete hain. Octet completion sabse badi priority hai — uske badle oxygen pe positive aa gaya to koi baat nahi."
- **…but check O's own octet:** O⁺ is tolerated only "jab at least wo kisi ka octet complete kar raha ho." O⁺ with 6 electrons (2 LP + 1 bond) is WORSE than C⁺ — "wo jhel to paayega; ye to bechara paagal sa ho jaata hai."
- **Arrow-pushing bans:** never move a π bond TOWARD a negative charge ("uske paas already extra electrons hain — usko nahi donate karna, kabhi"); never move a positive charge ("positive charge ko kabhi move mat kar dena — wahan kuch hai hi nahi move karne ke liye"); never move atoms, never move σ electrons; charge is always conserved.
- **Octet-exceed check before delocalizing:** if N or O already has 4 bonds, the π/lone pair cannot shift onto it — "nitrogen ke paanch bond ban jayenge, aisa Lewis structure valid nahi hoga." (First worked Q.)
- **Hyperconjugation jaldbaazi trap:** structure with three methyls on the alpha carbon — "Aap bologe sir 3 × 3 = 9. Are nahi bhai! Alpha carbon pe H to hai hi nahi — teeno carbon–carbon bond bane hain. No alpha hydrogen. Dekhne mein lag raha hai shayad nau honge — ye jaldbaazi mein bachcha galti karta hai."
- **PE vs stability inversion:** "Jitni kam potential energy, utni zyada stability. Decreasing PE ka matlab increasing stability" — convert before ordering.
- **"Same bond length" wording trap (squaric acid):** all C–C same length AND all C–O same length does NOT mean C–C = C–O. "Main ye nahi bol raha ki carbon-carbon aur carbon-oxygen ki barabar hai — ye matlab mat nikaal lena."
- **Blocked delocalization:** two single bonds in between ⇒ sp³ carbon breaks conjugation — "delocalize hi nahi hua to stability kaise milegi?"
- **σ→π\* next to a carbocation?** "Aap bologe sir, double bond ke bagal mein to positive charge hai — ye kaise aayega? Chaliye ek kaam karte hain, iska resonating structure draw karke dekh lete hain" — in the second RS the CH₃ sits next to the C=C, so σ→π\* hyperconjugation DOES exist. Draw the RS before denying an overlap.
- **OCOCH₃ vs COOCH₃ (which way the ester points):** "O pehle aaya, C=O baad mein aaya — to ye carbonyl ka derivative is taraf se count nahi hoga." Attached via O ⇒ moderate +M; attached via carbonyl C ⇒ −M. Read the attachment atom, not the formula.
- **"Conjugation = stability" reflex:** "Ye mat bol dena sir, conjugation is the reason — kyonki conjugation se kabhi-kabhi ulta stability ghat bhi jaati hai. Anti-aromatic mein yahi to hota hai."
- **Pyridine-type lone pair in π count:** "Nitrogen ke paas lone pair to hai — galti se isko count mat kar lena. Double single double — already conjugation khud poori hai. Is lone pair ki zaroorat hi nahi." (Counting it flips your aromatic/anti verdict.)
- **Negative charge counts as 2 π electrons** in the Hückel count (cyclopentadienyl-type cases).
- **"Bigger ring must be even less planar":** "Yahin to aap dhokha kha gaye — 14-annulene planar hai, aur aromatic bhi hai." 10 is the non-planar exception, not the rule.
- **Fused-benzene aromaticity trap (JEE Advanced asked this exact molecule):** benzene fused to a non-conjugated ring — "kai baar bachche jaldbaazi mein kya karte hain — sir, continuous conjugation nahi hai, ye non ho jayega. Are bandhuon, ye benzene ka derivative hai! Ek aromatic ring to hai na. Ye bolna ki dono hi rings mein aromaticity aani chahiye — aisa nahi hota. This is aromatic."
- **Don't force aromaticity by shifting bonds when it HURTS:** cyclopentadienone-type case — shifting the C=O π into the ring gives 4π (anti) ⇒ "usko fayda nahi hoga, ulta nuksaan. To wo isi form mein, non-aromatic form mein rahna prefer karega."
- **"Strongest EWG should stabilize CF₃⁻ best" reflex:** "Logical mind ye bolta hai — sir fluorine is more electronegative, teen fluorine lage hain... Actually CCl₃⁻ back-bonding kar sakta hai (Cl ke empty d orbitals); CF₃⁻ mein no back bonding." 
- **Tert-butyl bait (most-stable-intermediate Q):** "Ekdam se answer dikhta hai — sir, tert-butyl itna achchha donating group laga hua hai! Par positive charge se itna door hai ki inductive effect negligible. Alkyl group mesomeric show nahi karte. Funda hyperconjugation ka hai — mostly isme bachche khataak karke galat answer dete hain."
- **Benzyl vs phenyl confusion:** "Benzyl means benzene ke bahar waale carbon pe positive — very stable. Phenyl ka matlab benzene ke hi ek carbon ke upar positive charge" — opposite ends of the ladder.
- **"Allylic is stable" said without degree:** always specify 1°/2°/3° allylic — "allylic mein bhi you have to specify."
- **Radical ≠ charged species:** "Aap bologe sir donating group lagao to achchha hai, withdrawing lagao to... bhai sahab, ye charged hai hi nahi!" Both kinds stabilize it.
- **Carbene looks like a nucleophile:** lone pair visible ⇒ students say nucleophile; octet-incomplete ⇒ electrophile.
- **More resonating structures ≠ more stable (phenol vs acetic acid):** "Sir, phenoxide ke to zyada resonating structures ban rahe hain — phir wo zyada acidic kyon nahi? Dekhiye, zyada number of resonating structures banne se kuch nahi hota. Wo structures utne STABLE bhi hone chahiye. Phenoxide ke zyadatar mein negative charge CARBON pe aa raha hai — unki contribution badi kam hogi. Acetate ke sirf do hain, par dono mein negative charge do ALAG-ALAG oxygen pe hai." And the sub-trap: "Taaki aap ye na bolo — sir, phenoxide mein bhi do baar oxygen pe hai. Wo dono SAME oxygen hain na."
- **Carbonyl beside a carbocation = very destabilizing:** its withdrawal would put two + charges adjacent — "kahin bhi carbonyl carbocation ke agal-bagal laga ho, that's a very destabilizing group."
- **Ortho effect scope traps:** "Benzoic mein! Cyclohexane ring pe wo concept mat laga lena" (cyclohexane-COOH with a neighboring alkyl = just a donating group ⇒ LESS acidic). And in basicity: "Aap bologe sir ye to donating group hai, basicity badhaani chahiye — par ye ANILINE hai, ortho effect lagega" (decreases it); on a non-aniline amine the same donating group does increase basicity.
- **"F⁻ should be the weakest base, it's most electronegative":** "Aap bologe sir, yahan ye logic kyon nahi laga rahe? Across a period electronegativity is the major factor; down a group size ki variation zyada badi hai" — F⁻ is the strongest halide base (density), I⁻ weakest.
- **Ester alpha-H:** "Esters are not known for their acidic hydrogens" — the carbonyl is already resonating with OR, so it stabilizes the carbanion poorly; diketone middle-H is the acidic star.
- **Bridgehead resonance bait:** "Aapko lage sir nitrogen is tarah se donate karega — ye donate NAHI kar payega. Bridgehead position pe hai" (would force sp²/planarity).
- **Common-factor discipline (HW answer):** when O's +M resonance is common to both cations, "isko compare mat karo, ignore kar do, cancel out ho jayega" — decide on the remaining difference (1 vs 2 alpha-H: "ratti bhar less stable").
- **Cyclohexanal/cyclohexanonitrile/cyclohexanoic — the un-counted-carbon trap (IUPAC):** "Kai bachchon ne cyclohexanal likha hoga, jaise benzaldehyde likhte hain. Beta, butanal mein BUTE chaar carbon represent karta hai — CHO ka carbon bhi count hua hai. Cyclohexane mein wahi chhe carbon count ho rahe hain; SAATWAN carbon to naam mein kahin mention hi nahi ho raha. Isliye poora likha jayega — carbaldehyde."
- **Ring-numbering shortcut reflex (IUPAC):** students number so branches cluster ("1 2 3 ikatthe aa gaye sir!") but skip covering the double bond — "rule to yahi bolta hai: 1 ke baad wahi 2 aayega jo double bond cover kare. Tab lowest locant is not important. Maximum bachche yahan galti karte hain."
- **"Longer chain must be the main chain" (IUPAC):** 4-carbon chain vs 2-carbon chain WITH the OH — the 2-carbon chain wins. "Baat to theek hai aapki... par functional group is the priority."
- **Alphabetical-order placement trap:** with symmetric locant sets, the alphabetically-first group takes the lower locant (amino beats formyl at C2 — the "not correctly matched" PYQ answer).
- **"Hydrocarbon" misuse:** a ring with O/N in it can be alicyclic + unsaturated but is NOT a hydrocarbon — "is cheez ka thoda dhyaan rakhna."
- **Negative charge ≠ pair of electrons (Basics):** "Bahut saare bachchon ko confusion rehta hai ki CH₃⁻ mein negative charge represents a pair of electrons. NAHI. Pair mein se ek iska apna tha, ek bahar se liya — jo ADDITIONAL liya usi ki wajah se negative charge aaya. Yahi chhoti-chhoti galtiyaan hain jinse organic kharab hoti hai."
- **CH₃F dipole reflex (Basics):** "Immediately bachche ke mind mein — sir CH₃F, fluorine more electronegative! Are, aapse question poochha hai — agar jo humne socha hota wahi answer hota, to poochhte hi kyon?" μ = q × d; the longer C–Cl bond flips it.
- **Melamine reflex:** "Normally bachcha sochega — ring waale N sp² honge, bahar waale shayad sp³. Galti kar di — un NH₂ ke lone pairs resonance mein hain. All six sp²."

## Electron-effects narration moves

- **Definition flip as the opening move.** Electrophile is NOT introduced as "positive charge" but as the capability: "Aap ulta aise samjho — electrophile wo hai jo electron pair lena chahta hai. Kisi ke paas empty orbital pada hua hai..."
