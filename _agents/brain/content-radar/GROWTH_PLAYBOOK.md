# Canvas YouTube Growth Playbook — "Win the Game They Can't Play"

> **Why this exists.** Founder pushed back (2026-06-19) that trend-summary briefs are worthless ("insights I can find in 5 min") and that copying bigger channels is a losing game. This is the standing strategy. **Every Content Radar Hero recommendation must be built on a pillar + pass the gates below.** Tactics (which exam, which week) live in the dated briefs; this is the *why we win* that doesn't change week to week.

## 0. The metric trap (learned the hard way, 2026-06-19)
`vidiq_score_title` measures **searchability, not virality.** Live proof: a generic "Chemistry Revision One Shot for NEET 2026" scored **91**, while differentiated curiosity/truth titles scored 69–78. **Do not let the score pick Hero videos** — it will march us back into the commodity one-shot war we cannot win against PW/Unacademy (who own 600K+/mo branded search). Use the score ONLY for Hygiene (search) titles. Hero videos are judged by **CTR + first-48h watch-time**, which the tool can't see.

## 1. The one strategic truth
We will never out-publish the giants on revision content. Growth comes only from what they **structurally cannot or will not do.** We have three such advantages — every Hero video sits on one.

## 2. The 3 Pillars (each already proven on our own channel)
| Pillar | The unfair advantage | Why giants can't copy it | Our proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TRUTH** | The teacher who tells aspirants what the coaching/edtech machine hides. | PW/Unacademy channels *are* the machine — they're muzzled. | "dark secrets of Ed-tech", "broken promises, broken students", "RANK is worthless now" all overperformed. |
| **CURIOSITY** | Chemistry as a mystery, not a syllabus — open a loop the brain must close. | Their format is "Chapter X One Shot" (closes curiosity, no reason to click). | "Why PCl5 exists but PH5 doesn't" — the seed of a repeatable engine. |
| **RECEIPTS** | We're a *builder*: 15,000+ question bank, adaptive engine, college predictor. "I analyzed everything and found the pattern." | Pure-content channels have no product to show; they can only assert. | Crucible (15,226 live Qs), the adaptive engine, the predictor tool. |

## 3. The content architecture (Hero / Hub / Hygiene)
| Bucket | Cadence | Job | What goes here | Judge by |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HERO** | 1–2 / month | Grow (go wide, new subs) | TRUTH + CURIOSITY + RECEIPTS swings | CTR + first-48h watch-time |
| **HUB** | weekly | Bind (viewers → fans) | A signature recurring series uniquely ours | returning viewers, subs/video |
| **HYGIENE** | as needed | Capture search (evergreen) | "[topic] one shot" / searched topics | `vidiq_score_title` (91-type wins HERE) |

**The current mistake (root cause of flat 136K subs):** sequential course-dumps (Salt Analysis 1–4, BITSAT series, Amines 1–3 + DPP) are being published in the **Hero slot**. They serve enrolled students, not discovery — "Salt Analysis-1" gained ~3 views/day; zero videos beat the channel average in a year. **Fix:** series → playlists/unlisted for enrolled students; main feed protected for Hero swings.

## 4. The viral title recipe — 3 gates (every Hero title must pass all 3)
1. **Curiosity gap** — a question or a "wait, what?" the brain can't leave open.
2. **Stakes** — a cost / risk / secret to *the viewer* ("…is killing your rank").
3. **Specific & concrete** — a number, a name, a hard claim ("15,000 questions", "one electron").
Generic = rejected. Then A/B the title+thumbnail and watch CTR — don't trust the search score for Hero.

## 5. Packaging reality (search side)
Students search **"[topic] one shot"**, NOT "strategy/roadmap" (those return ~0 volume): "physical chemistry one shot" 66K/mo, "organic chemistry one shot" 31K, "chemistry one shot" 19K. Title Hygiene videos the way students actually type.

## 6. The lowest-competition lane we under-use
**Motivation/strategy:** "iit motivation" ~64K/mo at competition **27/100**; "jee motivation" ~19K at 27. We already overperform here. Make it a recurring pillar, not a side gig. (This is a TRUTH-pillar adjacency.)

## 7. How the Radar uses this
Each weekly brief's ranked cards must label which **pillar** + **bucket** each video is, and Hero cards must show how the title passes the 3 gates. A card justified only by "this is trending" is rejected (CONTENT_RADAR.md §1.5).

## 8. Channel-state first — we are HIBERNATING, so the reactivation rules are active
Per CONTENT_RADAR.md **§1.6**, the radar diagnoses channel-state before ranking. Canvas (2026-06) = **🔴 Hibernating** (136K flat for months, zero outliers in a year, feed = course-dumps). While hibernating, these override the generic ranking:
- **No chasing live waves** — plan the next 2–6 weeks (e.g. result-day content, not exam-day content); never a "last-48h" video during the exam.
- **≈70% non-academic / ≤30% academic.** Academic = credibility floor, not the growth engine. Motivation/strategy/truth/documentary acquire; one-shots only retain.
- **Emotional calendar > exam calendar.** Name the emotion each Hero fires; publish to despair/hope/regret peaks (result day is the biggest).
- **Packaging-first; cross-niche format transplant (≥1/run); sequenced 30-day relaunch, not a shopping list.**
- **North-star:** *"not a chemistry channel that needs more chemistry — a movement-with-a-teacher that went quiet and needs people to care again."*
